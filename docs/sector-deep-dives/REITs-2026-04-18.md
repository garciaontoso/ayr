Dividend growth decelerated from 4-5% (2018-2021) to 2.9% (2025). ### The Rate Environment — The Single Most Important Variable

REITs are, by their nature, rate-sensitive assets. The entire sector de-rated 30-40% from its 2021-2022 peak as the 10-year Treasury moved from 1.5% to 4.5%. Going forward, three rate scenarios produce materially different REIT outcomes:

**Scenario A (Base Case, 50% probability): Gradual Normalization**
- 10-year Treasury drifts from 4.5% to 3.75-4.0% over 12-18 months
- Fed cuts 2-3x in 2026, 1-2x in 2027
- REIT sector yields compress 30-50 bps on average
- User's portfolio gains ~8-12% from yield compression + 4-6% dividend growth = **12-18% total return**
- **Winners**: O (most rate-leveraged), VICI, NNN, REXR
- **Losers (relative)**: CLPR (structural), IIPR (refi risk persists)

**Scenario B (Bull Case, 20% probability): Aggressive Fed Easing**
- 10-year Treasury drops to 3.25-3.5% as recession fears accelerate Fed
- REIT sector yields compress 50-100 bps
- Cap rates re-compress across all sub-sectors
- User's portfolio gains ~15-25% from yield compression + dividend growth = **20-30% total return**
- **Winners**: SAFE (highest rate sensitivity), ARE (cyclical + rate tailwind), VICI
- **Losers**: none significant — this is broadly bullish for REITs

**Scenario C (Bear Case, 25% probability): Higher for Longer**
- 10-year Treasury stays 4.5-5.0% through 2027
- Sticky inflation prevents Fed cuts
- REIT yields widen 20-50 bps
- Refinancing headwinds intensify across the sector
- User's portfolio loses ~5-10% from yield expansion + 3-4% dividend growth = **-1% to -6% total return**
- **Winners (relative)**: REITs with 99%+ fixed debt (VICI 99.2%, NNN 100% unsecured), low leverage (SUI 1.75x post-divestiture)
- **Losers**: SAFE (bond proxy), ARE (cyclical + rate pressure compound), CLPR (refi impossible)

**Scenario D (Tail, 5% probability): Rate Spike to 5.5%+**

**REITs are hurt by inflation when:**
- Long-duration fixed leases (net lease REITs)
