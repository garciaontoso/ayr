**ZTS** has no material patent cliff. ### 2B. Medical Devices

Medical devices is the sub-sector most underrepresented in the portfolio. Structurally, med-tech is distinct from pharma in several dividend-relevant ways:

**Why med-tech is good for dividend investors:**
- **Recurring consumables revenue:** ~30-50% of revenue for most large med-tech names comes from consumables (catheters, stents, insulin pump supplies, surgical staples). This is recurring, sticky, and mostly insensitive to drug-pricing reform.
- **No patent cliff in the traditional sense:** Medical device patents exist but the regulatory moat is more about the "razor-and-blade" installed base — once a hospital installs a Medtronic pacemaker programmer or an ISRG da Vinci surgical system, switching costs are prohibitive.
- **Aging demographics tailwind:** Cardiovascular, orthopedic, diabetes, and surgical device demand grows ~4-6% annually driven by population aging, independent of economic cycle.
- **Limited IRA/MFN exposure:** Device pricing is negotiated with hospitals and GPOs, not with CMS formularies. IRA Part B/D pricing mechanisms largely do not apply.
- **Dividend aristocrat concentration:** MDT (48 years), ABT (53 years including Abbott Laboratories history — Dividend King) are bedrock dividend-growth stocks.

## PART V — MACRO/REGULATORY RISK OVERLAY

The dividend investor's analysis of healthcare cannot stop at company-level fundamentals. Four macro/regulatory forces are actively reshaping sub-sector economics over 2024-2028:

### 5A. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) Drug Pricing Reform

The IRA's Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Program entered its operational phase in 2024-2026. The mechanics:

**Phase 1 (effective 2026):** CMS negotiated Maximum Fair Prices (MFP) for 10 Part D drugs. Negotiated prices are ~38-79% below prior list prices. Affected user portfolio:
- **Eliquis** (PFE + BMS) — selected. MFP effective Jan 1, 2026. Estimated revenue impact to PFE: $1.2-2.0B in 2026 on a $7.96B base.
- **Xarelto** (JNJ) — selected. MFP effective Jan 1, 2026. Estimated impact to JNJ: $1.5-2.0B on a ~$6B base.
- Other Phase 1 selections include Jardiance (LLY/BI), Entresto (NVS), Januvia (MRK), Imbruvica (ABBV/JNJ), Enbrel (AMGN), Farxiga (AZN), Stelara (JNJ), NovoLog/Fiasp (NVO).

**Phase 2 (effective 2027):** Additional 15 Part D drugs. CMS announced the 2027 selections on January 17, 2025:
- **Ozempic/Rybelsus/Wegovy** (NVO) — selected. Material impact.
- **Ibrance** (PFE) — selected.
- **Xtandi** (PFE/Astellas) — selected.
- **Trelegy** (GSK) — selected.
- Others include Pomalyst (BMY), Austedo (Teva), Linzess (AbbVie), Calquence (AZN), Otezla (AMGN), Xolair (Novartis/Roche), Ofev (Boehringer), Vraylar (ABBV), Janumet (MRK), Brilinta (AZN), Tradjenta (BI/LLY).

**Phase 3 (effective 2028):** Part B drugs added for the first time. This is where oncology really gets hit — Keytruda (MRK), Opdivo (BMY), Rituxan/Rituxan Hycela (Roche biosimilars), Eylea (REGN), Prolia (AMGN) are likely candidates.

**Who wins, who loses:**

- **Loses: PFE, NVO, BMY, MRK, AMGN, ABBV** — All have multiple drugs selected or expected. Combined revenue compression across the peer group estimated at 5-10% over 2026-2028.
- **Mostly unaffected: JNJ** (Stelara was selected but it's already past peak due to biosimilars; Xarelto selected but represents ~2% of JNJ revenue), **ABT** (medical devices not in scope), **MDT** (same), **SYK** (same), **BDX** (same), **ZTS** (animal health not covered).

**Dividend investor conclusion:** The IRA is net negative for pharma and essentially neutral for medical devices, diagnostics, and animal health. This is a structural reason to tilt the healthcare basket away from pure pharma and toward device/diagnostics/animal health — which aligns with the Part IV recommendations to add ABT (and optionally MDT).

### 5B. GLP-1 Market Dynamics and Saturation Risk

The GLP-1 obesity/diabetes market is the defining growth story of pharmaceuticals in the 2020s. As of 2026, the question is no longer "will this market exist" — it's "what's the shape of the demand curve after initial adoption?"

**Current market structure:**
- NVO (semaglutide) — Ozempic (T2D) + Wegovy (obesity) + Rybelsus (oral T2D) + Wegovy Pill (oral obesity)
- LLY (tirzepatide) — Mounjaro (T2D) + Zepbound (obesity) — has taken US market share leadership in both categories during 2025
- Upcoming entrants: AMGN (MariTide monthly GLP-1 + GIP, Phase 2), PFE (Metsera MET-097i monthly + MET-233i amylin, Phase 2), Viking Therapeutics (VK2735, Phase 2), Roche/Carmot (CT-388, Phase 2)

**Key dynamics investors must watch:**

1. **Volume elasticity vs. pricing compression.** The IRA Phase 2 negotiations put Ozempic, Rybelsus, and Wegovy at negotiated prices effective 2027. Volume growth must outpace price compression or dollar revenue declines. Current trajectory: volumes expanding 40%+ annually, price cuts estimated at 30-50%, so net dollar growth is still positive but decelerating.

2. **Oral GLP-1 adoption (Wegovy Pill, orforglipron).** If oral formulations achieve even 50% of injectable patient enrollment, the market size expands 2-3x. NVO's Wegovy Pill launched Q1 2026; LLY's orforglipron is expected 2026-2027. This is the single biggest volume catalyst ahead.

3. **Compounding pharmacies.** Despite the shortage being resolved, unauthorized compounding continues. FDA enforcement has been inconsistent. The revenue leakage is estimated at $1-2B annually across NVO + LLY.

4. **Market share dynamics.** NVO lost US leadership to LLY in 2025. Consensus expects continued share erosion unless Wegovy 7.2mg (high-dose) and CagriSema regain the efficacy edge. This is a duopoly, not a winner-take-all market, but the leader earns premium valuation.

5. **Insurance coverage expansion.** The MFN agreement's Medicare Part D obesity coverage pilot (expected mid-2026) is the pivotal access catalyst. If successful, it opens a 100M+ Medicare eligibility pool to GLP-1 coverage.

**Portfolio implications:**
- **User already owns NVO** — direct exposure to the category leader-by-revenue
- **No exposure to LLY** — deliberate choice given 0.6% yield; this is a growth stock, not a dividend stock
- **PFE (Metsera) is a lottery ticket** — MET-097i Phase 2 data 2H 2026 is binary

**Saturation risk assessment:** The TAM is structurally large (~750M people with diabetes + obesity combined). Current penetration is <5% globally. Saturation is not the near-term risk; pricing pressure from IRA Phase 2 (2027) and commercial payer formulary management are the real risks over 2026-2028.

### 5C. Medicare Advantage V28 Risk Adjustment Reform

This is the acute regulatory event that has driven UNH's FY2025 crisis and will continue to affect all MA insurers through FY2026-2027.

**The reform:** CMS updated the Risk Adjustment Factor methodology in Feb 2023, affecting MA payments starting CY2024. Phased in over 3 years (CY2024, CY2025, CY2026), V28 reduced risk scores for many diagnosis codes, particularly those related to cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and cancer history. The reform aimed to eliminate "coding intensity bonuses" that were inflating MA payments.

**Impact on the user's portfolio:**

- **UNH (direct exposure)** — MCR deteriorated from 85.5% (FY2023) to 89.1% (FY2025), directly attributable to V28 plus medical cost inflation. Management has explicitly stated MA membership will "contract" in 2026 as they exit unprofitable counties. The Q1 2026 earnings (April 21, 2026) will be the critical test of whether repricing has worked.

- **No other direct MA exposure** — the user does not own HUM (pure MA play), ELV (partial MA), or CI (MA divestiture pending).

**The 2027 CMS Final Rate Notice** — to be finalized April 2026, affecting CY2027 — is the next catalyst. UNH has publicly flagged that the advance notice is "far below" industry forward medical cost trend. If the final rate notice improves by 50-100bps relative to the advance notice, UNH earnings recovery is materially accelerated.

**Secondary effect on CVS:** Aetna has MA exposure, but it's smaller relative to the consolidated entity. CVS is less MA-concentrated than HUM.

### 5D. Biosimilar Wave 2024-2026

Biosimilars to major pharmaceutical blockbusters have been entering the US market at an accelerating pace:

- **Eylea (REGN, aflibercept)** — biosimilar (Yesafili, Biocon) launched 2024; REGN responded with Eylea HD (aflibercept 8mg) high-dose formulation.

- **Humira (ABBV, adalimumab)** — the biggest biosimilar event in pharma history. US biosimilars launched Jan 2023; market share has flowed gradually to biosimilars through 2024-2025. ABBV's Humira revenue collapsed from ~$21B (2022 peak) to ~$9B (2025E); Skyrizi and Rinvoq have more than offset.

**2026-2027 biosimilar risks:**

## PART VII — KEY METRICS GLOSSARY FOR NON-SPECIALIST READERS

Because this report covers specialized healthcare terminology, a brief glossary for the general dividend investor:

- **MCR (Medical Care Ratio):** For managed care, the percentage of premium revenue paid out as medical claims. Lower is better for profitability. 85-88% is typical; above 89% signals stress.
- **IRA Drug Price Negotiation:** The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 enabled CMS to negotiate prices for high-spend Medicare Part D drugs. Phased in over 2026-2028. Reduces pharmaceutical revenue by ~30-50% on affected drugs.
- **GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 receptor agonist):** Drug class including Ozempic/Wegovy (semaglutide), Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide). Originally for diabetes, now dominant in obesity treatment.
- **ADC (Antibody-Drug Conjugate):** Targeted oncology class combining antibody targeting with cytotoxic payload. Examples: Enhertu (AZN), Padcev (PFE), Adcetris (PFE), Trodelvy (GILD).
- **CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitor):** Diabetes device. FreeStyle Libre (ABT) and Dexcom are the two dominant products. Fast-growing market.
- **Biosimilar:** Biologic drug equivalent to an originator biologic, approved via abbreviated pathway (BPCIA in the US). Lower-cost but not quite generic; typically captures 30-60% market share from originators over 3-5 years.
- **Patent cliff:** The steep revenue decline a pharmaceutical company faces when a blockbuster drug loses patent exclusivity and generic/biosimilar competition launches.
- **Keytruda (pembrolizumab):** MRK's immuno-oncology blockbuster. Largest-selling drug in the world ~$25B annual revenue. Patent expires 2028 — single biggest patent cliff in pharma history.
- **Humira (adalimumab):** ABBV's immunology blockbuster that was the best-selling drug in history. Patent cliff 2023-2025.
- **V28 Risk Adjustment:** CMS updated methodology for calculating Medicare Advantage risk scores. Reduced payments to MA insurers by ~2-4% starting CY2024.
- **Dividend King:** Company with 50+ consecutive years of dividend increases. Extremely rare status; healthcare Kings are JNJ, ABT, BDX.
- **Dividend Aristocrat:** Company with 25+ consecutive years of dividend increases and is an S&P 500 member. Healthcare Aristocrats include MDT, ABBV (via combined Abbott history), SYK.
