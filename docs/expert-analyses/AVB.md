## ¿Qué hace AvalonBay?

AvalonBay Communities, Inc. (NYSE: AVB) es **una corporación Maryland que ha elegido ser tratada como REIT a efectos del impuesto federal sobre la renta** (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 1, *"is a Maryland corporation that has elected to be treated as a real estate investment trust ('REIT') for federal income tax purposes"*). Sede en Arlington, Virginia. La compañía tenía **nearly 3,000 associates** trabajando en operaciones, desarrollo, construcción al cierre de 2025 (cita: Schall transcript Q4 FY2025 ¶3).

### Geografía + portfolio per 10-K

Per 10-K FY2025 Item 1, los segments operativos son:

**Established regions:**
- **New England** (Boston metro + área)
- **New York / New Jersey metro area**
- **Mid-Atlantic** (Washington DC, Baltimore, Northern Virginia)
- **Pacific Northwest** (Seattle metro)
- **Northern California** (San Francisco Bay Area)
- **Southern California** (Los Angeles, Orange County, San Diego)

**Expansion regions:**
- **Raleigh-Durham, Charlotte (North Carolina)**

- **Denver (Colorado)**

Total **11 states + DC** con 320 apartment communities (98,694 apartment homes) al 31-dic-2025 (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 2). De ese total:
- **24 están bajo construcción** (Development Communities) que se espera **agreguen 8,572 apartment homes + 69,000 sq ft commercial space** con costo capitalizado total **~$3,307M** (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 2, *"to add a total of 8,572 apartment homes and 69,000 square feet of commercial space to our portfolio for an estimated total capitalized cost, including land acquisition costs, of approximately $3,307,000,000"*).
- **8 están en JVs no consolidadas** con 2,394 apartment homes
- **Rights sobre 32 development opportunities adicionales** = 9,032 apartment homes potential (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 2)

### Las 3 brands

Per 10-K FY2025 Item 1, AVB usa diferenciación de brand para servir múltiples customer segments:

- **Avalon**: premium luxury, full amenities. (Brand histórica original)
- **AVA**: urban contemporary, millennial focused (cita: 10-K Item 1)
- **eaves by Avalon**: targeted al "cost conscious 'value' segment primarily in suburban areas" (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 1, *"eaves by Avalon is targeted to the cost conscious, 'value' segment primarily in suburban areas"*)
- **Kanso**: launched recientemente, "designed to create an apartment living experience that offers simplicity without sacrifice at a more moderate price point, featuring high-quality apartment homes, limited-to-no community amenities and a low-touch, largely self-service operating model that leverages technology and smart access" (cita: 10-K FY2025, Item 1)

Esta cuarta brand (Kanso) es nueva — el management menciona **2 starts en 2025** (cita: Birenbaum transcript Q4 FY2025 ¶42, *"another townhome BTR community planned, and we give another Kanso planned"*) — y refleja que AVB experimenta con product types fuera del traditional luxury apartment.

Multi-brand strategy (cita: 10-K Item 1) targets multiple customer segments + submarkets. AVA millennial-focused, eaves value, Kanso "simplicity at moderate price point", Avalon premium. Differentiation vs single-brand competitors.

**$1.65B starts en 2025 con yield 6.2% inicial** + 24 communities en construcción agregando 8,572 homes a costo $3.3B (cita: 10-K Item 2 + transcript Q4 ¶3). 30 años de development experience — saben evaluar entitlement risk + cost overruns.

**Developer Funding Program (DFP)**: capital provisto a 3rd party developers + acquisition pipeline opportunistic. Per Birenbaum (transcript Q4 ¶18), también permite buying assets "at a loss" cuando otros developers no pueden financiar.

2024: AVB **único peer en raise equity en size** ($900M @ 5% implied cost forward) cuando otros estaban "on the sidelines" (cita: Schall transcript Q4 ¶3, *"we were the only one to raise equity capital in size in 2024, having raised almost $900 million of equity on a forward basis at an implied initial cost of 5%"*).

Esta secuencia es **textbook contrarian capital allocation** — raise high cuando market lo permite, buy back cheap cuando market lo permite. La gran mayoría de REITs no ejecutan este timing.

## Riesgos

### 1. Same-store revenue growth desacelerando 2.1%→1.4% (probabilidad alta — esto ya pasó)

Guidance 2026 +1.4% < 2025 actual +2.1%. Management razona: weaker job + demand backdrop H1 2026. Si H2 no acelera, puede ser worst case scenario.

### 2. Mid-Atlantic + Boston job losses (probabilidad media — depend macro)

**Mid-Atlantic perdió ~60K jobs H2 2025** (cita: Breslin transcript Q4 ¶47) — DOGE federal cuts + demand softness. Boston también lost jobs back half 2025 (cita: transcript Q4 ¶4). 

Mid-Atlantic 2026 guide: ~1% revenue growth (con 30bps mejora occupancy + 30bps mejora bad debt). Boston: 40bps decline occupancy proyectado.

Si federal layoffs continúan o major employers también recortan, puede haber further erosión.

### 3. Denver heavy supply persistente (probabilidad alta — confirmed)

**16,000 units delivered 2025 + 9,000 más en 2026** + 0 net job growth 2025 (cita: Breslin transcript Q4 ¶53). Built-in lease rate growth **-1%** Denver. AVB's expansion region performing peor.

### 4. Rising interest rates vs capitalized interest dynamic (probabilidad media)

Capitalized interest GAAP rate **3.7%** vs cost of capital ~5% (cita: O'Shea transcript Q4 ¶5). Diferencia genera headwind temporal en construction projects. Si rates suben más, gap puede ampliarse.

### 5. California regulation tightening (probabilidad media — already happening)

**AB1414 California**: "provides residents with the option to opt out of a bulk Internet program" — drag en other rental revenue (cita: Breslin transcript Q4 ¶39). 

**Colorado SB legislation 2025**: limits fees ability + utility recovery — 15bps OpEx drag + revenue impact (cita: Breslin transcript Q4 ¶39).

### 6. Massachusetts rent control ballot 2026 (probabilidad baja-media)

Per Breslin (transcript Q4 ¶39): *"the way that ballot initiative was drafted is pretty onerous. And so onerous enough that already various political leaders in Massachusetts have already come out and said that they are opposed to it"*. Set up to defeat más fácil que California previas, pero risk no zero.

### 7. Transaction activity headwind 2026 (probabilidad alta — already in guide)

Dispositions disciplinadas pero earnings short-term pain.

### 8. Recession + tenant stress (probabilidad media — siempre tail risk)

Bad debt 2025 1.6% — manejable. NABE forecasting 750,000 net new jobs 2026 (cita: Schall transcript Q4 ¶3). Si recession material, premium tenants resilient pero bad debt sube.
