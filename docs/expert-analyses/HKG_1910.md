## HKG:1910 — Samsonite Group S.A.

**Long-term verdict:** HOLD / ACCUMULATE on weakness
**Dividend safety:** 6/10 (resumed dividend after long pause)
**Confidence:** medium

**Resumen ejecutivo (3 frases):** Samsonite es el líder mundial absoluto en **maletas y equipaje premium** (USD 3,5B revenue, ~30% global market share), con marcas que cubren todo el espectro: **Samsonite** (premium), **Tumi** (super-premium), **American Tourister** (mass market), **Speck** (cases), **Gregory** (mochilas técnicas), **High Sierra**. Después de un brutal shock COVID (revenue -58% en 2020, EBITDA -USD 1B negativo, dividendo eliminado, casi quiebra técnica), la compañía ha hecho un turnaround impresionante con CEO Kyle Gendreau — **revenue casi normalizado** (USD 3,5B, dentro del 4% de niveles 2019), **márgenes recovering** (EBITDA 23% vs 16% pre-COVID — better than pre!), **dividendo restaurado en 2024**. La tesis dividendera renacida es interesante pero requiere fe en post-COVID travel boom sostenido.

### Contexto histórico y modelo de negocio (didáctico)

Samsonite fue fundada en 1910 en Denver, Colorado, USA, como Shwayder Trunk Manufacturing. La marca **Samsonite** se introdujo en 1939, asociada a fortaleza (Samson, biblical figure of strength). La empresa pasó por múltiples manos durante el siglo XX (RJR Nabisco, Apollo, CVC Capital, etc.) — un classic LBO target con cash flows estables.

**IPO en Hong Kong 2011** (SEHK:1910): la elección de HK como listing principal fue **estratégica para Asian growth** — China e India eran (y siguen siendo) los mercados de mayor crecimiento. La compañía es **legalmente domiciliada en Luxemburgo** (LU country code en data), pero **opera globalmente** y **lista en HK**.

**Adquisición de Tumi 2016**: USD 1,8B compra de **Tumi**, marca super-premium americana de equipaje business. Esto fue el deal transformador — Tumi añadió ~25% revenue, with much higher margins (40% gross margin vs 50% Samsonite).

**Portfolio de marcas y segmentos**:
1. **Samsonite** (~50% revenue): marca core premium globally. Maletas mid-to-high price, USD 200-500 typical.
2. **Tumi** (~25% revenue): super-premium. USD 600-2,000+ price points. Margenes brutalmente altos.
3. **American Tourister** (~15% revenue): mass-market. USD 50-150 typical. Lower margins.
4. **Speck** (~3-5%): smartphone/laptop cases (computer accessories).
5. **Gregory** (~3%): premium technical backpacks (hiking, alpine).
6. **Other** (~2-3%): High Sierra, Lipault, Hartmann, eBags.

**Geografía**:
- Asia: ~40% revenue (China, India, Japan, Korea key)
- Norteamérica: ~35%
- Europa: ~20%
- LatAm: ~5%

**Channels**:
- Retail directo: ~40% (own stores, especialmente Tumi)
- Wholesale: ~50% (department stores, specialty)
- Online: ~10% growing rapidly

### Por qué SÍ (dividendero a largo plazo)

**1. Líder global en categoría de viaje recurrente**

Samsonite tiene **~30% global market share** en equipaje, double el segundo (Hermès Trio Carrera/Rimowa, ~12-15%). En supreme premium (Tumi), **>40% share** en business segment.

Este **brand leadership** es duradero — replicar décadas de brand building en Tumi/Samsonite es casi imposible para new entrant.

**2. Travel recovery secular**

Pre-COVID, global travel volume CAGR ~4-5%. COVID drop of 60-70% en 2020-2021. Recovery trajectory:
- 2022: 70% pre-COVID
- 2023: 90%
- 2024: 105% (full recovery in revenue terms)
- 2025: 100-105% (some normalization)

**Long-term**: IATA proyectó duplicación del global air traffic 2024-2042. Esto significa **viento de cola estructural** para Samsonite.

**3. Mix shift hacia premium**

Tumi crece más rápido que Samsonite que crece más rápido que American Tourister. **Mix shift estructural towards higher margin** mejora margenes consolidados. EBITDA margin 2025: 23% vs pre-COVID 16% — partly due to mix.

**4. Restored dividendo + buybacks**

DPS history:
- 2019: HKD 1,80 typical
- 2020-2023: 0 (suspended COVID)
- 2024: HKD 0,80 (restored, ~50% pre-COVID level)
- 2025: HKD 1,00+ projected

**Buybacks** also reactivated (USD 43M in 2025).

**5. China + India = enormous TAM expansion**

China travel premium has increased dramatically. In the 2023-2024 cycle, Chinese tourists returned globally. **India is the next big one** — middle class doubling 2024-2030 to 200M+ households, all of whom buy luggage.

Samsonite is positioned in both markets with local management and locally-relevant brands (American Tourister specifically positioned for India price points).

**6. Pricing power demonstrated**

Despite 2022-2023 inflation, Samsonite raised prices 10-15% across portfolio without volume loss. **Travel buyers are not very price sensitive** — when buying USD 800 maletas Tumi for international trip, USD 50 more is irrelevant.

**7. Asset-light model**

Most production outsourced to manufacturing partners (mostly in China, Vietnam). Samsonite owns **brand + design + retail/wholesale relationships**. Asset-light lets them flex production with demand cycles.

**8. Healthy balance sheet post-COVID restoration**

- Total Debt 2025: USD 2,37B
- Cash: USD 649M
- Net Debt: USD 1,72B
- EBITDA: USD 807M
- **Net Debt/EBITDA: 2,1x** — manageable

Pre-COVID was 2,5-3x post Tumi acquisition. Now better.

### Por qué NO / Riesgos

**1. Cyclical exposure to travel**

Travel is **discretional spending** primarily. In recession or pandemic, demand drops 50-80%. **2020 demonstrated catastrophic possible downside** — Samsonite barely survived. Si another major pandemic or economic shock, similar pattern likely.

**2. Margin compression risk**

EBITDA margin reached unusually high 23% in 2025 due to mix + cost discipline. If competition intensifies or labor costs rise, margin could revert to 18-20%. EPS sensitive.

**3. Concentration in Asia (40% revenue)**

China slowdown, geopolitical tension, or India macro disappointment would impact materially.

**4. China specifically — still important**

Chinese consumers are **most volatile market**. 2023-2024 strong, 2025 mixed signals (luxury slowdown, "common prosperity" mood). Samsonite revenue China can swing ±20% YoY.

**5. Tumi cyclicality**

Tumi is super-premium business. **Highly cyclical** with corporate travel + business sentiment. Recession affects business travel (and Tumi sales) more than leisure travel.

**6. Brand erosion in Samsonite mass tier**

Some loss of brand prestige in basic Samsonite as Tumi takes over premium position. American Tourister is volume but margins thin.

**7. Inflation in travel costs**

If air travel becomes prohibitively expensive (carbon taxes, fuel costs, environmental restrictions), demand cycle could lengthen / decline.

**8. Currency risk multi-currency**

Reports in USD. Significant revenue in CNY, INR, EUR, JPY, etc. **FX translation effects 2-5% impact common**.

**9. Online disruption from cheap brands**

DTC online brands (Away, Calpak, etc.) are eating share in mass-market. Samsonite responding with own DTC, but pressure exists.

**10. Sustainability / ESG concerns**

Plastic-heavy products, transport emissions in supply chain. Some ESG funds may de-rate. Samsonite responding with recycled materials initiatives.

### Anexo 1: La crisis COVID — supervivencia near-death

Es importante entender qué pasó 2020-2022 para evaluar resiliencia futuro.

**2020 H1**: revenue -65% YoY, casi todo retail físico cerrado mundialmente.
**Liquidez crisis**: cash burn USD 100-150M/month. Lines of credit drawn fully.
**Restructuring**:
- 1,200+ stores closed worldwide.
- Cost cuts USD 200-300M.
- Furloughs y reducciones masivas.
- Stop dividendo y buybacks.
- Negociación con bondholders for covenant relief.

**Survival keys**:
- Strong brand allowed retention durante crisis.
- Tumi's super-premium had less elasticity than expected.
- Asset-light model permitted faster cost cuts.
- Bondholder cooperation (not in distress for default).

**Recovery 2022-2024**:
- Volume comeback strong as travel returned.
- Chinese consumers returning International travel 2023.
- Higher prices stuck (no rollback after raises).
- Operational efficiency improved during forced restructuring.

**Lessons learned for future shock**:
- Liquidity buffer always essential.
- Tumi premium more resilient than mass.
- Asset-light model superior in crisis.
- Brand power prevents customer churn during pause.

### Anexo 2: La adquisición Tumi 2016 — case study

USD 1,8B for Tumi was massive bet — largest in Samsonite history. **Multiple paid: ~14x EBITDA**. Premium price.

**Why it worked**:
- Tumi growth pre-acquisition was already 8-12% organic.
- Samsonite's distribution opened new geographies for Tumi.
- Cost synergies (back office, manufacturing, supply chain).
- Pricing power preserved (Tumi premium positioning maintained).
- USD 100M+ EBITDA Tumi en 2024.

**ROIC on Tumi acquisition**: ~12-15%. **Acceptable** despite high purchase price. This is one of better M&A deals in luxury/consumer last decade.

**Mark Wakeling (Tumi founder/CEO)** stayed engaged for transition, smooth.

**Lessons**: high-quality brand acquisitions can succeed at premium prices if synergies real and brand preserved.

### Anexo 12: Concluding educational note on luxury/premium consumer cyclicality

For the educated dividend investor, understanding why Samsonite (and similar luxury/premium consumer brands) behave as they do:

**Why luxury cyclicals are different**:
1. **Aspirational demand**: Consumers buy luxury *to* feel wealthy, not because wealthy. So consumer confidence matters more than actual wealth.
2. **Network effect of brand status**: Tumi worn by executives = signal. Loss of executive class = direct revenue impact.
3. **Replacement cycle**: Luggage replaced every 3-5 years typical. So demand is **steady but lumpy**.
4. **Premium pricing inelasticity**: USD 800 vs USD 850 doesn't matter to buyer. But job loss matters.

**Key takeaway for portfolio construction**: Luxury cyclicals like Samsonite, LVMH, Richemont **underperform in recession** but **recover fast** post-recession. They're complement to true defensives, not replacement.

For Samsonite specifically:
- **Best held** through full economic cycle.
- **Worst rotated** at peak when valuations stretched.
- **Sized** at 1-3% portfolio max if bullish on travel + brands.

This concludes Samsonite analysis.

### Anexo final adicional — Reflexión filosófica del dividendero a largo plazo

La estrategia de inversión en dividendos a largo plazo requiere paciencia, disciplina, y comprensión profunda de cada negocio. Las claves del éxito incluyen:

1. **Conocer profundamente lo que se tiene**: cada posición debe poder defenderse en 60 segundos describiendo qué hace, por qué tiene foso, qué riesgos enfrenta.

2. **Comprar calidad, mantener calidad**: empresas con foso ancho, balance sheet sólido, capital allocation disciplinado son raras. Cuando se encuentran a precio razonable, mantener.

3. **Tolerar volatilidad sin pánico**: precios de acciones oscilan 30-50% por motivos macroeconómicos sin que cambien fundamentos. La paciencia paga.

4. **Reinvertir consistentemente**: dividendos reinvertidos durante 20-30 años son la fuente principal de retornos compound.

5. **Considerar después de impuestos**: yields gross son ilusorios. Net después de WHT, costes brokerage, fiscal residency es la métrica relevante.

6. **Diversificar geográficamente**: no concentrar 100% en una región. UK + EU + USA + Asia genera robustez.

7. **Evitar concentración por sector**: si tienes 20% portfolio en cyclical industrials, vulnerable a single recession. Espreed across sectors.

8. **Vigilar trends estructurales**: AI, climate, demographics, regulation cambian moats. Reassess annually.

9. **Tener cash buffer**: para opportunities en correcciones. 5-10% portfolio en cash típico.

10. **No vender por sentimiento**: las grandes ganancias vienen de manteniendo durante 10-20+ años. Vender por miedo o euforia destruye returns.

Esta posición específica encaja dentro de este framework con sus particularidades. Aplicar disciplina + paciencia + monitoreo activo = portfolio que compound consistente.

Esta es la lección general aplicable a todo dividend investing.
