## ¿Qué hace exactamente OBDC? (didáctico)

OBDC es un **Business Development Company (BDC)** — vehículo cotizado regulado SEC bajo Investment Company Act of 1940 §54 que **presta dinero a empresas medianas privadas**.

(Para repaso fundamental de qué es un BDC, ver el análisis MSDL o BIZD — aquí me centro en OBDC específicamente.)

### El modelo OBDC en detalle

OBDC fue lanzado en 2016 como Owl Rock Capital Corp, IPO 2019. En 2021, Owl Rock se fusionó con Dyal Capital para formar **Blue Owl Capital** (NYSE: OWL), un giant alternative asset manager con $170B+ AUM. OBDC sigue siendo el BDC flagship de Blue Owl.

OBDC opera en el segmento **upper middle market** (UMM) — empresas con EBITDA $50M-1B, más grandes que el típico mid-market. Esto es **importante**:
- UMM borrowers son más estables, menor default rate (~1.5% vs sector 2.5%).
- Loans tickets más grandes ($50-300M typical) — economies of scale en underwriting.
- Competition más limitada (solo few firms tienen capability para underwrite UMM).

### Portfolio composition (Q1 2025)

- **Total investments**: ~$13B.
- **Number of portfolio companies**: ~200.
- **% First-lien senior secured**: 80% (vs sector 75-80% average).
- **% Sponsor-backed**: 95% (PE firm-backed loans, lowest default risk class).
- **% Floating rate**: 95% (high beta to interest rates — beneficia in high-rate env).
- **Geographic**: 95% USA, 5% selective international.
- **Industry diversification**: software/tech (20%), business services (15%), healthcare (12%), industrial (10%), distribution (8%), consumer (7%), financial services (6%), other (22%).

### Recent Merger — OBDC + OBDE

In late 2024 / early 2025, OBDC merged with **OBDE** (Blue Owl Capital II), its sister fund:
- OBDE was BDC focused on similar UMM market, started 2017.
- Merger of equals — OBDC issued shares to OBDE holders.
- Combined entity: ~$17B total assets, $13B invested (+30% scale).
- Synergies: reduced overhead, scale benefits, more diversified portfolio.

This merger explains the 29.7% share count growth in 2025 (from finGrowth data).

## Deep dive — Blue Owl Capital ecosystem

OBDC is part of a broader **Blue Owl Capital ecosystem**. Understanding this is important.

### Blue Owl Capital (NYSE: OWL)

The publicly traded asset manager:
- **AUM**: $170B+ across multiple strategies.
- **Strategies**: Direct lending, GP equity (private equity stakes), real estate, technology lending.
- **Structure**: Founded 2021 from merger of Owl Rock and Dyal Capital.
- **Public BDC vehicles**: OBDC (this BDC, formerly Owl Rock Capital).
- **Private BDCs**: Owl Rock BDC II (lower fee, institutional-only).
- **Other vehicles**: Blue Owl Real Estate, Blue Owl Tech Lending.

### What this means for OBDC

1. **Dealflow advantage**: Blue Owl sees thousands of deals per year. OBDC gets pick of the best ones.
2. **Operational expertise**: Blue Owl has 1000+ investment professionals; OBDC benefits from this scale.
3. **Cross-investment opportunities**: OBDC can co-invest with other Blue Owl vehicles (sometimes).
4. **Manager alignment**: OWL (the publicly traded parent) wants OBDC to perform well — affects OWL stock indirectly via mgmt fees.

### Conflicts of interest watch

- Blue Owl operates multiple vehicles in same market. Allocation of deals is critical.
- SEC-mandated "best execution" standards apply.
- Blue Owl has internal allocation committee.
- Risk: high-quality deals may go to private (institutional-only) vehicles first; OBDC gets second tier.
- Mitigant: regulatory scrutiny, board oversight.

## Capital structure analysis

OBDC's debt is a mix:
- **Bank revolvers** ($3-4B): low cost, floating rate. Drawn as needed for new investments.
- **Bonds** ($3-4B): unsecured notes at various maturities (2026-2030).
- **CLO/securitization debt** ($1-2B): leveraged via CLO structures, lower cost.

This diversified debt structure is actually **superior** to many BDCs that rely on single bank line. Reduces refinancing risk.

**Maturity schedule**:
- 2026: ~$1B
- 2027: ~$1.5B
- 2028: ~$2B
- 2029-2030: rest

Manageable schedule, no concentrated maturity wall.

## Income flow analysis

Where does OBDC's $1.1B revenue come from?

1. **Interest income**: ~$1B (~91% of revenue) — this is the main engine.
   - Floating rate component: 95% of loans, mostly SOFR + 5-7% spreads.
   - As SOFR has risen (2022-2024), interest income has surged.
   - As SOFR potentially declines (2025-2026 if Fed cuts), interest income falls.

2. **PIK interest** (paid-in-kind): when borrowers pay interest in additional debt rather than cash. ~5% of OBDC interest is PIK.
   - Sounds bad but actually OK for solid borrowers (preserves cash for growth).
   - Watch ratio — if PIK > 20% means borrowers struggling.

3. **Fee income**: ~$50-100M (~5-9% of revenue) — origination fees, monitoring fees, prepayment fees.

4. **Dividend/equity income**: ~$10-20M (~1-2%) — from equity stakes in some borrowers.

The dependence on **floating rate interest income** (>85% of revenue) is OBDC's biggest opportunity AND biggest risk.

## FAQ rápido

**Q: ¿Por qué OBDC trades at 0.78× NAV?**

**Q: ¿Es OBDC mejor que ARCC?**
A: Different. ARCC is "blue chip" — bigger, longer history, premium valuation. OBDC is "value play" — smaller, discount valuation, higher yield. Most pros hold both.

**Q: ¿Pierdo dinero si Fed corta tipos?**
A: NII pressure, possible dividend cut. But OBDC's NAV is more stable than NII (NAV doesn't move with rates much). So total return depends on capital appreciation potential vs dividend yield.

**Q: ¿Cuál es el peor escenario para OBDC?**
A: Recession + Fed cuts simultaneously. Both NAV (defaults rise) AND NII (rates fall) hit at same time. Stock could drop 30-40%.

**Q: ¿Cuál es el mejor escenario?**
A: Sustained high rates + low default cycle. NII strong, NAV stable, multiple expansion. Stock could rise 50-70% in 18 months.

**Q: ¿Es buena idea reinvest dividends en OBDC?**
A: At 0.78× P/NAV, yes — DRIP at discount to NAV is good capital allocation. As multiple normalizes, less attractive.

**Q: ¿Qué hago si tengo solo OBDC y nada más en BDCs?**
A: Consider diversifying with ARCC (1-2%) for safety, MAIN (1%) for premium. Don't be 100% concentrated in single BDC.
