## ¿Qué hace exactamente IIPR? (didáctico)

IIPR es el primer y mayor **REIT cotizado especializado en cannabis real estate** en EEUU [Source: 10-K FY2025 sección Business — "We are an internally-managed REIT focused on the acquisition, ownership and management of specialized industrial and commercial properties in the United States. Our properties are primarily leased to experienced, state-licensed operators for their regulated cannabis facilities"]. Fue incorporada en Maryland el 15 de junio de 2016 [Source: 10-K FY2025] y salió a IPO en diciembre 2016.

### El modelo de negocio

IIPR no cultiva cannabis. **No tocan la planta directamente** — eso es importante para mantener compliance con las leyes estatales mientras a nivel federal sigue Schedule I [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "Cannabis (with the exception of hemp containing no more than 0.3% THC by dry weight) is illegal under U.S. federal law. The U.S. federal government regulates drugs through the CSA. The CSA classifies marijuana (cannabis) as a Schedule I controlled substance"]. Lo que IIPR hace es:

1. **Compran propiedades industriales** (warehouses, greenhouses, indoor cultivation facilities) adaptadas para cannabis.
2. **Las rentan via "sale-leaseback" agreements** a operadores cannabis estatales licenciados [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "We have acquired and expect to continue to acquire our cannabis properties through sale-leaseback transactions and third-party purchases. These properties are generally leased, and we expect to continue leasing them on a triple-net lease basis"].
3. **Cobran rents en triple-net structure** — el inquilino paga property taxes, insurance, maintenance, además de la renta.
4. **Yields iniciales premium** vs industrial REITs convencionales, por el gap regulatory.

### El "cannabis premium" en yields

¿Por qué los cultivadores cannabis pagan rent significativamente más alta que los warehouse tenants normales?

1. **Sin acceso a financiación tradicional**: los bancos no prestan a cannabis [Source: 10-K FY2025 sección "Laws Applicable to Financial Services" — "Despite these laws... many banks are hesitant to offer any banking services to cannabis-related businesses, including opening bank accounts. While we currently maintain banking relationships, our inability to maintain those accounts or the lack of access to bank accounts or other banking services in the future, would make it difficult for us to operate our business"]. Los operadores cannabis no pueden hipotecar a tasas convencionales — necesitan vender el real estate y rentarlo back.
2. **Capital intensivo build-outs**: instalar una facility cannabis requires "enhanced HVAC systems for climate and humidity control, high capacity electrical and plumbing systems, specialized lighting systems, and sophisticated building management, cultivation monitoring and security systems" [Source: 10-K FY2025], todo lo cual IIPR financia.
3. **High effective ROIC**: cannabis margins son altos, pueden permitirse rents premium [UNSOURCED: numerical claim of 50-60% gross margins on flower omitted — requires citation from operator filings].

## Calidad del negocio

### Lo que IIPR tiene de bueno

1. **First-mover advantage**: el más grande, el más reconocido [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "We are the first and largest real estate investment trust focused on the regulated cannabis industry"]
2. **Properties tangibles**: si un tenant defaulta, IIPR conserva el edificio. Los re-leasing de 2025-2026 demuestran que las facilities tienen demanda real.
3. **High operating margin**: 46.7% en 2025 (cayó del 54.6% en 2024 pero sigue muy alto vs industrial sector promedio 30-40%) [Source: 10-K FY2025 + cálculo]
4. **Cash flow real**: $258M OCF en 2024 [Source: 10-K FY2025 cash flow statement — referenced 258,446 in line 1800/3454]
5. **Bajo leverage**: Net Debt/EBITDA 1.4x [Source: Q4 2025 transcript] — extremely low for a REIT, gives runway to weather la crisis sin financial distress.
6. **Management team experimentado**: Alan Gold (Executive Chairman) y Gary Kreitzer (Vice Chairman) co-founded BioMed Realty (NYSE: BMR), exitosa REIT life-science vendida a Blackstone en 2016 [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "in August 2004, Mr. Gold and Gary Kreitzer ... founded BioMed Realty Trust, Inc. (formerly NYSE: BMR) ... an internally-managed REIT focused on acquiring, developing, owning, leasing and managing laboratory and office space for the life science industry"]. Tienen el playbook.
7. **Diversificación incipiente hacia life science**: $270M commitment a IQHQ Preferred [Source: 10-K FY2025] reduce dependencia 100% cannabis.

### Lo que IIPR tiene de malo

1. **Tenant concentration**: top 10 = 75% del rent. Si Curaleaf o Cresco tuviesen problemas serios, sería material [Source: 10-K FY2025 risk factors]
2. **Sector cíclico estresado**: 21.5% del rent en default a 31-dic-2025 demuestra que la crisis está in process, no resolved.
3. **Regulatory risk asimétrico**:
   - Si federal legalization NO pasa: sector restrictive forever, tenant defaults continúan
   - Si federal legalization SI pasa: llegan banks con financiamiento traditional 4-6%, IIPR pierde su yield premium estructural — multiple comprime [Source: 10-K FY2025 Risk Factors — "due to a number of factors, including but not limited to potential greater clarity of the laws and regulations governing regulated cannabis by state and federal governments, the number of entities and the amount of funds competing for suitable investment properties may increase substantially, resulting in increased demand and increased prices paid for these properties"]
4. **Re-leasing dilution**: re-rents pueden ser hasta 50% por debajo del contrato original [Source: Q4 2025 transcript — Ben Regin]
5. **California exposición**: dos properties (San Bernardino y Palm Springs) están evaluando uso non-cannabis por cambios de zoning [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "we continue to evaluate alternative non-cannabis uses for the properties, due in part to changes in the zoning of the properties that no longer allow for regulated cannabis cultivation and processing"]
6. **IQHQ exposure**: la inversión $270M en IQHQ Preferred (private REIT) introduce nuevos risks — illiquidity, dependency on issuer performance [Source: 10-K FY2025 Risk Factors sección IQHQ-related]
7. **Bond maturity Mayo 2026**: $291M Notes due 2026 deben refinanciarse a tasas higher [Source: 10-K FY2025]

**Veredicto Calidad**: 6/10 — modelo brilliant en boom, fragile en bust, pero el management está ejecutando bien la transición. Score sube de 5/10 (versión anterior) a 6/10 reflejando re-leasing visible y diversificación life-science.

## El executive order de diciembre 2025 — el catalizador potencial

El 18 de diciembre de 2025, el presidente Trump firmó un Executive Order ordenando rescheduling de cannabis a Schedule III [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "on December 18, 2025, President Trump issued an Executive Order (the 'Executive Order'), Increasing Medical Marijuana and Cannabidiol Research, that directs the Attorney General in the most expeditious manner in accordance with federal law, to complete the rulemaking process related to rescheduling that was begun during the Biden Administration"].

### ¿Por qué importa tanto?

**Section 280E** del Internal Revenue Code prohíbe a businesses traficando con Schedule I/II controlled substances deducir gastos ordinarios (salaries, depreciation, interest) [Source: 10-K FY2025 — "Section 280E of the Code provides that, with respect to any taxpayer, no deduction or credit is allowed for expenses incurred during a taxable year 'in carrying on any trade or business if such trade or business... consists of trafficking in controlled substances'"]. Resultado: cannabis operators pagan tax federal effectively en revenue, no en profit. **Effective tax rate cannabis operators: 60-80%** [UNSOURCED specific range — verificable con 10-K de Curaleaf/Trulieve/GTI].

Si cannabis se reclassifica a Schedule III, **280E ya no aplica** y los operators pueden deducir gastos normalmente, bajando effective tax rate a ~21% corporate norm. El management de IIPR estima en el Q4 call: "rescheduling may eliminate the punitive impact of 280E for our tenants which we believe would meaningfully improve operator cash flows, strengthens credit profiles and support additional investments across the industry" [Source: Q4 2025 transcript — Paul Smithers].

### Pero el timing y completion son inciertos

[Source: 10-K FY2025 — "However, it remains unclear if reclassification will occur and if so how long the reclassification process will take or whether reclassification will result in cannabis companies no longer being subject to Code Section 280E"]
