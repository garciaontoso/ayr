## ¿Qué hace exactamente NET.UN? (didáctico)

Canadian Net REIT es un **micro-cap Canadian REIT** especializado en **commercial real estate triple-net** distribuido por toda Canadá.

### El modelo "Triple-Net" (NNN)

Es **importante** entender este concepto porque define todo:

En un lease "triple-net" (NNN):
- El **tenant** paga rent base + property taxes + insurance + maintenance ("triple net" = tres categorías de costos transferidos).
- El **landlord** (REIT) recibe rent net puro — sin overhead de property management.
- Resultado: REIT operations son muy lean, márgenes operativos altísimos (>80%), but rent growth es slow (lease escalations 1.5-2.5%/year).

Triple-net REITs son la categoría más predictible y boring del real estate. Los más conocidos:
- **Realty Income (O)**: $50B mcap, monthly dividend, "The Monthly Dividend Company" branding.
- **STAG Industrial (STAG)**: industrial NNN focused.
- **National Retail Properties (NNN)**: smaller cousin de O.
- **NET.UN**: la versión Canadian micro.

### El portfolio NET.UN

- **~80 propiedades** distribuidas en todas las provincias canadienses.
- ~70-75% retail single-tenant (drugstores, banks, fast food, gas stations, dollar stores).
- ~15-20% office (small market profesional offices, no Class A premium).
- ~5-10% industrial (warehouse, light manufacturing).
- **WALT (Weighted Average Lease Term)**: ~9 años — buena duración.
- **Top tenants**: Loblaw (Canadian Tire), Dollarama, Tim Hortons, banks, government agencies.
- **Geographic mix**: ~40% Quebec, 25% Ontario, 15% Western, 20% Atlantic provinces.

### Por qué Canadá vs USA

NET.UN es **Canadian REIT** — esto es importante por varias razones:

1. **Currency**: distribuciones en CAD. USD investor está exposed a CAD/USD fluctuations.
2. **Tax treatment**: Canadian REITs distribuciones tienen complications fiscal:
   - Para US holders: 15% Canadian WHT on distributions (treaty rate).
   - Distribución can be ROC (return of capital), CAD dividend, or capital gain — different tax treatment each.
   - Para Chinese tax residents: similar 15% WHT applies.
3. **Market structure**: Canadian REITs trade in CAD on TSX/TSXV. NET.UN específicamente está en **TSXV (TSX Venture Exchange)** — junior exchange con menos requirements y liquidez menor.
4. **Sector structure**: Canadian commercial real estate market is healthier than US in some ways (less office vacancy, less retail apocalypse) — Canadian malls have not collapsed like US malls.

## ¿Qué es FFO/AFFO en context Canadian REIT?

Mismo concepto que US REITs:

**FFO** = Net Income + D&A − gains.
**AFFO** = FFO − maintenance capex − non-cash adjustments.

Canadian REITs tienen una pequeña diferencia: además de AFFO suelen reportar **AFAD** (Adjusted Funds Available for Distribution) que es similar pero ajusta también por cash distributions actualmente paid (vs accrued).

## Deep dive — Canadian REIT taxation for foreign holders

This is **complex** but important to understand. Canadian REIT distributions are NOT all the same tax-wise.

### Components of Canadian REIT distribution

A Canadian REIT distribution can include:
1. **Ordinary Canadian dividend** (eligible vs non-eligible — different gross-up rates for Canadian residents).
2. **Foreign business income** (less common for domestic REITs).
3. **Capital gains** (when REIT realizes property sales).
4. **Return of capital (ROC)** — reduces investor cost basis, NOT taxable when received but increases capital gain at sale.
5. **Other items** (small).

For a foreign holder (US or Chinese), ALL these are **subject to Canadian WHT 15% under tax treaty** (provided you fill out W-8BEN equivalent).

### Specifically NET.UN distribution breakdown (estimated typical)

- ~50% ROC (no current tax, reduces cost basis)
- ~35% Other income (taxed as ordinary)
- ~10% Foreign income (taxed)
- ~5% Capital gain (taxed at cap gain rate for some)

### Practical implications

**If you hold NET.UN $10K worth, get $530 distribution (5.3% yield)**:
- Canadian WHT: $80 (15% × $530).
- You receive: $450 net of WHT.
- US/Chinese tax authority then taxes the $450 (or $530 with tax credit for Canadian WHT in some cases).

**For Chinese tax resident** (per user memory):
- 15% Canadian WHT.
- China taxes the gross distribution at 20% but does NOT credit Canadian WHT in many practical cases (depending on your filing).
- Net yield: ~5.3% × 0.65 = ~3.4% in pocket.

### ROC basis adjustment

The ROC component (50% of distribution) reduces your cost basis. If you bought at $6.56 and receive $0.35/year of distribution with $0.18 (50%) being ROC:
- Year 1: cost basis $6.56 → $6.38.
- Year 5: cost basis $6.56 → $5.66.
- When you eventually sell, you have larger capital gain calculated against this lower basis.

**This is important**: ROC is NOT "free money" — it's tax deferred, not tax avoided.

## FAQ rápido

**Q: ¿Por qué NET.UN cotiza en CAD y no USD?**
A: Es Canadian REIT, listed on TSXV. CAD denomination is natural. Some Canadian REITs cross-list on US exchanges (e.g., AAM.UN), but NET.UN doesn't.

**Q: ¿Cómo lo compro desde Interactive Brokers?**
A: IBKR has TSX Venture access. Buy ticker NET.UN directly on TSXV. Pay commission similar to TSX (~CAD $1-5 per trade).

**Q: ¿Qué pasa con las distribuciones cuando cobro?**
A: IBKR receives net of 15% Canadian WHT, deposits in your account in CAD. You can keep CAD or convert to USD/CNY at IBKR's FX rate.

**Q: ¿Es buena idea reinvest distribuciones (DRIP)?**
A: NET.UN doesn't have official DRIP for foreign holders generally. Manual reinvest possible but small amounts may be inefficient with commissions.

**Q: ¿Está bien si la posición es solo $5-10K?**
A: Yes, micro-positions are okay for diversification but don't expect material impact on portfolio.

**Q: ¿Cómo se compara con O (Realty Income)?**
A: O is the gold standard NNN. $50B mcap, 30+ year history, monthly dividend, US-focused. NET.UN is "O Canadian micro version". O is objectively better for most holders. NET.UN appeal: Canadian diversification + yield similar.

**Q: ¿Qué señales me indicarían vender?**
A: 
- Distribution cut announcement.
- Tenant concentration rising > 30% in top 5.
- Net Debt/EBITDA > 12×.
- TSXV de-listing (would be very bad).
- Major Canadian retail tenant default cascade.

**Q: ¿Hay ETF que tenga NET.UN?**
A: Some Canadian REIT ETFs hold it (e.g., XRE, ZRE — iShares S&P/TSX Capped REIT and BMO Equal Weight REIT). But NET.UN small, only ~0.5% of those ETFs.
