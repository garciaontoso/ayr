## ¿Qué hace exactamente SHUR? (didáctico)

Shurgard opera **self-storage facilities** en Europa — los famosos "trasteros" donde gente y empresas guardan cosas que no caben en su casa o oficina.

### El modelo self-storage

Self-storage es **uno de los mejores subsectores de real estate** por varias razones estructurales:

1. **Demand pegajoso**: una vez que un cliente alquila un trastero y mete sus cosas, no se va fácilmente. Average tenure 18-24 meses.
2. **Costos bajos para operador**: trasteros no necesitan mucha infrastructure (no HVAC complex, no plumbing, no offices). Maintenance capex muy bajo.
3. **Margen operativo alto**: 60-70% NOI margin (vs 50-60% otros REITs).
4. **Pricing power**: cada año puedes subir rents 3-6% sin perder muchos customers.
5. **Diversified customer base**: residential (50%), small business (35%), commercial (15%) — no single-tenant concentration.
6. **Defensive**: en recesión, gente downsize de casa grande a casa pequeña → necesitan storage. Demand stable.

### Why Europe is different

US self-storage market is mature:
- **8 sqft per capita** of self-storage in US.
- ~52,000 facilities.
- Highly competitive.

European market is **WAY less developed**:
- **0.8 sqft per capita** in UK (best European market).
- 0.4 sqft per capita in Germany, France.
- 0.1-0.3 in other European countries.
- ~3,500 facilities in all of Europe combined (vs 52K in US).

**The structural opportunity**: European self-storage is **10-30 years behind US**. As Europe urbanizes, apartments shrink, e-commerce grows, the demand for self-storage will expand dramatically.

Forecasts: European self-storage market expected to grow 6-8% annually for next decade vs US ~3%.

## ¿Qué es FFO/AFFO en self-storage REITs?

(Standard for European REITs but with EU GAAP adjustments.)

**FFO** = Net Income + D&A − fair value gains on investment property.

European REITs (REOPCs in Belgium, Spanish SOCIMIs, etc.) use slightly different accounting under IFRS vs US GAAP. Specifically:
- They typically **mark properties to fair value** through P&L (vs depreciation in US).
- This makes Net Income very volatile — fair value gains/losses dominate.
- For comparability, **EPRA Earnings** (a standardized European REIT metric) is preferred.

**EPRA Earnings** ≈ Operating Income + Interest Expense (excluding fair value movements). It's similar to FFO + interest costs.

**EPRA NAV** ≈ Total assets fair-valued − liabilities − equity adjustments. Like a "fair value book equity".

## Deep dive — el mercado europeo de self-storage en detalle

Para entender Shurgard como inversión, hay que entender el mercado.

### Por qué Europa está under-penetrated

Reasons US has 10× more self-storage per capita:

1. **Larger homes**: US average new home 2,500 sqft vs UK 1,000 sqft, France 800 sqft, Germany 950 sqft.
2. **Garages are common**: US homes have garages (storage). European apartments don't.
3. **Mobility patterns**: Americans move every 5 years on average, creating storage transitions. Europeans more rooted.
4. **Cultural differences**: Americans accumulate more stuff; some European cultures emphasize minimalism.
5. **Industry maturity**: Self-storage industry developed in US 1960s, only arrived in Europe 1990s.

But these gaps are **closing**:
- European homes shrinking (urbanization, smaller apartments).
- Apartment living without basements/attics → need external storage.
- E-commerce explosion → more goods to store.
- Increased mobility (university, jobs in cities far from family).
- Aging population (downsizing creates need for transitional storage).
- Small business growth (SaaS startups, e-commerce sellers).

### Country-by-country dynamics

**United Kingdom**:
- Most mature European storage market.
- ~0.8 sqft per capita (still 1/10 of US).
- Big Yellow + Safestore are biggest competitors.
- Rents stable, occupancy >90%.
- Brexit impact on commercial demand variable.

**Germany**:
- Second-largest economy, much smaller storage market.
- Industry still nascent, ~0.4 sqft per capita.
- Highly fragmented; lots of small operators.
- Strong growth potential.
- Sub-leasing controls in some cities limit pricing.

**France**:
- Centralized, Paris-dominant.
- Higher acquisition costs in cities.
- Strong growth in suburbs.
- Sécurité concerns (theft) drive demand.

**Netherlands, Belgium**: Mature small markets.
**Sweden, Denmark**: Recent expansion.

### Shurgard's competitive moat

1. **First-mover scale**: 270+ stores vs nearest competitor ~150.
2. **Brand strength**: "Shurgard" most recognized European storage brand.
3. **Operational systems**: invested in IT, online booking, dynamic pricing.
4. **Capital cost advantage**: as larger entity, cheaper financing than small operators.
5. **Land bank**: own developable land that can be built later.
