## ¿Qué hace Comcast?

Comcast Corporation (NASDAQ: CMCSA) es **el mayor operador cable + broadband USA** con sede en Filadelfia. Origen 1963 (Roberts family). Hoy con ~190,000 empleados, revenue ~$125B.

### Las 4 categorías de productos

**1. Connectivity & Platforms (~50% revenues)**
- **Xfinity Cable** (broadband + TV + voice)
- **Xfinity Mobile** (MVNO using Verizon network)
- **Sky Group** (UK + Italy + Germany cable + satellite)
- **Comcast Business** (B2B telecom)

Margen alto (~35%+). Driver cash flow.

**2. Content & Experiences — NBCUniversal (~38%)**
- **Media** (NBC, MSNBC, USA, CNBC, Bravo, etc.)
- **Studios** (Universal Pictures, DreamWorks, Focus Features)
- **Theme Parks** (Universal Orlando, Hollywood, Japan, Beijing, Epic Universe Orlando opened 2025)
- **Peacock** (streaming service)

Higher revenue, lower margin (~15-20%).

**3. Sky International (~10%)**
- UK + Italy + Germany cable + satellite (already in segment 1 mostly)
- Diversification

**4. Other (~2%)**
- Comcast Spectacor (sports + venues)
- Various ventures

### Peacock — el game changer

Peacock es **el streaming service de NBCUniversal**:
- **46M subscribers Q1 2026** (+12% YoY)
- **Revenue $2.1B Q1** (vs $1B Q1 2025 — DOUBLE)
- **Approaching profitability Q2 2026** (first time)
- Content: NBC live + Premier League Soccer + Olympics + WWE + originals

**Q1 2026 driver**: Milan Cortina Winter Olympics + Super Bowl LX boosted Peacock subs + advertising revenue.

Si Peacock turns profitable Q2 2026 onwards = catalyst major.

### Universal Theme Parks — premium asset

Universal Studios theme parks:
- Orlando + Hollywood + Japan + Beijing
- **Epic Universe Orlando opened 2025**: largest USA theme park expansion in decades
- Universal Beijing growing
- Premium attractions Wizarding World, Jurassic World, Fast & Furious

Theme parks margen ~25-30% — premium business.

### Estructura accionarial

- **Roberts family**: ~33% voting (Class B supervoting shares)
- **Free float**: ~67%

**Roberts family controlled** desde fundación. Brian Roberts CEO 2002+.

CEO **Brian L. Roberts** (CEO desde 2002, Chairman desde 2004) — son of founder Ralph Roberts. Mike Cavanagh President.

### Geografía

- **USA (~75% revenues)**: Xfinity dominant
- **UK + Europe (~15%)**: Sky operations
- **International (~10%)**: theme parks Japan/Beijing

## Moat — qué hace defendible Comcast

### 1. Xfinity broadband network

Largest USA cable + fiber network. ~30M broadband customers. Difficult to replicate.

### 2. NBCUniversal premium content

NBC + Universal + Peacock + parks = premium content engine. Olympics + Premier League + WWE + Wicked.

### 3. Universal Theme Parks IP

Orlando + Hollywood + Japan + Beijing + Epic Universe. Unique attractions. Pricing power.

### 4. Roberts family control

Voting control = long-term strategy. Sin pressure short-term.

### 5. Buybacks aggressive

$5B+ anuales reducing share count = accretive at low múltiplos.

### 6. Bundling broadband + mobile

Xfinity Mobile (MVNO Verizon) + Xfinity Internet bundling = stickiness customers.

### 7. Sky International diversification

UK + Italy + Germany broadband + satellite = geographic diversification.

### Lo que NO es moat (sincero)

- **Cord-cutting secular** wireline TV
- **Competition fiber** Verizon/AT&T accelerating
- **Streaming wars** content costs masivos
- **Net Debt elevated** $95B
- **Sin AI/tech direct exposure**
- **CapEx heavy** $11B/año

AT&T + Verizon + Frontier fiber expansion. Comcast cable losses share.

### 3. Streaming wars cost

Content production costs $20B+ NBCUniversal annual. Peacock losses still material.

### 4. Net Debt $95B elevated

Manageable pero limits flexibility.

Consumers cancel premium services first.

Recession + travel slowdown affect parks attendance.

NBC + cable advertising sensitive to economic cycles.

### 8. Roberts family control

Sin pressure pero limits activist M&A potential.

Brian Roberts 65 años. Mike Cavanagh sucesor probable.
