## ¿Qué hace Centene?

Centene Corporation (NYSE: CNC) es **el mayor proveedor USA de managed care para Medicaid + ACA exchanges + Medicare** con sede en St. Louis, Missouri. Fundada 1984 por Elizabeth Brinn. Hoy con ~70,000 empleados, revenue ~$170B (#22 Fortune 500).

### Las 4 categorías de productos

**1. Medicaid (~60% revenues)**
Government program for low-income Americans:
- **Managed care** contracts con states
- **30+ states con presence**
- **20M+ Medicaid members** (largest USA)
- Brands: Centene, Wellpoint, IlliniCare, Sunshine Health, etc.

Margen operativo: 2-3% (regulated by state contracts MLR thresholds). Volume-driven business.

**2. Medicare (~15% revenues)**
- Medicare Advantage (MA) plans para seniors 65+
- Medicare Part D prescription drugs
- ~5M MA members
- WellCare brand acquired 2020 ($17B)

Margen operativo similar Medicaid (~3-4%).

**3. ACA Marketplace / Health Insurance Exchanges (~15% revenues)**
- Affordable Care Act (Obamacare) marketplaces
- ~3.6M members Q1 2026 (was 5.6M end 2025)
- Ambetter brand
- Growth durante COVID/post-COVID, declining 2026 post-APTC expiration

Margen 1-3%, volatile based on enrollment + risk pool composition.

**4. Specialty + Other (~10%)**
- Envolve Health (vision, dental, etc.)
- Pharmacy benefit management (PBM)
- International (small operations Spain + UK)

### Estructura accionarial

- **Free float**: ~99%
- **Top institucionales**: Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street
- **Sin accionista controlador**

CEO **Sarah London** desde marzo 2022 — interna, ex-COO. **Continuidad estratégica con foco execution + cost cutting + portfolio simplification**.

### El modelo de negocio: managed care states

Centene contrata con **state governments** para manage Medicaid populations:
- Estado paga **per-member-per-month (PMPM)** capitation rate
- Centene asume risk de medical costs > capitation
- Margen depende de **Medical Loss Ratio (MLR)**: % de premium gastado en medical care
- Lower MLR = higher margin
- States regulate MLR (typical 85-90%)

Para Medicaid: federal + state shared funding. Centene captures volume + slim margin.

### Historia M&A

Centene ha crecido masivamente vía M&A:
- **WellCare 2020 ($17B)**: Medicare Advantage expansion
- **Magellan Health 2022 ($2.2B)**: behavioral health
- **Operation Vergie (UK Bupa Russia exit)**
- **Envolve Health acquisition**: dental/vision

Trayectoria M&A mixed — algunos integration challenges + write-offs.

### Geografía: USA only

100% USA exposure. State-by-state contracts. Heavy concentration:
- **Florida**: Sunshine Health (largest Medicaid CNC operation)
- **California**: California Health & Wellness
- **Texas**: Superior HealthPlan
- **New York**: Wellcare of NY
- **Illinois**: IlliniCare

## Riesgos

### 1. Republican Medicaid cuts (probabilidad media-alta)

Trump 2.0 + Republican Congress podrían propose Medicaid cuts:
- Per-capita caps (block grants)
- Work requirements
- Reduced federal matching

Each could reduce Centene revenue 10-25%.

### 2. ACA expiration acceleration (probabilidad media)

Si Republicans repeal more ACA provisions, marketplace shrinks more.

### 3. Medical inflation (probabilidad estructural)

Medical inflation continues > general inflation. State capitation rates may not keep up.

### 4. Pricing pressure states

States want to reduce Medicaid spending. Capitation rate negotiations adverse.

### 5. Specific state losses

If Centene loses major state contract (Florida, California, Texas), revenue impact masivo.

### 6. M&A integration risks

Past WellCare integration cost overruns. Future M&A risk.

### 7. Regulatory CMS

Centers for Medicare/Medicaid Services regulations evolving constantly.

### 8. ACA risk pool deterioration

If healthier members leave, sicker members stay = MLR worsens.

### 9. Sin dividend = limited investor base

Income investors no consideran Centene.

### 10. Sucesión London

London CEO desde 2022. Cualquier cambio = uncertainty.
