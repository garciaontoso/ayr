## ¿Qué hace Omega Healthcare?

Omega Healthcare Investors, Inc. (NYSE: OHI) es **el mayor REIT especializado USA en skilled nursing + assisted living facilities** con sede en Hunt Valley, Maryland. Fundada 1992. Hoy con presencia en **42 states + UK**.

### Portfolio overview

- **800+ properties total**
- **~75% Skilled Nursing Facilities (SNFs)**: clinical post-acute care, Medicare-driven
- **~25% Assisted Living Facilities (ALFs)**: senior housing, private pay
- **UK exposure**: ~12% of revenues
- **Investment grade tenant base**: 70%+ rated A-

### Top tenants (concentration)

- **Genesis Healthcare**: ~9% rents (post-restructuring 2021)
- **Maplewood Senior Living**: ~7%
- **Healthpeak / others**: balance

### Las 4 categorías de revenues

**1. Triple-net lease rentals (~85% revenues)**
- Long-term leases (15-20 years)
- Tenants pay maintenance + insurance + taxes
- Rent escalators 2-3% anual

**2. Mortgage notes receivable (~10%)**
- Loans to operators secured by facilities
- Higher yield ~9-10%

**3. Joint ventures (~3%)**
- RIDEA structures with operators
- Share operating profits

**4. Investment securities (~2%)**

### Business model dynamics

OHI buys SNF/ALF properties + leases to operators. Risk shifts to operators:
- Tenants margin: ~12-15% EBITDAR
- Rent coverage: target >1.3x EBITDAR/rent
- Operators struggle when Medicare rates compress or labor inflation extreme

### Estructura accionarial

- **Free float**: ~99%
- **Top institucionales**: BlackRock, Vanguard, State Street

CEO **C. Taylor Pickett** desde 2001 — **uno de los CEOs longest-tenure REIT USA**. Ejecutando turnaround post-COVID + Steward Health crisis.

## Riesgos

### 1. Tenant credit risk (probabilidad estructural)

SNF operators thin margins. Cualquier operator stress = OHI rent risk.

### 2. Regulatory Medicare/Medicaid rates (probabilidad estructural)

CMS rate-setting affects operators. Reductions = operator stress = OHI risk.

### 3. Labor inflation SNF

Nursing labor costs rising. Operators absorb most but eventually flows up.

### 4. Aging facilities

Some SNF properties aging, capex maintenance high.

### 5. Net Debt elevated 5.5x EBITDA

High for REIT. Manageable but limits flexibility.

### 6. UK exposure currency + Brexit

GBP volatility. Brexit healthcare regulations.

### 7. Sucesión Pickett

Pickett 65+ años. Sucesión planning matters long-term.

### 8. Competition Welltower, Ventas

Larger healthcare REITs compete for premium properties.

### 9. ALF private pay sensitivity

Recession affects ALF demand (private pay).

### 10. Steward Health residual issues

Some Steward properties still in transition.
