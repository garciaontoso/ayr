## ¿Qué hace exactamente REXR? (didáctico)

Rexford Industrial es un **REIT puro industrial** especializado en una **estrategia muy específica**: comprar y operar warehouses, distribution centers, y light manufacturing facilities en los **mercados infill de Southern California** (SoCal).

### ¿Qué significa "infill"?

**Infill** se refiere a properties in **already-developed urban areas** — entre buildings existentes, no en suburbios o exurbios sin desarrollar. En Southern California específicamente significa los **mercados infill costeros**:

- **Los Angeles County**: South Bay, San Fernando Valley, San Gabriel Valley.
- **Orange County**: especially central/north OC.
- **Inland Empire West**: parts of Riverside and San Bernardino counties closer to LA.
- **San Diego County**: parts of central San Diego.

**Por qué importa** — estos mercados son **únicos**:
1. **Limited supply**: California zoning + environmental rules + community opposition makes building new industrial nearly impossible.
2. **High demand**: ports of LA/Long Beach handle 40% of US imports. Need warehouses nearby.
3. **No new supply growth**: while Inland Empire (East) saw massive new construction 2018-2023, infill markets cannot expand.
4. **Premium rents**: SoCal infill rents $15-25/sqft NNN vs Inland Empire $9-12 vs national avg $8-10.

REXR's 232 properties cover ~28M sqft across these markets.

### ¿Por qué Industrial REITs son buenos?

Industrial real estate has been the **best-performing REIT sector** of the 2020s:
- **E-commerce demand**: Amazon, Walmart, Target, FedEx, UPS need warehouse + distribution.
- **Onshoring/reshoring**: post-COVID, US companies want supply chain resilience → more warehouses domestically.
- **Last-mile delivery**: hub-and-spoke model needs many small "last mile" warehouses near urban centers.
- **Cold storage growth**: refrigerated warehousing growing 8-10% annually.
- **High occupancy**: industrial sector at 95-97% occupancy historically.
- **Pricing power**: in tight markets, rents grow 5-10% annually.

REXR has captured this perfectly — concentrated in supply-constrained markets with highest pricing power.

## ¿Qué es FFO/AFFO en industrial REITs?

(Repaso para context — same fundamentals as other REITs.)

**FFO** = Net Income + D&A − gains. Standard NAREIT.

**AFFO** = FFO − maintenance capex − tenant improvements − leasing commissions − straight-line rent adjustments.

**P/AFFO multiples industrial REITs**:
- Premium quality (PLD Prologis, REXR, FR First Industrial): 25-32×.
- Mid-tier (STAG Industrial, EGP EastGroup): 18-23×.
- Distressed/specialty: 12-17×.

## Deep dive — el Southern California industrial market

Para entender REXR, necesitas entender el SoCal industrial market.

### El market structure SoCal

SoCal es el **mayor industrial market en EEUU**:
- ~1.6 billion sqft industrial space total.
- ~2x el size del próximo mayor (NJ/NY).
- Vacancy históricamente: 1-3% (ultra-tight).
- Rents históricamente: $12-22/sqft NNN.

### Sub-mercados específicos REXR

REXR's portfolio breakdown:
- **South Bay LA** (Carson, Compton, Torrance): ~40% — ports proximity, premium logistics.
- **San Gabriel Valley** (Industry, La Puente): ~25% — central LA distribution.
- **San Fernando Valley** (Northridge, Sun Valley): ~15% — northern LA, manufacturing heritage.
- **Inland Empire West** (Ontario, Riverside near LA): ~10% — last-mile distribution.
- **Orange County**: ~10% — central OC, premium tenants.

### Why infill SoCal cannot expand

Multiple barriers:
1. **Land scarcity**: virtually all developable land already developed.
2. **Zoning challenges**: California's CEQA + community opposition makes rezoning impossible.
3. **Environmental regs**: SoCal Air Quality Management District (SCAQMD) restricts truck operations near residential.
4. **Infrastructure**: roads can't handle more truck traffic in many submarkets.
5. **Cost economics**: even if land available, construction cost $300+/sqft makes new builds uneconomic at current rents.

The result: **existing buildings have natural moat**.

### Trends 2024-2025

**Negative**:
- Inland Empire (East) saw 200M+ sqft of new construction 2021-2023, now vacancy ~6-8%.
- This **spillover** affects pricing in IE West and into LA fringe.
- Rent growth has slowed from 10-15% YoY (2022) to 2-4% (2025).
- Cap rates expanded from 4.0% (2021) to 5.5% (2024) — NAV decline implication.

**Positive**:
- Port LA/LB traffic 2024 was second-highest year ever.
- Onshoring discussions continue (some manufacturing moving from China).
- Cold storage expansion accelerating.
- Last-mile demand stable.

## Mark-to-market opportunity (the secret REIT play)

This is **the most underappreciated** aspect of REXR:

REXR has 232 properties with leases at various stages. Many leases were signed 5-7 years ago when SoCal industrial rents were $10-15/sqft. Today market rents are $20-25/sqft.

When existing leases expire and renew (or are released to new tenants), rents jump to market.

**REXR's "mark-to-market" pipeline**:
- 2024: 8.4M sqft renewed at +43% rent increase.
- 2025 estimated: 7.5M sqft renewed at +35% rent increase.
- 2026 estimated: 7M sqft renewed at +25% rent increase (slowing as backlog clears).

This represents **embedded rent growth** REXR will capture over coming years even if market rents stay flat.

Quantification:
- ~30% of REXR's 28M sqft renews each year.
- At +30-35% mark-to-market, that's **~10% same-property NOI growth per year** purely from rolling existing leases.
- Plus 2-3% market rent growth annually.
- = **12-13% annual SP NOI growth potential**.

This is **extraordinary** for a REIT. Most REITs grow SP NOI at 2-4%. REXR can grow 10%+ for 3-4 more years before mark-to-market is fully captured.

If this materializes, AFFO grows from $2.45 to $3.20+ by 2028, dividend can grow 10%+ annually.
