## TL;DR (resumen en 30 segundos)

Wendy's es la **3ª cadena de hamburgueserías USA** (después de McDonald's y Burger King por número de locales — aunque MCD aplasta a ambos en revenue). Están dominados por **Trian Partners** (hedge fund de Nelson Peltz) que es accionista mayoritario y ha presionado para "leveraged dividend recap" — pagar dividendo agresivo financiado con deuda.

Razones: (1) **negocio retail QSR estancando** (3 años de same-store sales flat-down), (2) **dividendo recortado en 2025** de $1.00/sh a $0.56/sh (-44%), (3) deuda enorme post-recap ($4.1B con equity de solo $117M — leverage extreme), (4) competition feroz (McDonald's app + delivery, Chipotle, In-N-Out, Whataburger expansion, Shake Shack), (5) Burger King reborn under Restaurant Brands.

**Veredicto preliminar**: **SELL para dividendero a largo plazo**. Recortar dividendo es el evento que **descalifica a un income stock**. La combinación de leverage extremo + estancamiento operativo + recorte reciente sugiere que dividendo podría seguir bajo presión. Yield 8% suena attractive pero es **trampa de yield clásica**.

**Dividend safety**: 4/10. **Confianza**: alta sobre el problema. La trayectoria es bear.

## ¿Qué hace exactamente esta empresa? (sin jerga)

Wendy's es la cadena de hamburgueserías founded 1969 por Dave Thomas en Columbus, Ohio. Marca conocida por:
- **"Square hamburgers"** (no redondas como MCD/BK).
- **"Frosty"** (helado-bebida especial).
- **Marketing humorístico en redes** (Twitter de Wendy's es famoso por roasts).
- **"Where's the beef?"** comercial 1984.

### El modelo de negocio

Wendy's NO opera la mayoría de los restaurantes — los franquicia. El revenue mix:

1. **Royalties (~40% revenue)**: cada franquiciado paga 4-5% de sus ventas a Wendy's HQ.
2. **Franchise rentals (~25%)**: Wendy's es dueña de muchos terrenos/edificios y los alquila a franquiciados.
3. **Sales at company restaurants (~30%)**: las ~400 ubicaciones que Wendy's opera directamente.
4. **Otros (~5%)**: training, equipment sales, advertising fund.

**Margen estructura**:
- Royalties + rentals son **alto margen** (~80%) — es income recurrente.
- Company restaurants son **margen normal de QSR** (~20% restaurant-level margin).
- Combined operating margin ~25%.

### La estrategia "Image Activation" y aceleración

Desde 2018, Wendy's ha presionado por **modernizar restaurantes** ("Image Activation"), expandir delivery (DoorDash, UberEats), launching breakfast (2020), y crecer internacionalmente. Resultados mixed:
- **Breakfast**: lanzado USA marzo 2020 (justo COVID start). Difícil pero ahora ~10% de ventas USA.
- **Delivery**: ~15-20% sales mix.
- **International**: 1,000+ restaurantes fuera USA (Reino Unido, India relaunch 2024, Spain entry).
- **Same-store sales USA**: fluctuando entre +1% y -3% últimos 3 años.

### El problema del "Trian leveraged recap"

Nelson Peltz / Trian Partners tomó posición control en Wendy's circa 2018. Su receta:
1. **Levantar deuda agresivamente** ($4B+ en bonds).
2. **Pagar special dividends** y aumentar dividend regular.
3. **Free cash flow del business pagar el interest** indefinidamente.

Esto es teoría "leveraged equity" — usar leverage para amplificar returns al equity holder. Funciona si:
- El business genera FCF predecible.
- Tipos de interés se mantienen bajos.
- No hay disruption competitive o macro.

En 2018-2021 funcionó. Pero después:
- **Tipos USA subieron** (interés expense de Wendy's casi se duplicó).
- **Inflación operativa** (labor +25%, food +20% in some categories).
- **Competition intensificada**.
- **Same-store sales se estancaron**.

Y el FCF, que solía cubrir cómodamente dividendo + interest, **se estrechó**. Resultado: el dividendo fue **recortado en 2025** de $1.00 a $0.56 anual (-44%).

## Calidad del negocio

### Moat

**Moat MEDIO en QSR brand recognition; FUERTE en franchise model economics; pero DEBILITANTE por competencia + labor inflation**.

- **Brand recognition**: Wendy's es marca top-3 USA QSR burgers. Reconocible.
- **Franchise system locked-in**: 95% franquiciado significa low capex requirement + recurring royalties.
- **Real estate ownership**: Wendy's posee ~$2B+ en real estate (terrenos restaurantes).
- **Switching costs cero** para customer (sale a otra burger chain mañana).
- **No price power**: cuando suben precios, customer migra a alternative.

**Yield 8% es siempre yield-trap signal** en restaurantes con balance like this.

## Riesgos clave

### 1. Otro recorte de dividendo

Si EBITDA cae 10%+ (recesión, comp sales decline), interest coverage se aprieta y management probablemente recortará otra vez. Stock fácilmente -25% más.

### 2. Refinancing risk

Wendy's tiene $4.1B en bonds maduros 2025-2032. Cuando refinanci, los nuevos bonds vienen a tasas mucho más altas (junk grade + tipos altos). Interest expense puede subir 30-50%.

### 3. Same-store sales declining

Si the comp sales se quedan negativas (ya están -1% to -3%), revenue erosiona y EBITDA con él.

### 4. Competition intensifying

McDonald's app mejorada + delivery + value menus. Burger King reborn. In-N-Out/Whataburger expanding nationally. Shake Shack premium positioning. Wendy's se aprieta de todas direcciones.

### 5. Labor cost inflation

QSR depends on minimum wage workers. Subidas de salario mínimo (especialmente California $20/hr restaurant minimum) presionan margins.

### 6. Trian Partners exit

Si Trian decide vender su posición (es ~13-15% holder), stock could crash on supply.

### 7. Recesión USA

QSR trading down patterns ayudan a value chains pero Wendy's NO es value (es mid-tier). Recession would hurt.

## Análisis profundo: el Trian Partners playbook

### Quién es Nelson Peltz / Trian

Nelson Peltz es un **legendary activist investor** — fundador de Trian Fund Management. Su track record:
- **Procter & Gamble** (2017-2022): Fights con management → eventually settled.
- **PepsiCo** (2014-2016): pushed for split → didn't happen.
- **DuPont** (2014-2017): forced merger with Dow Chemical.
- **Sysco**: simpler operational improvements.
- **Wendy's** (2008-present): largest holding of Trian.

Style: take significant minority stakes, push for "operational improvements" + "shareholder returns" (often via dividends/buybacks).

### El "Trian playbook" applied to Wendy's

1. **Take ~12-15% stake** (achieved circa 2008-2018).
2. **Get board representation** (Peltz himself sat on Wendy's board).
3. **Push for asset-light franchising model** (already happened — 95% franchise now).
4. **Demand "shareholder-friendly" capital allocation**:
   - Special dividends.
   - Aggressive buybacks.
   - Leveraged dividend recapitalizations.
5. **Eventually exit** when value extracted.

### Why this hurts long-term investors

Activist playbook **shifts value from balance sheet to current shareholders**. This:
- Creates short-term stock pop (special dividend → stock price reflects, holders profit).
- Burdens future cash flows with debt service.
- Reduces operational flexibility.
- Makes the business fragile to downturns.

When activists exit, they often leave behind a **highly levered, financially fragile company**. New shareholders bear the risk.

### Wendy's case study

- 2018: Total debt ~$2.7B. Manageable.
- 2022: Total debt ~$4.2B. Aggressive recap completed.
- 2025: Net debt / EBITDA = 7.3x. Distressed.
- 2025: Dividend cut -44% inevitable.

**The pattern is textbook**.

## Análisis profundo: la operación QSR

### Modelo unit economics

A typical Wendy's restaurant in USA:
- Average annual sales: ~$1.8M
- Restaurant-level margin: ~17-19%
- Restaurant-level EBITDA: ~$320-340k
- Royalty to Wendy's HQ (4%): ~$72k
- Rent (if HQ-owned): ~$120-150k
- Net to franchisee: ~$130-150k

Franchisee has invested ~$2.5-3M building the restaurant. Cap rate effectively 5%.

### The struggle since 2020

- **Labor inflation**: +25%+ in cost of restaurant labor (state minimum wage hikes, post-COVID labor shortage).
- **Food inflation**: beef +20%, fries +15%, dairy +10% in 2022-2024.
- **Same-store sales**: marginally positive +1-2%, doesn't keep pace with cost inflation.
- **Franchisee margins compressed**: from 19% to ~15%.
- **Refranchising** has slowed — fewer franchisees willing to invest at current returns.

### The breakfast strategy (mixed success)

Launched March 2020:
- Initial uptake strong but COVID dampened benefits.
- Today: ~10% of USA sales mix.
- Profitability: lower margin than core (more food cost ratio).
- Competitive: McDonald's breakfast is dominant, very hard to displace.

### International expansion

Positive:
- UK presence growing (~150 restaurants).
- India relaunch 2024.
- Spain entry 2024.

Negative:
- International margins lower than USA.
- Building new markets is expensive.
- Won't move the needle materially for 5+ years.

## Datos macro adicionales

### USA QSR industry trends

- Total industry: ~$300B.
- Growth: +3-4% annually (mostly inflation, not unit growth).
- Burger segment specifically: ~$100B, growing slower than overall QSR.
- "Better burger" segment (Shake Shack, Five Guys, Smashburger): growing 2x faster.
- Value chain segment (McDonald's, Wendy's, BK): growing slower.

### Demographic headwinds

- Younger generations (Gen Z, Millennials) less burger-focused than predecessors.
- Plant-based alternatives growing (although slowed 2024-2025).
- Health consciousness affecting QSR mix.

### Delivery dynamics

Delivery via DoorDash/UberEats:
- Pros: capture additional sales not possible without delivery.
- Cons: 25-30% commission to apps, margin reduction.
- Net: positive but margin-dilutive.
