## ¿Qué hace AT&T?

AT&T Inc. (NYSE: T) es **el segundo operador telecom USA** con sede en Dallas, Texas. Origen 1885 (Bell Telephone). Hoy con ~150,000 empleados, revenue ~$122B.

### Estructura post-divestments (2026)

Tras años de complejidad (compra DirecTV, WarnerMedia, etc.), AT&T ahora es **un pure-play telecom focused**:

**1. Mobility (~70% revenues)**
- Wireless service postpaid (88M customers)
- Wireless service prepaid (Cricket Wireless, FirstNet government)
- Equipment sales (devices, accessories)
- 5G network leadership (largest 5G coverage USA por sqmi)

**2. Business Wireline + Consumer Wireline (~25% revenues)**
- Fiber broadband (37M+ locations passed, growing to 40M+)
- Legacy wireline (declining)
- Business networking (enterprise + government)
- Lumen consumer fiber acquisition (4M locations adquiridas Q1 2026)

**3. Mexico + Latin America (~5%)**
- AT&T México (3rd carrier, behind América Móvil + Telcel)
- Wholesale + roaming agreements

### Las 3 transformations 2018-2026

**Transformation 1: WarnerMedia spin-off (2022)**
- AT&T compró Time Warner $85B en 2018 (mistake estratégico)
- Spin-off completo 2022 a Warner Bros. Discovery
- Dividend cut from $2.08 to $1.11 ($0.2775 × 4) en 2022 spin
- Recovery focus pure telecom

**Transformation 2: DirecTV exit (2021)**
- Vendió 30% DirecTV a TPG por $7.6B en 2021
- Aún detiene 70% pero spin/sale eventual
- Ya no es prioridad

**Transformation 3: Convergence + Fiber (2023-2030)**
- Strategic priority: **fiber 40M+ locations + wireless convergence**
- Cross-sell wireless customers to fiber, viceversa
- Margin expansion via convergence
- 5G + Fiber as bundled offering vs cable competitors (Comcast, Charter)

### Geografía: USA dominante

- **USA (~95% revenues)**: doméstico telecom
- **México (~5%)**: AT&T México (3er carrier)

### Estructura accionarial

- **Free float**: ~99%
- **Top institucionales**: Vanguard, BlackRock, State Street
- **Activist Elliott Management**: ~1% históricamente, less active now

CEO **John Stankey** desde julio 2020 — interno, ex-CEO WarnerMedia. Ha articulado claramente la transformación turnaround. **Continuidad estratégica fuerte**.

## Moat — qué hace defendible AT&T

### 1. Spectrum 5G nationwide

AT&T tiene the largest 5G coverage USA por square mile. Spectrum acquired desde décadas. Imposible para competition de replicar.

### 2. Fiber footprint expansion

37M+ fiber locations (growing 40M+) = #2 fiber USA después de Verizon. Capex heavy investment 2018-2026 paying off ahora.

### 3. Convergence advantage

Solo AT&T tiene fiber + wireless owned at scale. Comcast/Charter solo cable. Verizon más small fiber. Convergence cross-sell uniquely AT&T.

### 4. FirstNet government contract

AT&T won FirstNet (federal first responders network) contract 2017 — exclusive multi-year. Recurring revenue + brand prestige.

### 5. Cricket Wireless prepaid

Cricket is largest USA prepaid carrier (after Metro by T-Mobile). Captures price-sensitive segment.

### 6. Brand premium 140 años

AT&T brand (origins 1885) recognized universally USA. Trust premium.

### 7. Capital allocation Stankey post-2022

Post-WarnerMedia mess, Stankey has:
- Reset dividend to sustainable level (2022)
- Divestments DirecTV + Vrio
- Focus core wireless + fiber
- Deleverage gradual
- Buybacks return 2024+

**Track record execution since 2022 = strong**.

### Lo que NO es moat (sincero)

- **No tiene tech moat AI/cloud** vs hyperscalers
- **Wireless commoditizing** vs T-Mobile/Verizon
- **Cord-cutting secular** wireline declining
- **Scale alone** vs distribution/brand
- **Capex heavy** ~18% revenues

## Riesgos

### 1. Deuda alta (probabilidad estructural)

$126B net debt + leverage 2.71x. Alto. Cualquier downturn cyclical pressure. Stankey ha articulado deleverage path pero takes time.

### 2. Competencia wireless intense (probabilidad media)

Verizon + T-Mobile compete agresivamente. Pricing pressure recurring. Churn rates rising.

### 3. CapEx 5G/fibra heavy (probabilidad estructural)

$22B/año capex. 18% revenues. Limita FCF disponible para dividend o deleverage.

### 4. Cord-cutting wireline (probabilidad estructural)

Legacy wireline business declining. Aunque fiber grows, transitioning takes years.

### 5. Mexico operations underperforming (probabilidad media)

AT&T México #3 carrier behind América Móvil + Telcel. ROI cuestionable.

### 6. Regulación FCC (probabilidad media)

FCC bajo Trump más friendly que Biden. Pero regulation always changeable.

### 7. Recession + wireless ARPU (probabilidad media)

Si consumers downtrade to cheaper wireless plans, ARPU decline.

### 8. Verizon fiber acceleration (probabilidad media)

Verizon expandiendo fiber agresivamente (Frontier acquisition pending). Could erode AT&T fiber lead.

### 9. Lumen integration (probabilidad media)

4M fiber locations from Lumen need integration. Cost overruns possible.

### 10. EchoStar deal execution

EchoStar wireless spectrum deal pending. Closing risk + integration.
