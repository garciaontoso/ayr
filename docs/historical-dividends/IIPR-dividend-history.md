# IIPR — Innovative Industrial Properties, Inc.
## Historical Dividend Evolution (2017–2025, Pre-History 2000–2016)

**Status:** High-yield cannabis REIT with hypergrowth then deceleration (8 years of public history)
**Founded:** June 2016 · **NYSE listing:** December 2016 (IPO)
**Sector:** Real Estate / Cannabis REIT (the first and dominant public cannabis landlord)
**Report date:** 2026-04-17

## Section 2 — Notable Events Chronology

### Pre-History 2000–2016: Cannabis Legalization Wave
Context: US cannabis legalization began at the state level:
- 1996: California legalizes medical cannabis (Prop 215)
- 2000s–2010s: 20+ states follow with medical cannabis
- 2012: Colorado and Washington legalize adult-use (recreational) cannabis
- 2014: Oregon, Alaska, DC legalize adult-use

Federal cannabis remained illegal under the Controlled Substances Act, creating a unique situation: state-licensed cannabis operators needed capital but couldn't access traditional banking, insurance, or mortgage lending. This "banking problem" created an arbitrage opportunity for a specialized REIT.

### 2016: IPO — Paul Smithers Era
December 2016: IIPR IPO'd at $20/share, raising $67M. The thesis:
- Buy industrial properties (greenhouses, cultivation facilities) in state-legal cannabis markets
- Lease back to state-licensed operators on 15–20 year NNN leases
- Operators get capital they can't get from banks
- IIPR earns premium cap rates (typically 9–12%) to compensate for tenant credit risk and federal illegality

Founder Paul Smithers (also CEO 2016–2024) was a SEC attorney who specialized in real estate.

### 2017–2018: Initial Scale
IIPR's first acquisition was in Maryland (2016); by end of 2017, it owned ~6 properties. **Dividend growth reflected explosive asset scaling** — each new property added meaningfully to total dividend-paying assets. 2017 dividend = $0.65 (partial year); 2018 = $1.18 (+81.5%).

### 2019: The Breakout Year
2019 was IIPR's inflection. Acquisition volume reached $300M+. Portfolio grew to 46 properties by year-end. **Dividend grew +139.8% to $2.83 annual** — the fastest single-year dividend growth of any US REIT.

**Context:** this was also when US cannabis stocks (MSOs — Multi-State Operators) were going public at high valuations. Curaleaf, Green Thumb, Trulieve, and others were raising capital hand-over-fist. IIPR's tenant base expanded rapidly.

### 2020: COVID — Cannabis Deemed "Essential"
When COVID shut down most retail in March 2020, medical and adult-use cannabis dispensaries were deemed "essential" businesses in most states (exemption from shutdown orders). Cannabis sales actually GREW 40%+ in 2020. Tenant rent collection stayed at 100%. **IIPR's dividend grew +57.6% in 2020** — the fourth consecutive year of 50%+ growth.

### 2021: Portfolio Growth, Continued Scale
Portfolio reached 103 properties by end of 2021. **Dividend grew +26.0%.** But the stock, which had peaked near $280 in November 2021, began declining as the cannabis industry's oversupply and pricing pressure became visible.

### 2022: Peak Portfolio + First Warning Signs
IIPR's portfolio hit 110+ properties by mid-2022. **Dividend growth of +21.4% in 2022** was still strong, but several MSO tenants began showing stress:
- **Kings Garden** (Arizona tenant): defaulted on rent July 2022
- **SH Parent** (Colorado): defaulted late 2022
- **Medical Investor Holdings**: defaulted late 2022
- **Parallel** (FL/PA): defaulted late 2022

Tenants faced oversupply (especially in California, Michigan, Arizona), collapsing wholesale cannabis prices, federal tax burden (280E), and limited access to capital markets.

### 2023: The Cannabis Bust Year
2023 was devastating for IIPR:
- Multiple tenant defaults: Parallel, SH Parent, Kings Garden, Medical Investor, and others
- Re-leasing cycle: IIPR had to take back properties, re-market to new operators at LOWER rents
- Rent collection dipped from 100% to 93–95% at peak distress
- **Dividend was EFFECTIVELY FROZEN at $1.80/quarter for all of 2023.** (The annual figure rose from $6.82 to $7.20 only because of a Q4 2022 timing shift; the quarterly rate did not increase.)

This was IIPR's first meaningful signal of distress. The streak of consecutive annual increases was technically preserved by timing arithmetic but felt broken in substance.

### 2024: Catherine Hastings Becomes CEO
Paul Smithers stepped down as CEO in May 2024; Catherine Hastings (CFO) became CEO. Her mandate: stabilize the tenant portfolio, re-lease distressed properties, preserve the dividend.

**Q1 2024 raise: to $1.82. Subsequent raises were also small ($0.02–$0.04 per quarter).** Annual 2024 DPS: $7.35 (+2.1%).

### 2025: Stabilization
**Dividend at $1.90 quarterly by year-end.** Rent collection stabilized at ~95–97%. Re-leased properties, primarily to better-capitalized MSOs, at 5–10% lower rents than the original deals. **2025 annual DPS of $7.60 (+3.4%)** — modest growth, reflecting normalization.

## Section 3 — Pattern Analysis

### DGR by period
- **2017–2019:** ~100%+ CAGR (hypergrowth scaling from tiny base)
- **2019–2022:** ~35% CAGR (rapid portfolio expansion)
- **2022–2025:** ~3.7% CAGR (distress + normalization)

IIPR's pattern is the **classic cannabis REIT cycle**: explosive scaling → tenant distress → normalized slow growth. The 2019–2022 era is NOT the normalized run-rate; 2025 (+3.4%) is closer to the long-term trajectory.

### Dividend growth vs S&P 500
IIPR dramatically outpaced the S&P 500 over 2017–2022 (~6x vs 1.4x cumulatively). In 2023–2025, it has underperformed. The cycle complete is roughly neutral to the S&P over the full period.

### Yield-on-cost from 2017 purchase
A holder who bought IIPR at the 2017 year-end price (~$18) with the forward $1.00 dividend had a starting yield of ~5.6%.
- 2019 YOC: 2.83 / 18 = **15.7%**
- 2021 YOC: 5.62 / 18 = **31.2%**
- 2023 YOC: 7.20 / 18 = **40.0%**
- 2025 YOC: 7.60 / 18 = **42.2%**

Phenomenal YOC if you bought at IPO — over 40% cash-on-cost. However, IIPR's stock fell from $280 peak (2021) to ~$70 (2024), meaning many retail investors who bought near the peak have suffered 60%+ capital losses and much lower YOC.

### Buyback vs dividend mix
IIPR has NOT been a meaningful buyback company — all capital has gone to new property acquisitions and dividends. Share count grew from ~10M (2017) to ~28M (2025), reflecting equity offerings to fund the portfolio.

## Section 4 — Lessons Learned

### What made IIPR a reliable dividend grower (2017–2022)
1. **First-mover advantage.** IIPR was the only significant publicly-traded cannabis REIT for most of 2017–2022. Competition was limited to private capital and more distressed lenders.
2. **High initial cap rates.** Buying at 9–12% cap rates (vs traditional industrial at 4–5%) with 5–8% cost of capital generated enormous spread. Each acquisition was immediately accretive.
3. **Cannabis-sector tailwind.** 2016–2022 saw rapid state legalization (adult-use legalization in CA/NV 2016, MA/IL/MI 2018–2020, NY 2022) and strong sales growth.
4. **Simple, focused strategy.** Pure-play cannabis industrial landlord. No forays into retail or other asset classes.
5. **NNN structure.** Tenants responsible for taxes, insurance, maintenance — protecting IIPR from operating cost surprises.

### Close calls / red flags over the years
- **Tenant concentration risk.** In 2021–2022, top tenants (Parallel, Ascend Wellness, Curaleaf) each represented 10–15% of revenue. When multiple went distressed simultaneously, IIPR faced a concentrated default risk.
- **2022–2023 Kings Garden / SH Parent / Parallel defaults.** Multiple simultaneous defaults. IIPR had to take back properties and accept re-lease at lower rents. **This is when the dividend growth stopped.**
- **2023 "freeze" via timing arithmetic.** The quarterly rate was held at $1.80 for all of 2023 — only annual comparisons looked like growth due to 2022 Q4 timing. This is as close as IIPR has come to a freeze.
- **Cannabis market oversupply.** California, Michigan, Arizona, Colorado all saw wholesale cannabis prices decline 40–70% from 2021 peak. This stressed operators across the board.
- **Federal policy risk.** Safe Banking Act has been pending for years. Without federal legalization, cannabis operators remain capital-constrained and IIPR's moat is both protected (no banks compete) AND endangered (if federal legalization passes, cheaper capital arrives).

### What to watch looking forward
- **Tenant credit quality.** Current top tenants (PharmaCann, Cresco, Curaleaf, Trulieve, Green Thumb) — are they generating positive cash flow? If MSO fundamentals worsen again, IIPR's distressed exposure could re-intensify.
- **Safe Banking Act.** If federal legislation enables traditional banking for cannabis, IIPR's competitive moat (being the only capital source) narrows. Cap rate compression would hurt IIPR acquisition economics.
- **Rescheduling.** If DEA reschedules cannabis from Schedule I to Schedule III (as proposed 2024), operators' tax burden drops dramatically (removal of 280E), improving tenant credit. Net positive for IIPR.
- **Re-leasing cycle completion.** By 2026–2027, the worst of the 2022–2023 defaults should be fully worked through. Rent collection should stabilize at 98%+.
- **Dividend growth normalization.** Can IIPR resume 5–8% DGR once the portfolio stabilizes? Or is the long-term run-rate ~3%? Management hasn't guided clearly.

### The Big Lesson
IIPR is the archetypal **high-yield, high-growth REIT that hit its ceiling** when its tenant market entered distress. The pattern (explosive growth → tenant issues → growth stalls) is common for single-sector specialty REITs. Even without a formal cut, the growth freeze of 2023 was a meaningful event.

**Conclusion:** IIPR has technically cut the dividend zero times, but the 2023 rate freeze — while preserving the raise streak on an annual-timing basis — was a real signal of business model stress. The cannabis REIT structure is structurally higher-risk than traditional net-lease due to tenant concentration, federal illegality, and sector volatility. At current yield (~10%+ depending on price), IIPR offers significant income but with commensurate risk. Expect 3–6% DGR if cannabis market stabilizes; ~0–2% DGR or another freeze if another default cycle emerges. This is NOT a widow-and-orphan dividend stock.
