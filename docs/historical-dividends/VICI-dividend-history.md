## Section 2 — Notable Events Chronology

### Pre-History 2013–2017: The Caesars Chapter 11 Bankruptcy
VICI's origin story is the Caesars Entertainment Operating Company (CEOC) Chapter 11 bankruptcy, which filed January 2015 and emerged October 2017. As part of the reorganization plan:
- CEOC's real estate (the casinos and resorts) was separated from the operating business
- The real estate became **VICI Properties**, distributed to Caesars creditors
- Caesars continued operating the casinos under 35-year triple-net master leases to VICI

**This is the single most important structural fact about VICI:** it was born from a bankruptcy restructuring as a pure-play gaming landlord. The initial portfolio (at October 2017 spin) included 19 casino properties leased back to Caesars.

### 2018: IPO and Early Formation
February 2018: VICI completed its IPO at $20/share, raising $1.4B. The initial portfolio was heavily concentrated in Caesars (the single tenant) but diversified by geography (Las Vegas Strip + regional casinos). Ed Pitoniak (CEO) and John Payne (President) came from pre-VICI real estate backgrounds — Pitoniak ran a Canadian REIT (InterRent), Payne had been a Caesars executive.

**Initial dividend strategy:** consistent quarterly raises to signal a "growth REIT" positioning, distinct from passive NNN retail peers (O, NNN).

### 2019: First Full Dividend Year
VICI completed its first full year paying four quarterly dividends totaling $1.135/share. The focus was on tenant diversification: acquired the Century Casinos portfolio (3 regional casinos), Margaritaville Resort Casino, and additional JACK Cleveland/Thistledown properties.

### 2020: COVID-19 — The Ultimate Stress Test
COVID shut down the casino industry for 2–3 months in Spring 2020. Multiple casino operators faced immediate liquidity crises. Yet VICI's rent was CONTRACTUALLY required to be paid (20-year+ NNN leases with unconditional payment obligations), and every tenant paid in full throughout 2020.

**Why tenants paid:** (1) casino leases are senior obligations with very high switching costs — you can't easily relocate a casino; (2) Caesars and other operators had Treasury PPP access, bank lines, and government liquidity support; (3) the 20+ year lease terms and near-100% payment obligation features are designed for exactly this kind of scenario.

**Dividend raise in 2020: +12.3%** — the highest in VICI's history and among the highest of any S&P 500 REIT during the pandemic. This was a deliberate statement by management that the business model had been stress-tested and was stronger than the market assumed.

### 2021: Caesars-Eldorado Merger / Las Vegas Concentration
July 2020: Eldorado Resorts completed its acquisition of Caesars Entertainment, and VICI benefited from the resulting tenant consolidation and strengthening. Caesars Entertainment (the new combined entity) became a much stronger operator. VICI's rent coverage ratio improved meaningfully.

### 2022: MGM Growth Properties Acquisition — Transformative
April 2022: VICI closed its $17B acquisition of MGM Growth Properties (MGP), doubling the portfolio and adding 15 MGM-leased properties including Bellagio, Luxor, Mandalay Bay, New York-New York, and more Las Vegas Strip assets. **This made VICI the single largest Las Vegas Strip landlord.**

**Key deal mechanics:**
- All-stock transaction
- Post-close, portfolio value ~$33B and enterprise value ~$40B
- Immediate entry into the S&P 500 (June 2022)

**Dividend raise of +8.7% in 2022** — reflecting the accretive integration.

### 2023: Post-MGP Integration, Experiential Expansion
VICI expanded beyond gaming into "experiential" assets:
- Cabot Citrus Farms (golf resort, 2023)
- Bowlero Corporation sale-leaseback (bowling, 2023)
- Canyon Ranch Wellness Resort (2023)
- Great Wolf Lodge (indoor waterpark resort, 2024)

**Dividend raise of +8.0%** — consistent with the mid-high-single-digit growth cadence.

### 2024–2025: Deceleration to Mid-Single-Digit Growth
As VICI matured and slowed acquisition pace, **dividend growth moderated to +4.3% (2024) and +4.1% (2025).** This reflects:
- Higher cost of capital (rates peaked 5%+)
- Less accretive M&A opportunity
- Natural transition from high-growth to mid-growth REIT

## Section 3 — Pattern Analysis

### DGR by period (short history)
- **2019–2022:** ~14% CAGR (startup-to-mid-cap scaling phase)
- **2022–2025:** ~5.5% CAGR (mature phase)

VICI has a 6-year DGR of ~7.6% — higher than all traditional retail net-lease REITs (O ~3%, NNN ~3%) but lower than a typical consumer staples name. The trajectory suggests 4–6% DGR over the next decade is sustainable.

### Dividend growth vs S&P 500
VICI's 7.6% DGR since 2019 has **meaningfully outpaced the S&P 500 average (~6%)** — unusual for a REIT, driven by the bankruptcy-to-public scaling story plus the MGP acquisition.

### Yield-on-cost from 2018 IPO purchase
A holder who bought VICI at the IPO price of $20.00 and received the initial partial-year 2018 dividends had a starting forward yield of ~5.3%.
- 2019 YOC: 1.135 / 20.00 = **5.7%**
- 2020 YOC: 1.275 / 20.00 = **6.4%**
- 2022 YOC: 1.500 / 20.00 = **7.5%**
- 2025 YOC: 1.760 / 20.00 = **8.8%**

### Equity issuance vs organic growth
VICI has grown share count aggressively to fund acquisitions:
- Q1 2018: ~400M shares
- Q1 2022 (pre-MGP): ~650M shares
- Q1 2023 (post-MGP): ~1,030M shares
- Q4 2025: ~1,060M shares

AFFO per share growth has been more modest than total AFFO dollar growth, similar to O. However, the MGP merger was AFFO-per-share accretive on day one (~5% immediate accretion), distinguishing VICI from O's VEREIT integration.

## Section 4 — Lessons Learned

### What made VICI a reliable dividend grower
1. **Unique asset class.** Gaming real estate has very high switching costs — casinos cannot be easily relocated. Las Vegas Strip properties are irreplaceable. This gives VICI pricing power that retail NNN lacks.
2. **Master lease structures with unconditional payment.** Unlike typical NNN where a single tenant bankruptcy could impair one property's rent, VICI's master leases require the full portfolio rent to be paid as a single obligation. This concentration of obligation strengthens VICI's credit position.
3. **Investment-grade tenants at scale.** Caesars (BB+) and MGM (BB+) are both major publicly-traded operators. Tenant diversification improved meaningfully with the MGP acquisition.
4. **COVID stress test passed.** The single biggest test VICI could have faced — a global pandemic shutting down casinos — was passed with flying colors. All rent was paid, and the dividend was actually INCREASED by double digits during the crisis.
5. **Disciplined capital allocation.** MGP was an accretive deal (AFFO per share +5% on day one); experiential expansion has been measured ($200M-500M deals, not mega-transactions).

### Close calls / red flags over the years
- **2018 IPO concentration risk.** 100% Caesars tenant concentration was a risk. Eldorado-Caesars merger 2020 + MGP 2022 dramatically diversified this.
- **2020 COVID.** Could have been catastrophic but instead validated the business model.
- **2022 MGP integration.** $17B deal carries execution risk; integration appears successful based on post-close financial results.
- **2023 regional economic slowdown concerns.** Regional casinos (vs Las Vegas) can be more economically sensitive. So far, rent coverage has held.

### What to watch looking forward
- **Rent coverage ratio.** Currently 2.0x+ (EBITDAR to rent). If this drops below 1.5x, dividend flexibility shrinks.
- **Tenant credit quality.** Caesars and MGM both have meaningful debt loads. Watch for any ratings downgrades.
- **Capital allocation.** VICI has slowed M&A post-MGP; future growth depends on (a) organic escalators (~2% CPI-linked), (b) smaller experiential deals, (c) selective new gaming acquisitions.
- **Regulatory risk.** US regional gaming is expanding (more states legalizing online/sports betting), which is a tailwind for VICI's operators. Federal gaming regulation changes would be the primary risk.
- **Digital gaming disruption.** If online casino/sports betting substantially cannibalizes brick-and-mortar visitation, long-term rent coverage could decline. Watch Caesars/MGM same-property EBITDAR trends.

**Conclusion:** VICI has a 7-year dividend history with zero cuts, zero freezes, and consistent raises — including a bold +12% increase during the 2020 COVID crisis when the casino industry was shut down. The business model (unique gaming net-lease with long-term unconditional leases) has been stress-tested. DGR has decelerated from ~10%+ to ~4–5% as VICI has matured, but this is still among the highest in the REIT universe. The short operating history is the primary risk; VICI has not yet been through a true tenant bankruptcy or prolonged recession. However, early evidence suggests this is one of the strongest dividend-growth REITs available.
