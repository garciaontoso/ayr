## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: ACCUMULATE below $55. HOLD at $61.35. Capital Return Program Safety: 7/10. Conviction: Medium.**

Block, Inc. is the most intellectually challenging position in the fintech landscape. At $61.35, the market is valuing a business generating $10.4B in gross profit (up 17% YoY), $1.71B in GAAP operating income (versus -$625M just three years ago), $1.3B in net income, and $2.42B in free cash flow at just 3.6x trailing gross profit and 15.4x trailing FCF. For context, PayPal trades at roughly 7x FCF, Shopify at 10x revenue, and even mature processors like Fiserv command 15-17x forward earnings on far lower growth rates. The disconnect exists because the market cannot reconcile two competing narratives: Block as a high-growth fintech platform with a 59-million-user neobank, versus Block as a Jack Dorsey vanity project with a bitcoin fetish, an impaired $29B acquisition, and a now-40%-smaller workforce.

Our reading of six years of SEC filings, ten quarterly 10-Qs, and multiple earnings call transcripts is that the market is materially undervaluing the Cash App franchise and meaningfully over-penalizing the bitcoin exposure and Afterpay integration drag. Cash App produced $6.3B in gross profit in 2025 on $15.7B in segment revenue, growing GP 21% YoY. That segment alone, valued at even 8x gross profit (conservative for a 59M-user neobank growing 20%+), is worth approximately $50B -- more than the entire current market capitalization of $37.3B. Square produced $3.9B in gross profit on $8.5B in revenue, growing 9%. Even at a mature 5x GP multiple, Square is worth approximately $20B. Sum-of-parts math delivers $70B+ before assigning any value to Proto, Bitkey, TIDAL, or the bitcoin investment itself. The stock trades at $37B.

The problem, and the reason for only "medium" conviction, is threefold. First, Dorsey's February 2026 decision to cut more than 40% of the workforce (from 10,205 to approximately 6,000) is either the most visionary restructuring in fintech history or a catastrophic amputation of engineering capability. We will not know the outcome for 12-18 months. Second, $1.2B in annual stock-based compensation against $1.3B in net income means GAAP EPS is almost entirely SBC-funded -- real economic earnings (net income minus SBC) are roughly $89M, or less than $0.15 per share. The buyback is funded by free cash flow (SBC is non-cash), but the dilution from share issuance is real. Third, transaction, loan, and consumer receivable losses surged 68% to $1.34B in FY2025, driven by Cash App Borrow's aggressive expansion (loan losses alone up 154% to $821M). If credit losses accelerate beyond management's underwriting models in a recession, Cash App's most profitable growth engine could become a liability.

## PART II -- COMPANY SNAPSHOT

**Corporate form and history.** Block, Inc. was incorporated in Delaware in June 2009 by Jack Dorsey and Jim McKelvey as Square, Inc. The company IPO'd on the NYSE on November 19, 2015 at $9 per share (split-adjusted). It was renamed Block, Inc. in December 2021 to reflect its expanding ecosystem beyond the original Square payment processing product. The ticker was changed from SQ to XYZ in 2025. The company operates under a distributed work model with no formal headquarters; the principal executive office address is 1955 Broadway, Suite 600, Oakland, CA 94612.

**Dual-class share structure.** Class A shares carry 1 vote per share and are publicly traded on the NYSE. Class B shares carry 10 votes per share and are held by insiders. As of February 20, 2026: 539.1M Class A + 60.0M Class B = approximately 599M total shares outstanding. Dorsey controls disproportionate voting power through Class B ownership. The weighted-average diluted share count for FY2025 was 622.8M. The company is also dual-listed on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) as CHESS Depositary Interests (CDIs), a legacy of the Afterpay acquisition.

**Business segments.** Block operates two reportable segments:

1. **Square (seller ecosystem):** Cohesive commerce platform enabling businesses to accept card payments, manage operations (inventory, employees, locations), access financial services (Square Loans, Square Checking), and build online stores. GPV of $250B in FY2025 (up 10% YoY). ~8,452M in revenue, $3,935M in gross profit (46.6% GP margin).

2. **Cash App (consumer ecosystem):** Financial products and services for consumers: P2P payments, Cash App Card (Visa debit), direct deposit, stock and bitcoin investing, tax filing, Cash App Borrow (short-term loans), and BNPL (Afterpay integration). 59M monthly transacting actives as of December 2025. ~$15,742M in revenue (including $8.5B bitcoin pass-through), $6,325M in gross profit (40.2% GP margin; ex-bitcoin approximately 83-85%).

**Non-segment businesses:** TIDAL (music streaming, acquired from Jay-Z), Proto (bitcoin mining hardware), Bitkey (self-custody bitcoin wallet), Spiral (open-source bitcoin development).

**Revenue categories (new FY2025 presentation).** Starting in FY2025, Block reclassified revenue into three categories that cut across segments:

Gross profit from bitcoin was approximately $419M on $8.5B revenue (4.9% margin). Excluding bitcoin, Block's core revenue grew 14% to $15.7B. Gross profit of $10.36B growing 17% YoY is the real business -- not headline revenue of $24.2B growing 0.3%.

## PART IV -- DIVIDEND DEEP DIVE

**Block has never paid a dividend. There is no dividend history, no streak, no payout ratio, no yield.**

The 2025 10-K states: "We do not expect to pay any dividends on our capital stock in the foreseeable future. Instead, we anticipate that all of our future earnings will be retained to support our operations and to finance the growth and development of our business. Any future determination to pay dividends will be at the discretion of our board of directors."

This section therefore analyzes the buyback program as the capital return mechanism and assesses the probability and potential timeline of a future dividend initiation.

**Buyback program parameters:**

- Total authorization: $9.0B (original $4.0B in May 2024, increased by $5.0B in November 2025)
- Amount deployed through December 31, 2025: $3.66B
- Remaining authorization: $5.34B as of December 31, 2025
- FY2025 activity: 36,502 thousand shares repurchased at approximately $63.85 weighted average (derived from $2,331M deployed / 36.5M shares), including excise tax
- Q4 2025 activity: 11,894 thousand shares at weighted average $67.47 (ranging from $63.84 to $76.98 per month)

Coverage of 1.04x in FY2025 is tight by design -- management is deploying essentially 100% of FCF into repurchases. Based on the Q4 2025 earnings call, Ahuja stated Block expects to continue share repurchases in 2026 while simultaneously investing in AI infrastructure, go-to-market, and talent upgrading.

**Buyback effectiveness score: 6/10.** The buyback is well-timed (average price of approximately $64 is below our base-case fair value of $75-90) but partially offset by SBC dilution. Net share reduction in FY2025: approximately 17.5M Class A shares (36.5M repurchased minus approximately 19M SBC-related issuance), representing 3.1% net dilution offset. This is acceptable but not exceptional compared to PayPal (which retired approximately 8% of float in 2025) or Apple (approximately 4% annually).

**Dividend probability assessment:**

- Probability of inaugural dividend within 24 months: 10%. Management's language is unambiguous: no dividend in the foreseeable future. A dividend would signal growth deceleration -- not a narrative Block wants while repositioning as a lean, AI-driven platform company.
- Probability within 5 years: 25-30%. If Cash App's growth decelerates to single digits, FCF exceeds $4B annually, and the buyback authorization is substantially exhausted, a dividend initiation becomes plausible. The earliest scenario would be FY2028-2029.
- Most likely first dividend: $0.25 per quarter ($1.00 annual), implying approximately 1.6% yield at $61.35 and approximately $600M annual payout (25% of projected FCF).

**Dividend sustainability narrative (hypothetical).** If Block initiated a $1.00 per share annual dividend today, it would cost approximately $600M against $2.42B in FCF (4.0x coverage). This is extremely safe coverage. The risk is not affordability but allocation discipline -- Dorsey could redirect capital to bitcoin, acquisitions, or moonshot projects at any time. The dual-class share structure means shareholders have no recourse.

## PART V -- BUSINESS QUALITY AND MOAT

**Moat type:** Two-sided network effects (Square sellers + Cash App consumers), switching costs (integrated commerce tools), data advantage (transaction-level underwriting for lending products).

**Moat trajectory:** Widening on Cash App, stable on Square.

Block's moat rests on the dual-sided ecosystem: Square serves sellers and Cash App serves consumers, with shared infrastructure enabling products that span both sides (e.g., Afterpay, Cash App Pay at Square merchants, "neighborhoods" connecting local sellers with Cash App users). No competitor replicates this architecture. PayPal tried (eBay + PayPal) and divested. Stripe, Toast, Shopify, and Adyen are seller-only. Venmo, Chime, and Apple Pay are consumer-only.

**Pricing power evidence:** Square implemented new tiered pricing and packaging in FY2025, creating three bundles combining software and payments. The fact that GPV grew 10% and new volume added grew 17% during a pricing transition suggests pricing power is intact. Cash App charges fees on Instant Deposit, ATM withdrawals, bitcoin transactions, and Borrow loans -- all of which saw revenue growth with no disclosed volume-to-fee degradation.

**Network effects strength:** Cash App's 59M monthly transacting actives generate direct network effects: every new user makes the P2P network more valuable, which drives Cash App Card adoption, which drives interchange revenue, which funds marketing to acquire more users. Primary banking actives (users with direct deposit set up) grew 22% YoY to 9.3M monthly actives in December 2025 and generate nearly 10x the gross profit of peer-to-peer-only actives, per the Q4 2025 earnings call.

**Switching costs in Square:** Sellers using Square's integrated hardware (Square Register, Square Terminal, Square Handheld), point-of-sale software, inventory management, employee scheduling, payroll, and lending products face meaningful switching costs. Food and beverage is the strongest vertical, where Square's multi-device setup (KDS, customer displays, handheld ordering) creates deep integration.

**Return on invested capital trend:**

| Year | ROIC (approximation) | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| 2020 | 1.5% | Pre-Afterpay, low profitability |
| 2021 | 3.1% | Growing but investment mode |
| 2022 | Negative | Afterpay goodwill load, operating loss |
| 2023 | 2.7% | Profitability pivot began |
| 2024 | 4.1% | FCF positive, improved margins |
| 2025 | 6.8% | $1.71B OpInc on ~$25B invested capital |

ROIC is improving but remains well below the cost of capital for a high-beta fintech stock (estimated WACC 10-12%). This is the fundamental issue: Block's moat is real but not yet generating returns commensurate with the risk. The FY2026 restructuring is designed to close this gap.

## PART VI -- CAPITAL ALLOCATION

Block's capital allocation priorities in FY2025, ranked by dollar amount:

1. **Share buybacks: $2,331M (96% of FCF).** The dominant priority. Management is buying back shares at an average price approximately 25% below our base-case fair value, which is optimal capital allocation.

2. **Loan portfolio investment: $3,549M net outflow in investing activities.** Beginning Q2 2025, Cash App Borrow loans are retained on balance sheet (held for investment) rather than sold to bank partners. This shifted cash flows from operating to investing activities and inflated investing cash outflows. The loans generate interest income and are the core growth engine.

3. **CapEx: $155M (6% of FCF).** Minimal and consistent ($151-155M annually for three years). Block is a software company with negligible maintenance capex requirements.

4. **Debt management: $1,001M 2025 Convertible Note settlement; $2,200M new senior note issuance.** Block refinanced the March 2025 convertible maturity and extended duration with 2030 and 2033 senior notes. Credit ratings: BBB- (Fitch, investment grade), Ba1 (Moody's), BB+ (S&P). One notch from full investment-grade across all three agencies.

5. **Bitcoin investment: $41M (398 BTC purchased in FY2025).** Modest relative to the balance sheet. Block holds 8,883 BTC with a fair value of $777.5M (cost basis approximately $759M). No bitcoin was sold in FY2024 or FY2025. The $55.9M remeasurement loss in FY2025 (versus a $420.9M gain in FY2024) creates P&L noise but does not affect cash flow.

6. **M&A: Immaterial in FY2025.** No major acquisitions. The Afterpay integration is ongoing.

7. **Dividends: $0.** Zero.

**Capital allocation grade: B.** The buyback is well-executed and well-timed. The Afterpay acquisition ($29B in 2021 stock, generating goodwill of $11.8B with no impairment yet despite the BNPL downturn) was a C- decision. Non-core ventures (TIDAL: impaired $60.3M in Q4 2024; Proto: pre-revenue mining hardware; Bitkey: negligible adoption) collectively represent a D in allocation discipline. The recent pivot to FCF-funded buybacks has improved the composite grade from C to B over the past 18 months.

**CEO: Jack Dorsey.** Title: "Block Head and Chairperson" (per 10-K signature page). Co-founder. Age 49. Has served as CEO since inception, except for a brief period in 2020-2021 when Alyssa Henry held interim operational authority. Also co-founded Twitter (now X). **CFO/COO: Amrita Ahuja.** Joined January 2019 from Goldman Sachs (Managing Director). Was elevated to dual CFO/COO role. Signed the FY2025 10-K as "Chief Financial Officer & Chief Operating Officer (Principal Financial Officer)." She has been the operational anchor during Dorsey's increasingly public bitcoin advocacy. Her Q4 2025 earnings call commentary was notable for specificity: quarterly AOI margin cadence, Borrow cohort loss rates trending below targets as of mid-February, $3.2B AOI guidance for FY2026 (54% growth), and $3.66 adjusted diluted EPS guidance.

**Other key executives:** Owen Jennings (business lead), Nick Molnar (Sales and Marketing Lead, Afterpay co-founder), Ajmere Dale (Chief Accounting Officer).

## PART X -- RISKS AND STRESS TESTS

**Risk 1: The 40% workforce cut breaks product velocity.**

Reducing from 10,205 to approximately 6,000 employees is not optimization -- it is amputation. If engineering output suffers, Cash App loses its edge against Venmo, Chime, Apple Pay, and dozens of neobank challengers. Dorsey's Q4 2025 call argued that "intelligence tools have changed what it means to build and run a company" and cited "greater than 40% increase in production code shipped per engineer since September." This claim needs verification over multiple quarters. The risk is asymmetric: if the cut works, margins improve 5-7 percentage points; if it fails, Block permanently impairs its competitive position and a recovery would take years.

Probability: 30%. Severity: High. Mitigant: Management has been functionalizing the organization (removing duplication between Square and Cash App teams) for 18+ months. AI coding tools are real and measurable in developer productivity metrics. The FY2026 AOI guidance of $3.2B already embeds the expected savings.

**Risk 2: Cash App Borrow credit deterioration in a recession.**

Loan losses surged 154% to $821M in FY2025, driven by volume growth. Cash App Borrow origination volume grew more than 3x YoY in Q4 2025. Management claims loss rates are stable ("all 2026 cohorts are trending below our risk loss targets" per Ahuja on the Q4 call, as of mid-February). But the absolute dollar amounts are alarming: total transaction, loan, and consumer receivable losses of $1.34B represent 12.9% of total gross profit, up from 8.9% in FY2024 and 9.0% in FY2023.

Estimated sensitivity: every 100 basis point increase in Borrow loss rate on the growing portfolio costs approximately $80-100M annually. In a moderate recession scenario (unemployment rising from 4% to 6%), Borrow loss rates could increase 300-500 bps, implying $240-500M in incremental losses -- enough to reduce FY2026 FCF by 8-17%.

Probability: 25% (recession within 18 months). Severity: Medium-high. Mitigant: Borrow loans are short-term (average repayment under 4 weeks), allowing rapid portfolio de-risking. Originating through Square Financial Services (started Q2 2025) improves unit economics by eliminating bank partner fees.

**Risk 3: SBC dilution permanently offsets buyback accretion.**

SBC of $1.215B in FY2025 created approximately 19M new shares (at average $64 per share). The buyback retired 36.5M shares at a cost of $2.33B. Net reduction: 17.5M shares. If Block continues spending $2.3B annually on buybacks but SBC does not decline proportionally with the workforce reduction, the net share reduction may remain in the 2-3% range -- adequate but not transformative.

The critical question: does the 40% headcount reduction also reduce SBC proportionally? If SBC falls from $1.2B to $700-800M (proportional to headcount), the buyback's net accretion doubles. If SBC remains elevated due to retention packages for remaining AI-skilled employees, the dilution problem persists.

Probability: 50% (SBC remains elevated). Severity: Medium. Mitigant: FY2025 SBC was already down $60M from FY2024 ($1.215B vs $1.273B). The restructuring SBC acceleration ($12.3M in FY2025) is a one-time item.

**Risk 4: Jack Dorsey's ideological priorities destroy shareholder value.**

Dorsey renamed the company twice (Block in 2021, ticker XYZ in 2025) for ideological reasons. Non-core projects -- TIDAL (impaired $60.3M), Proto (pre-profit mining hardware), Bitkey (negligible adoption) -- divert capital and management attention. The $778M bitcoin position creates P&L volatility ($420.9M gain in FY2024, $55.9M loss in FY2025). The dual-class structure prevents shareholders from constraining these decisions.

Probability: 20% (material value destruction from bitcoin/non-core ventures). Severity: Medium. Mitigant: The $9B buyback authorization is the most shareholder-friendly action in Block's history. Proto shipped its first units in Q4 2025 with gross profit scaling; it may eventually justify the investment. Bitcoin is a free call option on BTC if bullish.

**Risk 5: $11.8B goodwill impairment.**

Afterpay was acquired for $29B in all-stock consideration in January 2022. BNPL economics have deteriorated (higher rates, increased regulation, competition from Apple, Klarna). Goodwill has not been impaired. If the BNPL market does not recover or if regulatory pressure increases globally, a multi-billion-dollar impairment charge is possible. This would be non-cash but would damage investor confidence and could trigger debt covenant concerns.

Probability: 15% within 24 months. Severity: High (optics). Mitigant: BNPL consumer receivable losses increased only 15% YoY in FY2025 while volumes grew, indicating stable loss rates. Afterpay is deeply integrated into Cash App (Afterpay Post-Purchase contributed $88M incremental commerce enablement revenue in FY2025). The franchise value may exceed book value even if standalone BNPL is impaired.
