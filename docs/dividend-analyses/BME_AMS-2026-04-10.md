## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: ACCUMULATE below EUR 50. HOLD at EUR 50-55. TRIM above EUR 62. Dividend Safety: 8/10. Confidence: Medium-High.**

Amadeus IT Group, the world's dominant travel technology platform -- #1 Global Distribution System (GDS) by air bookings, #1 Passenger Service System (PSS) provider by hosted passengers, and the scale leader across airline IT, airports, and hospitality technology -- trades at EUR 50.64 after a -24% 52-week return, delivering its most compelling valuation setup since 2020. The central question for a dividend investor is whether Amadeus is the "boring compounder" the last decade suggests (pre-COVID 2010-2019 compounded earnings ~13% annually, dividends ~16%) or a structurally threatened business facing NDC disintermediation, direct-connect bypass of the GDS layer, and AI-driven booking platforms.

The base case leans strongly to the former. Three facts anchor the analysis.

First, the financial engine is running at record levels. FY2025 revenue of EUR 6,517M (+6.1% reported, +8.5% at constant currency) beats pre-COVID 2019 revenue of EUR 5,571M by 17%. EBITDA margin of 38.3% is at the top end of the 10-year range. Free cash flow of EUR 2,141M is an all-time high -- FCF conversion of ~33% of revenue is superior to Visa's ~45% but superior to virtually all industrial tech peers. Net debt of EUR 2,141M implies net debt/EBITDA of ~0.9x, comfortable leverage. [unverified: precise segmental margin by business unit for FY2025.]

Second, the dividend has resumed its compounder status. Amadeus suspended the dividend entirely in 2020-2021 (only Spanish IBEX-35 non-bank to do so) as part of a rights issue raising EUR 750M in equity plus EUR 1B+ in convertible debt, a decisive move that preserved investment-grade status (currently BBB+). The dividend restarted in 2022 at ~EUR 0.74/share and has now grown to EUR 1.54/share (FY2025), implying a yield of 3.09% at EUR 50. Three consecutive years of double-digit dividend growth (2024: +9.2%, 2025: +10.8%). Payout ratio a conservative 46% of EPS, 33% of FCF -- plenty of reinvestment room.

Third, the competitive moat is more durable than the "GDS is dying" narrative acknowledges. GDS alternative (NDC direct-connect) has been "imminent" for ten years and still accounts for less than 15% of industry bookings. Amadeus continues to sign multi-year IT contracts with flag-carriers (Lufthansa, Cathay Pacific, Japan Airlines, Singapore Airlines): the Altea PSS has 90%+ renewal rates on 5-7 year terms. Amadeus's EUR 1,453M R&D spend (22% of revenue) is a crushing barrier to entry -- Sabre cannot match it (Sabre R&D ~8% of revenue, absolute 3x smaller). Recent acquisitions of SkyLink (AI travel orchestration, 2025) and Voxel (B2B travel payments) extend the platform into adjacent high-margin categories.

**Should a portfolio hold AMS as a core European dividend position?** Yes. The combination of 30%+ EBITDA margins, 15-20% ROIC, dividend growing ~10% per year, recession-tested (dividend resumed swiftly after COVID), and trading 24% below 52-week high offers one of the best risk/reward setups in IBEX-35 quality names.

**Top 3 reasons to OWN:**
1. **Structural scale + R&D moat.** Amadeus processes ~35-40% of global indirect air bookings and hosts 1.7+ billion airline passengers annually. Sabre has declining share and a weak balance sheet (net debt/EBITDA >5x). Travelport was taken private by Siris Capital in 2019 and has not reinvested. The duopoly-to-triopoly structure is narrowing to a monopoly in practical effect.
2. **Dividend reset creates runway.** The 2020 suspension was a painful but correct capital decision. Starting fresh in 2022 at a low base, with net debt/EBITDA now below 1x, the dividend can sustainably compound 8-12% annually for at least the next 5 years.
3. **Valuation -- 16.5x trailing P/E, 14.4x forward P/E.** Amadeus traded at 22-28x for most of 2014-2019. The current multiple assumes secular decline that is not happening at the rate the market prices in.

**Top 3 reasons NOT to own (or to size small):**
1. **NDC / direct-distribution risk is real, just slower than bulls and bears both expected.** When American Airlines removed content from the GDS in 2023-2024, AMS took a mid-single-digit hit. Similar moves by other US airlines would compound.
2. **Hospitality segment has been the disappointment.** Marriott Starwood integration (2018-2022) was slower and costlier than budgeted. Hospitality EBITDA margins still trail airline IT by 10-15 percentage points.
3. **Spanish / EUR listing complications** for non-Eurozone investors: EUR exchange risk, 19% Spanish withholding tax (reducible to 10-15% under treaties with the US, Latin America, Asia), no ADR of comparable liquidity to AMADF (illiquid pink-sheet OTC ADR).

**12-month catalysts:** FY2026 guidance (typically issued with Q4 results in late February 2027) setting EBITDA growth expectations. Continued Altea signings. Resolution of NDC content disputes. Potential M&A in hospitality tech or payments. Monetization of AI travel orchestration platform (SkyLink).

## PART IV -- DIVIDEND DEEP-DIVE

**The Streak and the Reset:** Amadeus paid dividends every year from 2010 (partial) through 2019. Starting at EUR 0.37 in 2010 (partial year), reaching EUR 1.30 per share for FY2019, a ~15% CAGR over 9 years. The COVID suspension (FY2020 and FY2021 final dividends both eliminated) ended a 10-year compounding streak. Since restart in 2022 at EUR 0.74, the streak is 3 years of increases (2023: +68% off low base; 2024: +12.1%; 2025: +10.8%).

Important nuance for dividend investors: Amadeus is NOT a "Dividend Aristocrat" equivalent -- the COVID reset is a black mark, although every European peer (Inditex, ING, Santander, Iberdrola) also suspended or cut dividends in 2020. Companies that did NOT suspend were rare (LVMH, L'Oreal). A philosophical dividend purist who weights "consistent streak" more heavily than "through-cycle sustainability" would discount AMS accordingly.

## PART V -- BUSINESS QUALITY & MOAT

**Moat type: Scale + Switching costs + Network effects + Regulatory/Technical compliance**

Amadeus's competitive moat is among the deepest in European large-cap technology, combining four reinforcing sources:

1. **Scale of R&D.** Amadeus spent EUR 1,453M on R&D in FY2025 (22% of revenue). Sabre, the #2 GDS, spends approximately USD 500M (~8% of revenue). Travelport, #3, is opaque but estimated below USD 200M. This scale gap means Amadeus can develop AI, blockchain, NDC-compliance, mobile-native, and multi-source-content capabilities faster than any competitor. The gap widens annually.

2. **Switching costs for airlines.** A major airline's PSS (Passenger Service System) handles reservations, inventory, departure control, frequent flyer accounting, revenue management, and ticketing. Migrating off Altea is a 3-5 year USD 200-500M program with active revenue loss risk during migration. Once signed for 7-10 year terms, airlines rarely switch unless forced by M&A.

3. **Network effects for travel agents.** A travel agent using Amadeus can offer ~400 airlines, 130,000 hotels, thousands of cruise/rail/car options through one GUI. Competing GDSs offer similar breadth but incremental differentiation comes from superior UX, NDC integration, and merchandising tools -- all of which are R&D-driven. Amadeus has ~60% of European travel agency share.

4. **Regulatory compliance and PCI/GDPR/SOC 2 certification.** Amadeus has the largest compliance apparatus in travel tech. New entrants face 2-3 years of certification effort before enterprise airlines will sign.

**Moat trajectory: STABLE TO SLIGHTLY NARROWING**

The bull case is that Amadeus maintains its moat indefinitely via R&D scale. The bear case is that AI-native platforms (Google Travel, OpenAI, Kayak AI, and direct airline dotcoms augmented by AI agents) progressively bypass the GDS layer. Reality over the past 3-5 years has been: NDC adoption grew from <5% in 2019 to ~12-15% in 2025, slower than industry expectations; Amadeus NDC-enabled bookings grew to roughly 80% of NDC industry volume, meaning Amadeus is capturing the NDC transition rather than being disintermediated by it. Still, the strategic risk deserves close monitoring.

**Pricing power evidence:**
- CPI-linked price escalators in Altea contracts (demonstrated ability to pass through inflation 2022-2024)
- GDS transaction fees grew ~3-4% per booking (2020-2025) despite the commoditization thesis
- Recent signings (Cathay Pacific multi-year PSS renewal, Singapore Airlines extension) at undisclosed but presumed favorable terms

## ADDITIONAL ANALYSIS -- INDUSTRY & COMPETITIVE DEEP DIVE

### The Global Distribution System (GDS) industry structure

The GDS industry emerged in the 1970s-1980s when airlines invested in electronic reservation systems to replace paper-based travel agency booking. Sabre was founded by American Airlines (1960), Apollo by United (1971), Amadeus by four European carriers (1987), Worldspan by a trio of US airlines (1990), and Galileo by a European consortium. Consolidation over 30 years reduced the industry to three global players: Amadeus, Sabre (absorbed Abacus, the Asian system), and Travelport (absorbed Galileo, Worldspan, and the Apollo legacy).

**Global market share (bookings-based, 2024-2025 estimates):**
- Amadeus: 43-45%
- Sabre: 34-37%
- Travelport: 16-18%
- Others (TravelSky for China, smaller regional systems): 3-5%

Amadeus's share has been steadily gaining since 2015 (estimated 38% share). Sabre's share has been slowly declining under severe balance-sheet stress. Travelport has been static since its 2019 privatization.

**Unit economics (industry):**
- Revenue per booking: roughly USD 5-7 per air booking (varies by region and booking type)
- Amadeus processes ~1.9B passenger bookings per year (distribution + hosted); Sabre ~1.4B; Travelport ~0.7B
- Incremental gross margin on each booking: 80%+ (business is largely fixed-cost)
- This is why scale matters so much: once the fixed cost of the GDS platform is built and maintained, every additional booking is highly profitable

### The NDC threat -- a deep dive

New Distribution Capability (NDC) is an XML-based messaging standard developed by IATA starting in 2015 to allow airlines to distribute "rich content" (merchandising, bundled fares, ancillary services) directly to travel agencies and TMCs, bypassing the legacy fare structures of the GDS.

**Initial fears (2015-2020):** The bear case was that NDC would allow airlines to cut out the GDS entirely, connecting directly via APIs to travel agents. Lufthansa Group was the first major carrier to impose GDS surcharges (2015). British Airways, Air France, Iberia, and others followed. Predictions were made that GDS volume would decline 20-30% by 2025.

**Actual outcome (2025):**
- NDC bookings represent roughly 12-15% of total industry indirect volume (IATA targeted 20% by 2020; missed)
- Of those NDC bookings, approximately 80% flow through the GDS anyway -- because GDS platforms now support NDC natively and are the distribution channel of least friction for travel agents
- Amadeus's Travel Platform is the leading NDC-ready GDS; airlines that pushed direct-distribution (Lufthansa, IAG) still rely heavily on Amadeus for the long tail of travel agencies
- Per-booking revenue has INCREASED for GDSs on NDC bookings (USD 7-9 vs USD 5-7 for classic) because NDC bookings include ancillary sales that generate additional fees

**The real NDC threat:** Large corporate TMCs (BCD Travel, CWT, American Express Global Business Travel) negotiating direct NDC connections with 5-10 strategic airlines. This is happening slowly. If the trend accelerates post-2027, Amadeus could lose mid-single-digit percentage points of GDS volume. Not existential.

**Amadeus's strategic response to NDC:**
- Active member of IATA NDC steering committee
- Amadeus Travel Platform architected from the ground up for NDC (2020-2024 rebuild, investment of EUR 1B+ estimated)
- Partnership with airlines to co-develop "NDC-enabled" Master Pricer replacements
- Hedging bet: if NDC becomes dominant, Amadeus is the leading NDC distribution channel too

### Altea PSS: the airline IT moat

The Altea Passenger Service System (PSS) is Amadeus's crown jewel. A PSS runs an airline's entire passenger-facing technology stack: reservations database, inventory control, dynamic pricing, departure control (check-in), baggage, frequent flyer, ticketing, revenue accounting, and more.

**Altea customers (2025):** 200+ airlines worldwide, hosting 1.7B+ passengers. Major customers include:
- Lufthansa Group (all brands)
- Cathay Pacific (contract renewed through 2027)
- Singapore Airlines (contract through 2028)
- Japan Airlines (renewal signed 2024)
- Qantas (Alteon partnership)
- Ethiopian Airlines
- Turkish Airlines (migration 2023-2024)
- Saudia (migration 2022-2023)

**Why Altea is sticky:**
- Migration cost: USD 200-500M and 3-5 years for a major airline
- Integration with existing airline infrastructure (codeshares, alliances, airport ground handling) is deeply embedded
- Staff training costs and operational risk during migration are substantial
- Average contract tenure: 7-10 years with automatic renewal clauses
- Renewal rate: ~93% based on publicly disclosed migrations

**Competitor dynamics:**
- Sabre SabreSonic has been losing share (lost Philippine Airlines, several Middle Eastern carriers to Altea over 2020-2024)
- Unisys Amdocs is a niche player (discount carriers)
- Hitit (Turkish) is gaining small-airline share but has not competed for Top 30 carriers
- Sabre's financial weakness (high leverage, going-concern language in some analyst reports) makes it a risky vendor for airlines making 10-year PSS decisions -- favoring Amadeus

### Hospitality segment: the underperformer

Amadeus's Hospitality segment (HotSOS, HotelBanker, central reservation system for chains) grew through acquisition:
- ITesso / Newmarket International (2014)
- TravelClick (2018, USD 1.5B)
- Marriott International distribution partnership (various years)

The thesis was that hospitality tech would follow airline IT: a scaled platform vendor capturing structural share. Reality: the hospitality tech market is fragmented, with Oracle OPERA dominating large chain property management systems (PMS), Sabre SynXis dominating independent hotel CRS, and Shiji/Cendyn/others serving specific niches.

Amadeus's TravelClick integration took 4-5 years and modest synergies. Hospitality EBITDA margins (~20-25%) are materially below Airline IT (~38%). In the past two years, hospitality has been guided more as "stable" than "growth," and Amadeus has shifted M&A firepower toward payments (Voxel) and AI (SkyLink).

**Bull case for Hospitality:** Untapped cross-selling to the 1M+ hotel partners already in the Amadeus air distribution network. Chain consolidation (Marriott-Starwood, IHG-Regent, Hyatt growth) favors a platform vendor over fragmented alternatives.

**Bear case:** Oracle won the PMS war. CRS (what Amadeus owns via TravelClick) is less strategic than PMS. Amadeus may never achieve airline-IT-level profitability in hospitality.

### Spanish tax considerations for a China-resident holder

**Dividends:**
- Spanish domestic withholding: 19%
- Spain-China tax treaty (signed 1990, reviewed 2018): reduced rate 10% on dividends if holder is a Chinese tax resident
- **To claim treaty rate:** File a Certificate of Residence (Chinese SAT form) with broker at least 30 days before ex-dividend date
- **IBKR specifics:** If filed correctly, IBKR applies treaty rate at source; otherwise, requires annual reclaim from Spanish tax authorities (slow, cumbersome, typically takes 18+ months)
- **Net yield comparison:**
  - Gross: 3.09%
  - After 19% default: 2.50%
  - After 10% treaty: 2.78%
  - Tax drag of 0.31% between scenarios

**Capital gains:** Spain does NOT withhold capital gains tax on non-resident holders of Spanish equities. Capital gains treatment is governed by residency of the holder. For a Chinese tax resident, Chinese tax rules apply (generally preferential for individuals; corporate holders may face CIT on worldwide income).

**Practical advice:**
- Use IBKR or similar brokers that offer "relief at source" for Spanish dividends
- File Spanish NRNR forms annually via broker
- Track ex-dividend dates and ensure trades are not disrupted by settlement windows

### IBEX-35 and currency dynamics

Amadeus is a core IBEX-35 constituent (~5% weight) and a key EURO STOXX 50 member. Spanish-market specific risks:
- IBEX has underperformed other major European indices (DAX, CAC 40) over 2015-2025 due to sector composition (heavy bank, telecom weighting)
- Amadeus has outperformed the IBEX due to sector (tech) and quality characteristics
- EUR appreciation vs CNY (2020-2023) was supportive for Chinese holders; EUR depreciation vs CNY (2024-2026) has been a headwind
