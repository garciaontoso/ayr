## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: BUY below EUR 26. ACCUMULATE at EUR 25.87. HOLD above EUR 30. -- Dividend Safety: 6/10 -- Confidence: Medium**

FDJ United -- the entity formerly known as La Francaise des Jeux plus the October-2024 fully-consolidated Kindred Group -- is the most mispriced regulated quasi-monopoly in European equities. The stock trades at EUR 25.87 on roughly 183M shares, giving a market cap near EUR 4.74B. Adding ~EUR 2.3B of net debt assumed with the Kindred acquisition yields an enterprise value around EUR 7.0B [approx] against FY2025 EBITDA of EUR 943M. That is 7.4x EV/EBITDA for a business whose core French lottery segment enjoys a legislated exclusive 25-year license (valid through 2044, renewable), generates 80%+ cash conversion, and has grown revenue at double-digit CAGR since the 2019 IPO. The dislocation is the Kindred integration, a full-year 2025 consolidation effect that brought EUR 199.1M of "other unusual items" that compressed reported net income from EUR 398.8M in FY2024 to EUR 175.9M in FY2025 -- a 55.9% collapse that is largely one-off goodwill write-downs and integration charges rather than operating deterioration.

At EUR 25.87, the dividend yield computes to approximately 8.3% on the FY2025 proposed dividend of EUR 2.10 per share. This is the highest yield FDJ has ever offered and sits in the 99th percentile of the stock's post-IPO range. It is elevated for two overlapping reasons: (i) the share price has been punished by the market during the Kindred integration year (stock down ~14% over the past 12 months, range EUR 22.12 - EUR 34.04), and (ii) the EUR 2.10 DPS itself reflects management's deliberate dividend step-up rather than a passive distribution of declining earnings. The FY2025 payout ratio on reported EPS of EUR 0.95 exceeds 200% -- optically alarming. On **FY2025 operating profit-adjusted EPS [approx EUR 2.20-2.40]**, the payout is 85-95%, at the upper bound of sustainability but defensible for a regulated monopoly with minimal reinvestment drag in the legacy lottery segment.

The business itself is transforming. Pre-Kindred FDJ was a clean, simple, somewhat boring story: France's 90-year national lottery operator, privatized November 2019 at EUR 19.90 per share by the Macron government in France's largest IPO of the era, running 30,000+ physical retail points (tabacs, news agents) plus a rapidly-growing digital channel, printing cash with a 25-year exclusive constitutional license. Pre-Kindred FDJ was an infrastructure-quality business with a gaming skin. The EUR 2.6B all-cash Kindred acquisition, announced January 2024 and completed October 2024 after 90%+ tender acceptance, doubled the balance sheet, quintupled net debt, and gave FDJ a pan-European online betting and gaming platform (Unibet, 32Red, Stan James, Maria Casino, Bingo.com) across UK, Nordics, Netherlands, Belgium, Australia and other regulated markets.

The market is pricing Kindred close to zero. Strip out pre-Kindred FDJ 2023 EBITDA of roughly EUR 500M at 13x EV/EBITDA and you arrive at EV of EUR 6.5B for the legacy business alone -- which is approximately the current total enterprise value. The implicit market valuation of Kindred is therefore EUR 0-500M against a EUR 2.6B purchase price. This is an extreme form of Mr. Market pessimism that characteristically reverses within 24-36 months once synergies demonstrate delivery.

**Top 3 reasons to own (specific):**

1. **Constitutional monopoly with 92-year operating history and a 25-year exclusive license.** FDJ has operated France's national lottery since 1933 (originally Loterie Nationale). The 2019 privatization law embedded the exclusive lottery license for 25 years -- through 2044 -- with strong renewal presumption baked into the privatization framework. No competitor can legally offer draw lottery, instant scratch, or Loto products in France. This is not a competitive moat. It is a legal fortress underpinning approximately 90% of pre-Kindred revenue.

2. **Kindred acquisition transforms the growth profile and re-rates the combined entity.** Pre-Kindred, FDJ was a 4-5% organic grower locked inside France. Post-Kindred, it owns Unibet, 32Red, and other brands across UK, Nordics, Netherlands, Belgium, Australia -- diversifying by geography (~35-40% of revenue from outside France), by product (sports betting + iGaming alongside lottery), and by customer demographic (Kindred skews younger digital-native). The EUR 80M cost synergy target by 2027 is heavily back-loaded to 2026-2027 and is **not yet in the run-rate**, meaning the FY2025 financial picture shows the cost of integration without the upside of synergies.

3. **Valuation is at trough multiples with a high-yield carry.** At 27x reported FY2025 P/E (depressed by one-off charges) but approximately 10-11x FY2027E normalized EPS [approx EUR 2.35-2.60], with 8.3% dividend yield supported by EUR 568M of FCF, FDJU offers an unusually attractive package: regulated monopoly economics + high-single-digit yield + optionality on Kindred synergy delivery + defensive cash flow profile that weathered COVID without a dividend cut.

**Top 3 reasons not to own:**

1. **Regulatory risk is omnidirectional and worsening.** FDJ operates under the French ANJ regulator (Autorite Nationale des Jeux) and must comply with strict responsible gaming requirements. Any government change -- and France has experienced deep political instability in 2024-2025 -- could impose higher gaming taxes, advertising restrictions, or stake limits. The Kindred markets each carry their own regulatory volatility: the UK Gambling Act review remains a permanent overhang, the Netherlands raised online gambling tax to 37.8% effective October 2025, and Sweden continues tightening. Every regulatory change is asymmetric -- unlikely to reduce burdens, likely to increase them.

2. **Integration execution risk is real and quantifiable.** Kindred was a EUR 2.6B all-cash acquisition funded with debt. The pro forma entity carries net debt/EBITDA above 2.0x (from net-cash pre-deal) with significant goodwill recognized. FY2025 other unusual items of EUR 199.1M indicate integration charges, restructuring, and possibly goodwill impairment. If synergies underdeliver or Kindred's revenue contracts in competitive UK/Nordic markets, the deleveraging timeline slips and the dividend becomes vulnerable. Management has not formally articulated revenue synergies; cost synergies of EUR 80M are the stated target.

3. **French political risk and state shareholder dynamics.** The French state retains approximately 20% of the equity and one board seat, a legacy of the 2019 privatization. Government priorities -- responsible gaming, tax revenue, consumer protection -- can conflict with shareholder returns. France's 2024-2025 political instability (three prime ministers in 18 months, contested budget framework) amplifies uncertainty about the gaming tax regime. The state shareholder is simultaneously a stabilizer (cannot easily exit) and a brake on maximalist capital return.

**Next 12 months catalysts:**

- **EUR 80M cost synergy ramp** -- market needs quarterly evidence of run-rate delivery; currently tracking behind schedule per anecdotal channel checks.

**One-sentence thesis:** FDJU at EUR 25.87 offers a legal-monopoly cash-cow plus post-Kindred European betting/iGaming optionality at an implied valuation for Kindred near zero, paying 8.3% yield while you wait for the synergy evidence that should close the EUR 35-45 fair-value gap over 18-24 months.

## PART II -- COMPANY SNAPSHOT

FDJ United (corporate rebrand announced Q4 2024 post-Kindred close; formerly "La Francaise des Jeux SA" / "FDJ SA") operates France's and Ireland's national lottery systems and a portfolio of pan-European online gambling brands following the Kindred acquisition. The company is headquartered in Boulogne-Billancourt (Paris suburb), trades on Euronext Paris under ticker FDJU (historical ticker FDJ.PA), and is a constituent of the CAC Mid 60 index. Founded in 1933 as Loterie Nationale, the company operated as a state-owned entity for 86 years before the November 2019 partial privatization. Post-IPO, French state ownership declined from 72% to approximately 20%, with the remainder distributed across retail investors (over 500,000 individual subscribers at IPO), institutional investors, and a small employee shareholding.

The business operates four reported segments post-Kindred integration:

1. **Lottery & Sports Betting (France)** -- legacy FDJ core. Exclusive draw games (Loto, EuroMillions, Mission Patrimoine, Cash, Amigo), instant scratch cards (Millionnaire, Astro, Cash), digital lottery (FDJ.fr platform). Sports betting via the Parions Sport brand, France's leading regulated sports betting operator. Approximately 55-60% of consolidated post-Kindred revenue [approx].

2. **Online Betting and Gaming** -- the acquired Kindred Group portfolio. Operates nine brands including Unibet (flagship, present across 10+ European markets), 32Red (UK iGaming), Stan James (UK sports), Maria Casino (Nordics), Bingo.com, and others. Serves markets in UK, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Netherlands, Belgium, France, Australia, Romania, and other regulated jurisdictions. Approximately 30-35% of post-Kindred revenue [approx].

3. **International Lottery** -- primarily Premier Lotteries Ireland DAC (acquired November 2023), operator of Ireland's national lottery. Also includes minor international lottery services contracts. Approximately 5-8% of revenue [approx].

4. **Payment and Services** -- B2B payments infrastructure, retailer services, and adjacent non-gambling services leveraging the 30,800+ retail point-of-sale network. Approximately 3-5% of revenue [approx].

## PART III -- HISTORICAL REVIEW: THE STRATEGIC ARC

**Phase 1 -- State-Owned Monopoly (1933-2019).** Loterie Nationale, FDJ's predecessor, was founded in 1933 during the Third Republic, operating as France's exclusive lottery operator under successive government mandates. The business was stable, profitable, and chronically underinvested in technology. Revenue grew steadily with population and GDP. The French state extracted value through gaming taxes (approximately 55-65% of gross gaming revenue flows to public treasuries under French law) and dividend distributions. Major product launches included Loto (1976, still France's flagship draw game), Rapido (1998), and Millionnaire scratch cards (1989). The physical retail network expanded from 10,000 points in 1980 to 30,000+ by 2015. Technology investment was minimal -- online lottery did not launch meaningfully until 2011-2012.

**Phase 2 -- IPO and Privatization (2019-2023).** The Macron government privatized FDJ in November 2019 at EUR 19.90 per share in France's largest IPO in nearly two decades. The offering was oversubscribed 5x by retail investors; 500,000+ individual investors participated with median ticket sizes of EUR 1,500-2,500. The IPO raised EUR 1.9B for the French state while granting FDJ a 25-year exclusive lottery license (running to 2044, with strong renewal presumption embedded in the privatization framework). Post-IPO, management under CEO Stephane Pallez accelerated digital transformation: online lottery sales grew from ~10% of French lottery revenue (2019) to over 22% (2023), the Parions Sport sports betting brand expanded aggressively, and the company launched a comprehensive responsible gaming technology platform with mandatory player limits. The period included several bolt-on acquisitions: Sporting Group (UK sports betting data, 2019), Aleda (payment services, 2020), and Bimedia (retail services technology, 2020). Through COVID (which briefly shuttered physical retail in March-May 2020), the digital channel proved resilient. Revenue dipped only 1.8% in 2020 and snapped back 17.5% in 2021. Earnings grew from EUR 133M (2019) to EUR 425M (2023), a 3.2x expansion over four years.

**Phase 3 -- International Expansion (2023-2024).** In November 2023 FDJ acquired Premier Lotteries Ireland DAC for approximately EUR 350M [approx], becoming Ireland's national lottery operator. The deal was explicitly a proof-of-concept for FDJ's "international lottery operator" ambitions. Management signaled interest in additional European lottery tenders, positioning FDJ as a consolidator in the fragmented European regulated-gaming landscape. Revenue crossed EUR 2.6B in 2023 with operating margin above 19%.

**Phase 4 -- Kindred Acquisition and Transformation (2024-present).** The Kindred Group deal, announced January 2024 at EUR 2.6B (30 SEK per Kindred share), was the most aggressive strategic move in FDJ's history. Kindred (formerly Unibet Group) was a Malta-incorporated, Stockholm-listed online gambling operator with EUR 1.3B revenue across 10+ regulated European markets and ~30M registered customer accounts. The tender offer succeeded with over 90% shareholder acceptance by October 2024, leading to Kindred's full consolidation and delisting from the Stockholm Stock Exchange. The deal rationale: (i) geographic diversification beyond France, (ii) deep online sports betting and iGaming technology platform, (iii) access to regulated growth markets (UK, Netherlands, Nordics, Belgium, Australia), (iv) entry into the fastest-growing segments of European gambling. The acquisition was funded entirely with debt, taking net leverage from 0.5x EBITDA (pre-deal, effectively net cash) to approximately 2.3x EBITDA post-deal. The stock reacted poorly, dropping from EUR 38 pre-announcement to EUR 25-30 range as the market repriced for the leverage, integration risk, and dilution of FDJ's pure-play monopoly narrative. FDJ concurrently rebranded to "FDJ United" to signal the post-Kindred identity.

**Phase 5 -- Integration Year (2025).** FY2025 was the integration execution year. The reported financials show the worst of it: EUR 199.1M "other unusual items," EPS declining 55.9% to EUR 0.95, operating margin compressing to 15.4%. Beneath the headline noise, operating profit held essentially flat at EUR 566M (vs EUR 568M in 2024) despite dilutive integration charges -- demonstrating that the underlying economics are intact. EBITDA grew 31% to EUR 943M on revenue of EUR 3,678M, confirming the combined entity's cash earnings power. The Kindred integration roadmap targets EUR 80M run-rate cost synergies by end of 2027, comprising technology platform consolidation, corporate and shared services rationalization, and procurement savings. The company announced the dividend would rise to EUR 2.10 per share for FY2025 -- a 18% increase over the EUR 1.78 paid in 2024.

**CEO continuity matters.** Stephane Pallez has served as CEO since 2014, spanning the pre-IPO preparation, the privatization itself, the 2024 Kindred transformation, and now the integration execution. She is the longest-tenured CEO in European gambling and has demonstrated the operational patience required for a business that straddles regulated monopoly economics and competitive online markets. This continuity is a significant positive for the thesis -- unlike the DEO / Diageo situation where forced CEO departures create transition risk, FDJU has stable leadership fully accountable for the Kindred integration it initiated.

**DGR observations:**

- The dividend has compounded at approximately 15% CAGR from 2019 to 2025 -- among the fastest in European large-caps. Six of seven years showed an increase; one year (2020) held flat through COVID.
- The growth is lumpy: 0% during COVID (2020), +38% recovery (2021), modest increments (2022-2023), then two large consecutive increases of +34% (2024) and +18% (2025). Management appears to use the dividend as a step-function signal rather than a smooth growth model -- stepping up when they feel confident about forward earnings trajectory and pausing when uncertainty rises (COVID 2020).
- The 2025 DPS of EUR 2.10 on reported EPS of EUR 0.95 shows a 221% payout ratio on GAAP earnings, which is of course unsustainable if the 2025 earnings level were the new normal. Adjusted for the EUR 199.1M one-off charges (approximately EUR 1.10 per share after-tax [approx]), normalized 2025 EPS is in the EUR 2.05-2.15 range, placing the payout at 97-102% on adjusted earnings. This is at the edge of sustainability but reasonable given the integration year nature and the strength of FCF (EUR 568M vs EUR 385M dividend outlay = 1.5x coverage).

**FCF coverage analysis (the proper lens for dividend sustainability):**

- 2025 FCF of EUR 568M against estimated dividends of EUR 385M (EUR 2.10 x ~183M shares) gives **1.5x coverage**.
- If we strip out Kindred integration cash costs (estimated EUR 40-80M in 2025), normalized FCF coverage is approximately 1.6-1.7x.
- The Kindred EUR 80M cost synergy target by 2027 would, if achieved, add directly to FCF and improve coverage to 1.7-1.8x on flat earnings, providing substantial dividend protection.
- Pre-Kindred FCF coverage was consistently 1.3-2.2x -- 2025's 1.5x is within that range despite integration drag.

**Dividend safety assessment:**

The core lottery business generates predictable, recession-resistant cash flows. Lottery ticket sales in France were essentially flat through COVID (down 2%) and have grown through every recession since 1933. The business is a near-perfect defensive asset within the gaming sector: (i) monopoly floor, (ii) small ticket size (average lottery stake EUR 3-5), (iii) broad customer base (25M+ French adults play at least annually), (iv) habitual purchase behavior (15M+ monthly actives).

The Kindred overlay adds cyclicality and competition exposure. Online sports betting revenue correlates with major sporting event calendars (Euros, World Cup years, Olympics are strongest) and macroeconomic confidence. Q2-Q3 tend to be strongest due to summer sports density. UK iGaming is saturated and highly competitive; Kindred holds mid-single-digit share versus Flutter/Entain leaders.

**Leverage constraint on the dividend:** With net debt/EBITDA at ~1.7x (2025) targeting 1.5x by 2026, debt service absorbs approximately EUR 60-80M of interest expense annually. Credit agreements likely contain maintenance covenants at 3.5-4.0x net leverage [unverified -- check loan documentation]. For the dividend to face genuine pressure, either (a) EBITDA would need to fall to EUR 600M (from EUR 943M -- implies 36% decline) or (b) net debt would need to spike to EUR 2.5B+ (unlikely absent further M&A). Neither scenario is plausible in a base case.

**French state influence on the dividend:** The ~20% state stake and board seat ensure the dividend is politically visible. Any cut would embarrass the government that privatized FDJ and encouraged 500,000 retail investors to buy at EUR 19.90. The state's continued capital return expectation is a structural support for the dividend.

**3-year dividend cut probability: 10-15%.** The risk is not from the legacy lottery business (rock-solid) but from the Kindred integration. If Kindred revenues decline in the highly competitive UK market and synergies underdeliver, management could choose to freeze the dividend rather than cut -- preserving the nominal amount while reducing the payout ratio through earnings growth. An outright cut is unlikely unless net leverage exceeds 3.0x AND EBITDA declines for two consecutive years.

**Risks specific to the Kindred integration:**

## PART VII -- MOAT AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION

**Moat pillars (combined entity):**

The legislation would require parliamentary action to alter, and the state's 20% ownership provides built-in political alignment.

2. **Distribution scale (France).** 30,800+ physical retail points (tabacs, news shops, certain supermarkets) across France. This network took 90 years to build and is not replicable. A hypothetical competitor entering the French market (even if it could) would require 5-10 years and EUR 1B+ to construct a similar network. The digital channel now accounts for ~25% of French revenue but the physical network remains essential for cash-heavy demographic and customer acquisition.

3. **Brand trust (France).** FDJ's brand awareness in France exceeds 95% (historical disclosures). Lottery is culturally embedded -- Loto draws are a French tradition dating back to the 18th century, with multiple generational franchise. Customer loyalty is high; average customer has participated in lottery for 15+ years.

4. **Technology platform (post-Kindred).** Kindred's proprietary tech stack is among the best in European online gambling, enabling rapid product deployment across regulated markets. The sports betting engine, iGaming platform, and responsible gaming tools represent 20+ years of iterative development that a new entrant could not easily replicate.

5. **Regulatory expertise.** Combined entity has deep relationships with gaming regulators in France (ANJ), UK (UKGC), Netherlands (KSA), Belgium, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, and more. Regulatory compliance is a significant barrier to entry in gambling and the combined footprint is uniquely broad.

6. **Customer data.** Combined customer base of 30M+ registered accounts provides data advantages for marketing, product development, and cross-sell optimization.

**Moat weaknesses:**

- **Online betting is highly competitive.** Unlike the French lottery business, the Kindred online segment competes on product, odds, bonusing, and marketing against deep-pocketed peers. No structural moat exists; competitive position requires continuous investment.
- **Regulatory risk is always adverse.** A moat built on regulatory exclusivity is, by definition, hostage to the regulator. Any populist government could increase gaming taxes or tighten rules.

## PART XII -- TAX CONSIDERATIONS (CHINA RESIDENT HOLDER)

**Entity structure:** FDJU is a French societe anonyme (SA) incorporated in France, with all operational entities and tax residence in France. Dividends are sourced from French corporate operations and subject to French withholding tax on cross-border distributions.

**Standard French WHT:** 25% on dividends paid to non-resident individuals (as of 2024-2025 tax code).

**France-China tax treaty:** The France-China double taxation agreement (ratified 2013, effective 2015) reduces French withholding on portfolio dividends paid to Chinese tax residents to **10% WHT** (subject to proper documentation -- certificate of Chinese tax residence).

**Steps for Chinese-resident holder to claim treaty rate:**

1. Obtain Chinese certificate of tax residence from local tax authority (formulaire 6166 equivalent).
2. Submit French form 5000/5003 ("Convention de double imposition -- demande de reduction d'impot") to the custodian/broker before dividend record date.
3. Custodian transmits to French tax authority; reduced 10% WHT applied at source.
4. If deadline missed, file form 5001/5002 for post-payment refund (18-24 month processing).

**Effective tax cost for Chinese-resident holder:**

- If treaty rate applied at source: 10% French WHT, net dividend 90% of gross.
- If full WHT withheld: 25% French WHT initially, 15% refund available via reclaim (subject to documentation and 3-year statute of limitations).
- China domestic taxation: individual investor typically taxed 20% on foreign dividend income, with credit available for French WHT paid. Net all-in tax rate ~20% on gross dividend.

**Capital gains treatment:** France does not impose capital gains withholding on non-resident portfolio shareholders. Capital gains are taxable only in the country of residence (China, in this case).

**Franking credits / tax credit equivalents:** France does not operate an imputation system; dividends are fully subject to French corporate tax (approximately 25%) before distribution, then subject to WHT on payment. There is no France-specific credit like Australian franking credits.

**Practical recommendation:** Ensure your broker/custodian is set up to apply the France-China treaty rate of 10% at source. The 25% default rate without treaty benefit is punitive.

**Currency exposure:** All dividends paid in EUR. Chinese-resident holders convert to CNY at prevailing exchange rate at payment date. EUR/CNY has ranged 7.0-8.0 over 2023-2025; some holders may consider EUR forward hedging for income stability but cost of hedging (annual ~1-2%) typically exceeds benefit.
