## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: BUY at $239. ACCUMULATE aggressively below $210. Dividend Safety: 8/10. Confidence: High.**

Lowe's Companies is the #2 home improvement retailer in the US (behind Home Depot) with approximately 1,700 stores generating $86B in annual revenue. The company operates primarily a US retail format serving both DIY (Do-It-Yourself) and Pro (professional contractor) customers across the United States, with smaller operations in Canada and a recently sold Mexican business. FY2025 revenue reached $86.3B, up 3.1% year-over-year as the housing market showed signs of recovery after 2023-2024 weakness.

At $239.71, Lowe's trades at approximately 20x forward earnings and yields approximately 1.92%. The dividend of $1.15 per quarter ($4.60 annualized) has been raised for 61 consecutive years, making it a Dividend King. ## PART II -- COMPANY SNAPSHOT

Lowe's Companies, Inc. is a Mooresville, North Carolina-headquartered home improvement retailer, founded in 1946. The company operates approximately 1,700 stores across the US and Canada, focusing on the home improvement category including building products, appliances, tools, home decor, and seasonal products. Under CEO Marvin Ellison (since 2018), the company has executed a significant operational transformation emphasizing Pro customer acquisition, technology modernization, and margin expansion.

**CEO:** Marvin Ellison (since July 2018). Prior JC Penney CEO (2014-2018), Home Depot EVP. African-American trailblazer in retail. Focused on "perpetual productivity improvement" and Pro market share gains. Age 60.

**CFO:** Brandon Sink (since April 2022). Internal promotion.

**Employees:** ~300,000 as of January 2026.

- **Sector / Industry:** Consumer Cyclical / Home Improvement Retail
- **Business segments:** Single reporting segment (home improvement retail), with internal views on:
  - DIY (Do-It-Yourself) customers: ~60% of revenue
  - Pro (professional contractor) customers: ~25% of revenue (growing)
  - Online: ~10% of revenue (growing)

- **Key product categories (FY2025 approximate):**
  - Appliances: ~$12B (major appliances, HVAC)
  - Tools & Hardware: ~$11B
  - Lumber & Building Products: ~$10B
  - Paint: ~$6B
  - Kitchen & Bath: ~$8B
  - Outdoor & Seasonal: ~$9B
  - Flooring: ~$7B
  - Lighting & Electrical: ~$4B
  - Storage & Organization: ~$3B
  - Installation services: ~$4B
  - Other: ~$12B

- **Geographic exposure (FY2025):** United States 98%+; Canada ~2%. Mexico business sold January 2024.

- **Top customers:** Mostly retail consumers; no customer concentration
- **Main competitors:**
  - *Primary:* Home Depot (HD) -- dominant competitor
  - *Paint:* Sherwin-Williams (owns retail), Behr (via HD)
  - *Appliances:* Best Buy, Amazon, e-commerce
  - *Building Products:* Small local lumber yards
  - *Pro Market:* Ferguson, HD Supply, specialty Pro dealers
- **Recent corporate actions:** Mexico business sold to Mexican investor group (closed January 2024); Canadian business continuing operations; store optimization program ongoing

**Domicile note:** Lowe's is a US C-corporation. Dividends to non-US holders subject to standard 30% withholding, reducible to 10% under US-China treaty.
