## EXTENDED ANALYSIS -- ROUTE DENSITY ECONOMICS

Route density is Cintas's biggest moat:

**How it works:**
- Drivers complete 100+ customer stops per day
- Each additional customer has minimal marginal cost
- Fixed costs (truck, driver, fuel) spread across more revenue

**Route math:**
- Average route: 110 customers × $2,000 annual = $220K annual revenue
- Route cost: $100K annual (driver + truck + maintenance)
- Route margin: 55% gross
- Route EBIT margin: 30%+

**Density advantage:**
- Cintas: 120+ stops/route (industry leader)
- Aramark: 95 stops/route
- UniFirst: 85 stops/route
- Local: 60-70 stops/route

**Scale advantage:**
- Larger customer base = higher density
- Higher density = lower costs
- Lower costs = pricing flexibility
- Pricing flexibility = customer retention

**Replication barriers:**
- Cannot build density without customers
- Cannot acquire customers without service quality
- Service quality requires investment
- Investment requires capital
- Circular moat structure
