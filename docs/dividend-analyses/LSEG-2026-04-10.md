## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: ACCUMULATE at GBP 92.38. BUY below GBP 85. Dividend Safety: 8/10. Confidence: Medium-High.**

The London Stock Exchange Group is no longer a stock exchange. This is one of the most persistently misunderstood businesses in European large-cap equities. Following the transformational USD 27 billion (approximately GBP 21 billion at deal close) Refinitiv acquisition in January 2021, LSEG today generates approximately 88% of revenue from recurring data subscriptions and financial market infrastructure services -- with the legacy London Stock Exchange equities matching engine contributing less than 5% of group revenue. What LSEG actually owns is:

- **LSEG Data & Analytics** (rebranded from Refinitiv in August 2023), the world's #2 financial data platform behind Bloomberg, serving 40,000+ institutional clients in 190 countries via Workspace terminal, Eikon, Datastream, and thousands of specialized data feeds
- **FTSE Russell** -- the indices business (FTSE 100, Russell 1000/2000/3000, and thousands of specialty indices) that benchmarks trillions of dollars of passive assets globally
- **Risk Intelligence** -- anti-money laundering, sanctions screening, KYC, identity verification data (a +13.9% organic growth segment in 2025)
- **Markets** -- equities and derivatives exchanges (LSE, Turquoise, MTS for fixed income)
- **Post Trade** -- LCH (the world's largest rates clearing house, 50%+ share of global interest rate swap clearing, 82.6% owned by LSEG), Monte Titoli settlement, EuroCCP
- **Tradeweb** -- 51.3% stake in the leading electronic fixed-income and derivatives trading platform
- **Microsoft strategic partnership** -- announced December 2022, a 10-year commercial agreement under which Microsoft invested GBP 1.4B for a ~4% LSEG stake, LSEG migrates its entire data infrastructure to Microsoft Azure, and the two co-develop AI-powered financial data products including Copilot integration and the "LSEG Everywhere" agentic AI ecosystem

2025 produced the inflection point the bulls have been waiting for. Total income (excluding recoveries) grew +7.1% on an organic constant currency basis -- ahead of the top end of management's 6-7% medium-term guidance. Adjusted EBITDA margin improved +150 basis points on a reported basis (+210 bps constant currency), reaching 50.3%. Adjusted EPS grew +15.7% to 420.6p. Total dividend rose +15.4% to 150.0p. Equity free cash flow reached GBP 2.4 billion. And the company announced a further GBP 3.0 billion buyback program through February 2027, on top of the GBP 2.1 billion already completed in 2025. The 2026 outlook is strong: 6.5-7.5% organic top-line growth, +80-100 bps EBITDA margin expansion, and >GBP 2.7 billion of equity free cash flow.

At GBP 92.38, the stock trades at 22.0x 2025 adjusted EPS of 420.6p, 12.1x EV/EBITDA on GBP 4.5 billion EBITDA, and offers a modest 1.6% trailing dividend yield. This is NOT a dividend income story -- the yield is well below Intertek's 3.7%. It IS a dividend GROWTH story -- the 5-year DPS CAGR is approximately 14%, annual raises have compounded from 43p (2016) to 150p (2025), and management expects continued 10-15% dividend growth for the medium term. On a 5-year yield-on-cost basis at current dividend trajectory, a 2026 entry produces 3-4% YoC by 2031. Combined with expected multiple expansion as adjusted margins normalize to the 55-60% range, total returns of 12-15% annualized are achievable.

My view: LSEG is the closest European equivalent to S&P Global (SPGI). Both run recurring-revenue data businesses with high margins, strong FCF, competitive moats, and consistent dividend growth. LSEG trades at roughly half SPGI's EV/EBITDA multiple (12x vs. ~22x). Part of this reflects the ongoing Refinitiv integration complexity and elevated capex intensity (approximately 9.5% of revenue in 2026 guidance), but part is simple geographic arbitrage -- the "London discount" applied to UK-listed financial services companies. As integration completes, capex normalizes, and AI product launches drive revenue re-acceleration, the discount should compress materially.

## PART III -- HISTORICAL REVIEW: THE STRATEGIC ARC

**Phase 1 -- Exchange Operator (Pre-2014).** Before FTSE Russell, LSEG was a traditional exchange group: London Stock Exchange, MTS (European government bonds), LCH (stakes acquired in stages 2011-2013), and smaller specialty venues. Revenue was GBP 1-1.5B. Operating margins were in the mid-40s percent. Growth was modest (3-5% organic). The business model was dependent on transaction volumes (cyclical) and listing fees (cyclical). Market cap was approximately GBP 7-10B.

**Phase 2 -- FTSE Russell and Data Pivot (2014-2019).** The Frank Russell Company acquisition (announced 2014, completed 2015 for approximately USD 2.7B) was the critical strategic decision that set LSEG on its current path. FTSE Russell is structurally different from exchange revenue: it is a recurring-revenue business tied to AUM benchmarked to indices, with embedded switching costs (moving a passive fund from FTSE Russell to MSCI involves tax events, regulatory filings, and investor disclosures). By 2019, revenue had grown to GBP 2.3B with operating margins in the high-30s. Market cap approached GBP 15-20B.

**Phase 3 -- The Refinitiv Bet (2019-2021).** CEO David Schwimmer (appointed April 2018 from Goldman Sachs) announced the USD 27 billion all-share acquisition of Refinitiv in August 2019. Refinitiv had been carved out of Thomson Reuters in October 2018 by a Blackstone-led consortium; Blackstone held 55%, Thomson Reuters retained 45%. Refinitiv was the world's #2 financial data provider behind Bloomberg, with:
- 40,000+ institutional clients across 190 countries
- USD 6 billion annual revenue
- 18,500 employees
- Flagship Eikon/Workspace terminal, Datastream, World-Check (sanctions screening)

The strategic rationale: LSEG -- a small exchange with high margins and low growth -- needed to pivot to the structurally faster-growing data segment. Refinitiv offered a global client base, a comprehensive product portfolio, and the scale needed to compete with Bloomberg and S&P Global. The deal was transformative: LSEG transitioned from a GBP 2B exchange to a GBP 7B data-and-infrastructure giant overnight.

Execution obstacles:
- EU antitrust required divestment of Borsa Italiana (sold to Euronext for EUR 4.3B in 2021)
- EU antitrust also required commitments on index data access
- Deal closed 29 January 2021 -- nearly 18 months after announcement due to regulatory review
- Immediate-integration revenue synergies were modest; cost synergies of GBP 350M+ (achieved) required multi-year execution

**Phase 4 -- Microsoft Partnership and AI Era (2022-Present).** The December 2022 Microsoft strategic partnership announcement marked the next strategic inflection. Key terms:
- 10-year commercial agreement
- Microsoft investment of GBP 1.4 billion for ~4% of LSEG
- LSEG migrates entire data infrastructure to Microsoft Azure
- Co-development of AI-powered financial data products
- Microsoft board representation
- Revenue commitments by both parties (undisclosed specific figures)

The strategic bet: in a world where AI is the defining technology theme of the next decade, LSEG gets privileged access to Microsoft's AI capabilities (OpenAI GPT models, Copilot, Azure AI services), enterprise distribution (Microsoft 365, Teams), and cloud infrastructure at preferential economics. By 2025, this partnership had expanded into what LSEG calls "LSEG Everywhere" -- a broader ecosystem of AI partnerships including Anthropic, Databricks, Open AI, Rogo, and Snowflake. The Q3 2025 Trading Update described LSEG as "the partner of choice in AI" for financial data.

**Phase 5 -- 2025 Inflection (Present).** 2025 results validated the strategic thesis. Total income (excl. recoveries) grew +7.1% organic constant currency -- ahead of the top end of the 6-7% medium-term guidance. Adjusted EBITDA margin improved +210 bps constant currency to 50.3%. Adjusted EPS grew +15.7%. Equity FCF reached GBP 2.4B. The company announced the additional GBP 3.0B buyback through February 2027. Importantly, capex intensity remained elevated at 9.5% for 2026 guidance -- signaling the integration and Azure migration are still in progress, not complete.

## PART VI -- THE MICROSOFT PARTNERSHIP -- DETAILED ANALYSIS

The December 2022 Microsoft strategic partnership is arguably the most important strategic decision LSEG has made since the Refinitiv acquisition. It deserves dedicated analysis.

**Terms (per December 2022 announcement and subsequent disclosures):**
- 10-year commercial partnership
- Microsoft acquires approximately 4% of LSEG for GBP 1.4 billion (implying LSEG enterprise value at time of announcement ~GBP 45B [approx])
- Microsoft appoints a board observer (not a full director) to the LSEG Board
- LSEG commits to migrate its data infrastructure to Microsoft Azure
- Co-development of products including AI-powered analytics, natural language interfaces, agentic AI tools
- Revenue sharing arrangements on co-developed products [unverified specific terms]
- Integration of LSEG data into Microsoft 365 ecosystem (Teams, Outlook, Excel, Copilot)
- Launch of first-of-its-kind Azure-based trade routing network serving 1,600 investment firms and brokers (per LSEG Q3 2025 Trading Update)

**Strategic rationale -- three reasons this matters:**

1. **Cost reduction and scalability.** Migrating from Refinitiv's legacy on-premise data centers to Microsoft Azure reduces infrastructure costs through operational leverage and shared investment. Specific savings are not publicly quantified but management has indicated meaningful run-rate savings [unverified quantum, estimates range GBP 100-200M annually at steady state].

2. **AI capabilities and competitive positioning.** Microsoft's AI infrastructure (OpenAI GPT models, Copilot, Azure AI services) is world-class. ## PART VII -- BUSINESS QUALITY & MOAT

**Moat Assessment: Wide, with multiple structurally reinforcing pillars.**

1. **Data network effects (Data & Analytics).** More institutional clients contributing and consuming data makes the dataset more valuable. Refinitiv's 40,000+ institutional clients generate proprietary pricing, trading, and reference data that no competitor can replicate. The Workspace terminal is a classic network-effect product: adoption by key clients drives feature prioritization, which drives further adoption.

2. **Clearing house natural monopoly (LCH).** Per regulatory data, LCH clears more than 50% of global interest rate swap notional volume (as of 2010; current market share is consistently above this threshold [unverified for 2025]). Post-2008 regulatory mandates require OTC derivatives to be centrally cleared. LCH's network effects are self-reinforcing: more participants clear through LCH, which creates more netting benefits, which attracts additional participants. Switching to a competing CCP requires regulatory approvals and multi-million-dollar infrastructure investment.

3. **Index intellectual property (FTSE Russell).** FTSE 100, FTSE All-World, Russell 1000/2000/3000, and thousands of specialty indices are embedded in trillions of dollars of passive investment products. AUM benchmarked to FTSE Russell indices generates recurring revenue tied to asset values. Switching from FTSE Russell to MSCI or S&P Dow Jones requires tax events, regulatory filings, investor communications, and typically fund restructuring. The cost is substantial; the migration risk is high. This creates long-term customer lock-in.

4. **Customer switching costs (Data & Analytics).** Financial institutions build their risk management, compliance, and trading systems around LSEG's data feeds. Switching from Workspace to Bloomberg or S&P Capital IQ requires months of technology integration work, retraining of users, and potential gaps in data continuity. Average customer life at the institutional level is measured in decades.

5. **Regulatory moats.** FCA (UK Financial Conduct Authority) and other national regulators have approved LCH as a critical financial market infrastructure. New entrants face multi-year, multi-million-dollar regulatory approval processes. Similarly, FTSE Russell's benchmark administrator status under EU BMR (Benchmarks Regulation) creates barriers to entry.

**Moat trajectory: Widening.** The Microsoft partnership adds AI-enabled differentiation that no competitor currently possesses. Risk Intelligence (fastest-growing segment) is increasingly critical to financial crime compliance -- regulatory demand continues to expand. FTSE Russell continues to gain share in thematic and ESG indices. LCH maintains dominant market share in rates clearing.

**Return on invested capital:** Given PPA complications, reported ROIC is depressed. Adjusted ROIC (excluding PPA amortization from denominator and numerator, adjusted EBITDA-based) is estimated at 15-18% [approx]. Cash ROIC (operating free cash flow / invested capital) is likely in the 20-25% range for steady-state operations [approx].
