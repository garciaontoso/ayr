## PART I · EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: ACCUMULATE at GBP 89.68. BUY below GBP 80. · Dividend Safety: 8/10 · Confidence: Medium-High**

LSEG is no longer a stock exchange. This is one of the most commonly misunderstood businesses in European equities. Following the transformational GBP 27B Refinitiv acquisition (closed January 2021), LSEG is now a global data, analytics, and market infrastructure company with approximately 70%+ of revenue from recurring data subscriptions. The name "London Stock Exchange" is legacy branding — the actual exchange (equities matching engine) contributes less than 5% of group revenue. What LSEG actually owns is Refinitiv's Workspace terminal (the #2 financial data platform behind Bloomberg), FTSE Russell indices, LCH (the world's largest rates clearing house), Tradeweb (minority stake), FXALL (the largest institutional FX trading platform), and a strategic partnership with Microsoft to rebuild the entire data platform on Azure.

The stock trades at 8,968p (GBP 89.68) against an FMP DCF of 19,288p (GBP 192.88) — a 54% discount. Revenue has grown from GBP 2.0B (2020 pre-Refinitiv) to GBP 9.3B (2025). Adjusted operating margins are expanding as integration synergies land. The dividend has grown at 13.5% CAGR over recent years. And LSEG sits at the intersection of two structural mega-trends: (1) the explosion in financial data consumption, driven by quantitative investing, regulatory reporting, and AI-driven analytics; and (2) the shift from bilateral OTC trading to centrally-cleared, electronically-traded markets.

The current yield of approximately 1.5% (GBP 1.36 DPS / GBP 89.68) appears low, but the DGR of 13.5%+ means the yield-on-cost will double within five years if the trajectory holds. This is a dividend *growth* story, not a dividend *income* story.

**Top 3 reasons to own (specific):**
1. **Data & Analytics is a recurring-revenue toll road.** Approximately 70% of LSEG revenue comes from data subscriptions with 90%+ renewal rates. Financial professionals need Refinitiv Workspace, FTSE Russell index data, and regulatory data feeds the way lawyers need Westlaw — it is embedded in daily workflows. This is not cyclical exchange revenue; it is subscription SaaS economics.
2. **The Microsoft partnership is a generational technology catalyst.** In December 2022, LSEG and Microsoft announced a 10-year strategic partnership: Microsoft invested GBP 1.4B for a ~4% stake, LSEG migrates its entire data infrastructure to Azure, and the two co-develop AI-powered financial data products. The Microsoft partnership gives LSEG access to Azure's AI capabilities (Copilot integration into Workspace) and Microsoft's enterprise distribution channel — advantages no competitor can replicate.
3. **LCH clearing is an irreplaceable monopoly asset.** LCH clears over 90% of global interest rate swaps and is the dominant rates CCP (central counterparty). Post-2008 regulatory mandates require OTC derivatives to be centrally cleared. LCH's network effects are self-reinforcing: the more participants clear through LCH, the more netting benefits, the harder it is for competitors to attract volume. This is a natural monopoly protected by regulation.

**Top 3 reasons not to own:**
1. **The Refinitiv integration has been slower and costlier than planned.** The original GBP 350M annual synergy target has been achieved, but the technology migration (decommissioning legacy Refinitiv systems) is still ongoing in 2025-2026. The GBP 1.9B CapEx figure in 2025 reflects this ongoing integration spend. Until the migration is complete, LSEG is spending more on technology than its steady-state model implies.
2. **Thomson Reuters retains a ~15% stake and board influence.** Thomson Reuters held 15% at IPO and has been gradually selling down, creating periodic share supply overhang. Their continued presence also limits LSEG's strategic flexibility (any major deal requires TRI concurrence).
3. **Valuation is demanding on reported earnings.** P/E on 2025 reported EPS of 2.37p is 37.8x. Even adjusting for amortization of Refinitiv intangibles (GBP 2.2B D&A, much of which is purchase price amortization), the adjusted P/E is approximately 18-22x. For a 5-7% organic growth business, this is premium pricing that requires consistent execution.

**Next 12 months catalysts:**
- Full-year 2025 results (March 2026) — Refinitiv synergy and integration update
- Microsoft Copilot integration into Workspace — product launch expected 2026
- Potential Thomson Reuters stake sell-down — removes overhang
- LCH clearing volumes in rates and FX
- 2026 dividend announcement — expect 12-15% increase

## PART III · HISTORICAL REVIEW — STRATEGIC ARC

**Phase 1 — Exchange Operator (Pre-2019).** LSEG was a traditional exchange group: London Stock Exchange, MTS (European government bond platform), LCH (acquired in stages, completed 2013), and FTSE Russell indices (Russell acquired 2015). Revenue was GBP 2-2.3B. The business was high-margin (35-40% operating) but low-growth (3-4% organic). Market cap was approximately GBP 15-20B.

**Phase 2 — The Refinitiv Deal (2019-2021).** CEO David Schwimmer (formerly Goldman Sachs M&A banker) announced the GBP 27B Refinitiv acquisition in August 2019. Refinitiv — carved out of Thomson Reuters in 2018 by a Blackstone-led consortium — was the world's second-largest financial data provider after Bloomberg, with 40,000+ institutional clients and revenues of ~GBP 5B. The deal was transformational: LSEG went from a GBP 2B exchange to a GBP 7B data-and-infrastructure giant. Borsa Italiana was sold to Euronext for EUR 4.3B to satisfy EU antitrust. The deal closed January 29, 2021.

**Phase 3 — Integration and Microsoft Partnership (2021-2024).** The first three years post-close were integration-heavy: migrating thousands of data feeds, consolidating technology platforms, achieving GBP 350M+ of cost synergies, and building the Microsoft partnership. The strategic bet was that LSEG could take Refinitiv's comprehensive-but-aging data platform and rebuild it on modern cloud infrastructure (Azure), with AI capabilities (Copilot), and Microsoft's enterprise distribution. Margins were depressed during this period (16-23%) as integration costs, platform decommissioning, and dual-running systems consumed investment.

**Phase 4 — Inflection (2025+).** 2025 results show the inflection: revenue GBP 9.3B (+5.1%), operating income GBP 2.16B (+47.9% vs reported 2024), net income GBP 1.25B (+82.3%). The integration spend is peaking and starting to decline. Adjusted operating margins are approaching the mid-to-high 40% range that management has targeted for the medium term. The Microsoft partnership is beginning to produce commercial products.

## PART VI · MICROSOFT PARTNERSHIP — DEDICATED SECTION

The Microsoft strategic partnership, announced December 2022, is the most important external catalyst for LSEG's medium-term value.

**Terms:**
- Microsoft acquired ~4% of LSEG for GBP 1.4B, with board representation
- 10-year commercial agreement: LSEG migrates its entire data infrastructure to Azure
- Co-development of AI-powered financial data products
- Integration of Refinitiv data into Microsoft Teams and M365 for enterprise distribution

**Why it matters:**
1. **Cost reduction.** Migrating from legacy on-premise data centers to Azure reduces infrastructure costs by an estimated GBP 100-200M annually at steady state.
2. **AI capabilities.** Microsoft's Copilot and GPT-4 integration into Workspace gives LSEG a generative AI layer that Bloomberg is still building. An analyst asking natural-language questions of Workspace data — summarizing earnings transcripts, screening for credit events, generating regulatory reports — is a product moat that compounds over time.
3. **Distribution expansion.** There are millions of Microsoft Teams users in financial services. Embedding LSEG data feeds into Teams creates a distribution channel that Refinitiv Workspace alone could never access. This potentially expands the addressable market from 400,000 terminal users to millions of casual financial data consumers.

**Risks:** Technology migration of this scale is complex and multi-year. If the Azure migration causes service disruptions for institutional clients, reputation damage could drive churn. The partnership also creates dependency on Microsoft — if the relationship sours, LSEG faces significant switching costs.

## PART VII · MOAT AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION

**Moat pillars:**
1. **Data network effects.** More clients contributing and consuming data makes the dataset more valuable. Refinitiv's 40,000+ institutional clients generate proprietary pricing, trading, and reference data that no competitor can replicate.
2. **Clearing house natural monopoly (LCH).** 90%+ market share in rates clearing. Regulatory mandate + netting benefits + default fund structure create insurmountable barriers to entry.
3. **Index intellectual property (FTSE Russell).** FTSE 100, Russell 2000/3000, and thousands of specialty indices are embedded in trillions of dollars of passive investment products. AUM benchmarked to FTSE Russell indices generates recurring revenue tied to assets under management.
4. **Switching costs.** Financial institutions build their risk management, compliance, and trading systems around LSEG's data feeds. Switching requires months of technology integration work.

**Capital allocation scorecard:**
- **Dividends: A.** 14% 5-year CAGR with conservative payout ratio.
- **Buybacks: A.** GBP 2.15B in 2025 — aggressive, well-timed (stock below DCF fair value).
- **M&A: B+.** Refinitiv was bold and strategically correct but execution has been slower than planned. The GBP 350M synergy target was achieved but margin recovery is still in progress.
- **Organic investment: A.** The Microsoft partnership and platform rebuild demonstrate willingness to invest for long-term competitive advantage.
