## TL;DR (10 lines)

1. **VERDICT: HOLD with a bias toward SMALL ADD on 5%+ pullbacks.** Not a buy at 3.2% regular yield; not a sell given leverage now at 1.75x net debt / EBITDA.
2. The Safe Harbor divestiture ($5.65B, closed Q2-Q3 2025) is **value-unlocking, not destroying** — it produced a $1.5B book gain and crushed leverage from 6.6x to 1.75x.
3. **MH core business moat is real** — 99.1% Same Property occupancy, 5.2% 2025 rent increase, avg. 5y voluntary turnover <1% (2025 10-K Item 7). Affordable housing is structurally short supply.
4. **RV is the weak segment** — 2025 Same Property NOI -1.4%, second consecutive year of decline driven by transient revenue weakness. Bulls ignore it; I don't.
5. **UK (Park Holidays) is a disappointment** — $180.8M goodwill impairment in 2024 + $386.7M broader impairment in 2025 + 30 bps occupancy decline. The 2022 acquisition at $1.24B is underwater.
6. **Dividend safety 7/10.** Regular $1.04/qtr is 62% of 2025 Core FFO ($6.68) — conservative even stripped of special — and covered 2.1x by interest coverage. But Core FFO has **declined 3 consecutive years** from $7.35 peak (2022).
7. **Leadership transition risk**: Gary Shiffman retired Oct 1 2025 after 40 years; Charles Young (ex-Invitation Homes COO) now CEO. Shiffman stays as Chairman. First CEO handoff in company history during a strategic pivot.
8. **Elliott Management activism, per user's brief, is NOT verifiable in the SEC filings I've reviewed** — I flag this in the Unverified section. The strategic pivot (divestiture, special dividend, buyback, material weakness disclosure, CEO change) is documented; the activist pressure narrative is not in the 10-Ks.
9. **Fair value $130-$145** using 19-21x 2026E Core FFO ~$6.90. Current $128.62 is a rounding error from fair. Below $115 = table pounder.
10. **Do NOT buy this for the aristocrat thesis.** SUI is NOT a Dividend Aristocrat — 2024 dividend was flat vs. 2023. The "25+ year increase streak" narrative circulating online is incorrect. Real streak per SEC filings: broken at least once in 2024.

## PART I · EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: HOLD (bias toward ADD below $120).** **Dividend Safety: 7/10.** **Confidence: Medium.**

Sun Communities is in the second act of the messiest strategic pivot the MH REIT space has ever seen. The company that spent 2020-2022 lunging headlong into marinas (Safe Harbor, $2.0B, Oct 2020) and UK holiday parks (Park Holidays, $1.24B, Apr 2022) spent 2024-2025 unwinding most of it — 25 non-core sales in 2024 ($476.8M), a material weakness in ICFR tied to a botched ERP rollout, the retirement of 40-year CEO Gary Shiffman (announced Nov 2024, effective Oct 1 2025), and finally the Safe Harbor divestiture to Brookfield Infrastructure's BIP Poseidon vehicle for **$5.65B generating a $1.5B book gain** (2025 10-K Item 7 line 9399).

The net effect: Sun is now what it was in 2019 — a focused, low-leverage MH+RV platform — with a bolt-on UK holiday park business that probably shouldn't be there, and **the best balance sheet in its public-company history.** Total debt fell from $7.35B (2024) to $1.83B (2025); net debt / LTM EBITDA from 6.6x to 1.75x; 100% of remaining debt is fixed at 3.38% weighted coupon with 7.1y weighted maturity. Management redeemed $956.5M of unsecured notes, repaid $1.6B of revolver, paid $737.7M of mortgage debt, bought back 4.3M shares at $125.62, paid a **$4.00 special distribution** (total $521.3M in May 2025), and **raised the regular distribution 10.6%** from $0.94 to $1.04 per quarter (2025 10-K line 10129).

That is the uplift pitch. The variant: **Core FFO/share has declined three consecutive years** from $7.35 (2022) to $6.68 (2025) — a 9.1% cumulative decline — while MH rents were compounding 5-6%. RV Same Property NOI: -1.4% in 2025, -2.8% in 2024. Park Holidays took a $180.8M goodwill impairment in 2024 + $386.7M in broader 2025 impairments "primarily within the RV and UK segments" (2025 10-K line 9947). Shiffman is now Chairman only. New CEO Charles Young is roughly a week in the seat at report date.

**This is not a broken thesis — it is a deliberately slimmed-down one, and at 3.2% regular yield the market has already priced most of the unlock.**

**Top 3 to own at $128.62:** (1) cleanest balance sheet ever — 1.75x net debt/EBITDA, 3.66x fixed charge coverage; (2) MH is genuinely durable — 99.1% Same Property occupancy, inelastic affordable housing demand, zoning-protected supply; (3) 2025 capital return ($1.58B combined) signals a shareholder-friendly board.

**Top 3 NOT to add today:** (1) 3.2% yield from a REIT with 3 consecutive years of Core FFO/share declines and a flat 2024 distribution; (2) Park Holidays impairment cycle looks incomplete — expect more 2026 writedowns; (3) new CEO + first full post-Safe Harbor year = execution risk concentrated in 2026.

**12m catalysts:** 2026 Core FFO guidance; RV stabilization in Apr-Sep 2026 summer; Park Holidays 2026 restructuring signal; additional special distribution if unused 1031 escrow ($57.2M) isn't deployed; any tax surprise on the $1.5B Safe Harbor gain.

## PART III · HISTORICAL REVIEW — 10-YEAR ARC

**Phase 1: The Quiet Compounder (2014-2019).** Through 2019, Sun was a mid-cap MH/RV REIT compounding at ~8-10% TR, run by founder Gary Shiffman since the 1975 predecessor. Core FFO/share grew from $3.14 (2015) to $4.92 (2019); revenue from $690M to $1.25B; DPS from $2.56 to $3.00 (a 4.1% CAGR tracking MH rent increases). The business was a masterclass in compound inevitability: affordable housing under persistent short supply, site-owned homes with near-zero voluntary turnover, 5-6% annual rent increases.

**Phase 2: The COVID Blowout (2020).** SUI posted $1.38B revenue and $5.09 Core FFO/share. **In October 2020, Sun acquired Safe Harbor Marinas for $2.0 billion** — its largest acquisition to date (2020 10-K Item 7 line 11411) — for 99 properties with 38,800 wet slips/dry storage spaces. Total 2020 deal value including bolt-ons: $3.0B, funded with $1.9B of equity. The thesis was that marinas would be the "coastal experiential" analogue to inland RV resorts. SUI stock hit an all-time closing high near $198 in December 2021.

**Phase 3: The Park Holidays Overreach (2022).** On April 7, 2022, SUI closed its Park Holidays UK acquisition for £950M (~$1.24B per 2022 Q1 10-Q line 2496-2506) — 40 owned + 2 managed parks, 15,906 sites, 1,140 development sites. Funded with cash on the amended $4.2B senior credit facility, new 4.2% unsecured notes due 2032, and 186,044 common shares issued to PH management. First-ever international footprint; FX exposure to GBP/CAD/AUD; leverage rose from ~5x to ~6.4x just as the Fed began hiking from 0.25% toward 5.50%. Top tick of the cycle. Revenue grew 29.9% to $2.9B but Core FFO/share peaked at $7.35. **Everything after 2022 was compression.**

**Phase 4: The Pain Cycle (2023-2024).** By YE2023, net debt/EBITDA touched 10.0x. Core FFO/share fell to $7.10. Sun posted a **$213.3M net loss** in 2023, driven by a $369.9M Marina-segment goodwill impairment. RV transient revenue softness emerged. In 2024, Sun sold 25 properties ($476.8M), began an internal restructuring initiative, implemented a new ERP that immediately triggered a **material weakness in ICFR** (2024 10-K Item 9A line 15844), took a further $180.8M Park Holidays goodwill impairment, lowered net debt/EBITDA to 6.0x, and — most important — **Gary Shiffman informed the Board in November 2024 of his intent to retire** (2024 10-K line 14802). Core FFO/share fell to $6.81 — the first YoY decline in a decade. Dividend was **held flat at $0.94/quarter all four quarters of 2024** (2024 Q3 10-Q). **The pristine dividend growth record broke.**

**Phase 5: The Divestiture + Reset (2025).** The defining year. Chronology:
- **Feb 24, 2025**: Definitive agreement to sell Safe Harbor to BIP Poseidon Holdco L.P. (Brookfield Infrastructure vehicle) for **$5.65 billion** — 2.8x the $2.0B 2020 acquisition cost (2024 10-K Note 20 line 4017; Exhibit 321).
- **Q2 2025**: Initial closing generates $5.25B pre-tax cash. $900M of 2029 and 2033 senior notes redeemed with $56.5M make-whole; $1.6B revolver and $737.7M mortgage debt repaid (2025 10-K line 10128, 10258).
- **May 14, 2025**: $4.00/share special distribution declared, $521.3M total (Q3 2025 10-Q line 1838).
- **May 2025**: Regular distribution raised from $0.94 to $1.04/share (+10.64%) (Q3 2025 10-Q line 1846).
- **July 23, 2025**: Charles D. Young named incoming CEO (Form 8-K, per 2025 10-K line 9402).
- **Aug 29, 2025**: Final Delayed Consent Subsidiaries transfer, completing Safe Harbor divestiture.
- **Sep 2025**: New $2.0B revolving credit facility signed, matures Jan 2030 (line 10262).
- **Oct 1, 2025**: Young becomes CEO; Shiffman → Chairman.
- **Full year 2025**: 4.3M shares repurchased at $125.62 avg ($539.1M); 32 UK ground lease titles bought for $386.8M (eliminating $355.9M liability, $51.8M gain); $1.5B Safe Harbor gain in discontinued ops; $386.7M additional impairments at 22 RV and UK properties; Core FFO/share $6.68 (-1.9% YoY, third consecutive decline).

At YE2025, Sun is 294 MH + 166 RV + 53 UK parks, 178,650 sites, $569.6M cash, 100% fixed debt at 3.38%, 1.75x leverage, $4.16 annualized regular dividend, new CEO. **The multi-year lunge into experiential real estate is over.** The company is back to its 2019 shape, with a tougher cost of capital and a European holiday park business that is underwater relative to its acquisition price.

## PART V · DIVIDEND DEEP-DIVE

### Is the dividend safe at $4.16 annualized (3.23% yield)?

Short answer: yes, with a medium-high confidence rating of **7/10**.

**The coverage math:**
- 2025 Core FFO/share: $6.68
- 2025 regular DPS declared (4 quarters post-raise): $4.06 actual, ~$4.16 run-rate on $1.04 quarterly
- Run-rate payout: 62.3%
- Interest coverage ratio at YE25 (from FMP): 2.15x — comfortably above 1.50x indenture covenant
- Fixed charge coverage ratio (senior credit facility): 3.66x vs. 1.40x covenant floor
- Unrestricted cash: $569.6M
- Undrawn revolver: $2.0B
- Weighted avg debt coupon: 3.38%
- Weighted avg debt maturity: 7.1 years
- 2026 debt maturities: $534.8M (short-term) per 2025 10-K line 10314

The short-term obligation of $534.8M in 2026 is fully covered by cash on hand ($569.6M). Long-term (>1 year) obligations of $3.7B are covered by operating cash flow and revolver capacity many times over. **This is not a balance sheet that is about to cut a dividend.**

**The variant concern is growth, not safety.** The dividend can remain flat or grow 2-3% for 3-4 years without threatening the coverage ratio. But if Core FFO/share continues to decline — which it has done for three straight years — then by 2028 we could be back in the 70%+ payout territory that pressures the board. The safety rating reflects both the low covenant risk AND the medium growth-durability risk.

**Scenarios:**

| Scenario | 2027E Core FFO/share | 2027 DPS | Payout | Safety impl. |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Base (MH +5%, RV flat, UK +2%) | $7.10 | $4.40 | 62% | Safe, 5% grower |
| Bear (MH +3%, RV -2%, UK impairments continue) | $6.60 | $4.30 | 65% | Tight, possible freeze |
| Bull (MH +6%, RV +3%, UK restructured) | $7.50 | $4.60 | 61% | Safe, 7% grower |
| Disaster (RV -5%, UK writedown, rate spike) | $6.10 | $4.20 | 69% | Squeezed, watch list |

Base case: 3.2% entry yield + 5% growth = ~8.2% TR. Bull case: ~10%. Bear case: -2% to -9% TR as multiple de-rates. Outcome distribution is wider than AMT or O. **You need a view on Charles Young and the RV/UK restructuring; if you don't have one, you are overpaying for optionality.**

## PART VII · SEGMENT DEEP-DIVE

### Manufactured Home Communities (MH) — the crown jewel

294 properties, 100,150 developed sites, 56.1% of the total site count. Same Property MH NOI grew 8.9% in 2025 — the fastest growth rate the segment has posted in at least seven years (2025 10-K line 9813). The drivers are textbook:
- 5.2% monthly base rent increase (2025 10-K line 9689)
- Occupancy increased to 98.1% Same Property (2025 10-K line 9680-9682)
- Rental Program (12,518 occupied homes vs. 10,923 in 2024) contributed "NOI outperformance"
- 2025 Rental Program investment: $921.3M (+17.7% YoY per 2025 10-K line 9615)

**Why the moat is durable:**

1. **Affordable housing is structurally short-supplied in the US.** MH site rents of ~$650-$750/month vs. $1,650+ for apartments — a 2-3x differential, not 20%. For bottom-third income households, MH is often the only viable homeownership path.
2. **Voluntary turnover <1%/yr.** Per 2025 10-K line 198, "less than 1.0% of the homes in our MH communities have been removed by their owners" over the 5-year period — and ~5.3% of homes transfer to new residents without physical relocation. Effective exit cost is prohibitive; the tenant is captive in the best sense.
3. **Zoning protection is de facto irreplaceable.** Most US zoning has made new MH community development functionally illegal. No new supply → pricing power in perpetuity.
4. **CapEx intensity is low.** Recurring CapEx of $69.8M on ~$1.05B NOI = 6.6% — vs. 15-20% for multifamily apartments. The land-owner model transfers most maintenance cost to residents.

**Where the bulls over-state:** affordable housing is not recession-proof in a 2008-style tail (2-4% occupancy declines historically); Florida concentration (26.4% of sites) has delivered real hurricane expense ($13.9M in 2024 Helene+Milton debris + $80.2M Ian settlement receivables per 2025 10-K line 10119); California (4.9% of sites) has creeping rent-control legislation risk; the "rent vs. CPI" headline obscures that SUI's rent growth is stairstepped rather than smooth.

**Bottom line on MH:** this is the reason to own SUI. Highest-quality, hardest-to-replicate segment in the portfolio, delivering the strongest growth in it right now.

### RV Resorts — the broken middle

166 communities, 33,330 annual sites + 23,550 transient sites. Same Property RV NOI -1.4% in 2025 (2025 10-K line 9814) and -2.8% in 2024 — two consecutive years of decline. The problem is explicitly transient revenue: "a decrease in real property transient revenue of $21.1 million or 9.0%" (2025 10-K line 9814).

**What's happening:** transient RV demand softened as post-COVID discretionary travel budgets compressed and fuel/towing-vehicle inflation bit the RV lifestyle. Management is converting transient sites to annual — margin-positive but slow. Same Property transient revenue was down 9.0% in 2025 — a material number.

**What's working:** real property (excluding transient) revenue +7.5% on site rent increases; annual conversions are NOI-quality additive; the Sun Outdoors brand provides marketing reach in a fragmented industry.

**My view:** RV will determine whether Core FFO/share grows or stays flat in 2026-2027. The Apr-Sep 2026 travel season is the test. **RV is the Charles Young test.**

### UK Holiday Parks — the expensive mistake

53 properties, 17,750 annual sites + 3,870 transient UK sites. Same Property UK NOI grew 3.5% in 2025 (vs. 9.0% in 2024 and 4.1% per-property rent growth in 2025) — a material deceleration. The 2024 goodwill impairment of $180.8M (2025 10-K line 9948: "declines in the fair value of our Park Holidays reporting unit") was the loudest bell the 10-K rang. Then 2025 brought an additional $386.7M in asset impairments "primarily within the RV and UK segments" (2025 10-K line 9947).

**What went wrong:** peak-cycle acquisition timing (£950M April 2022 at GBP $1.30); first-ever international integration with different lease structures and FX; sub-optimal ground-lease acquisition structure that required a $386.8M remediation in 2025; weather/flooding damage cycles; fundamental brand/marketing mismatch between RV-lifestyle Michigan and UK seaside holiday parks.

**Current state:** Same Property UK NOI decelerating (+3.5% 2025 vs. +9.0% 2024); occupancy 89.1% (-30 bps adjusted); rent growth cooled from 8.4% to 4.1%; 2025 impairment cycle appears incomplete.

**Opinion:** Park Holidays should be sold. The new CEO has credibility to make the decision without owning the original error (it would be Shiffman's error, not his). A 2026-2027 UK exit would eliminate FX volatility (14.4% of equity in UK/CA/AU per 2025 10-K line 10360), simplify the operating model, generate further capital return, and let SUI re-emerge as a pure US MH+RV story. **If Charles Young announces a UK strategic review in 2026, that is a buying catalyst. If he doubles down, that is a reason to exit.**

### Safe Harbor — the correct divestiture

Covered in Parts III and VI. One-line summary: bought Oct-2020 for $2.0B (generating $322.7M NOI in 2024), sold Q2-Q3 2025 for $5.65B gross ($1.5B book gain) to Brookfield Infrastructure's BIP Poseidon vehicle at a ~5.7% exit cap rate. Sun got paid to learn the lesson that it lacked marina-operator DNA.
