## PART III · HISTORICAL REVIEW — THE LEONARD MODEL

**The Philosophy.** Mark Leonard's approach to capital allocation is codified in his annual President's Letters, which constitute some of the clearest thinking on value creation in corporate history. The core principles:

1. **Buy small, mission-critical VMS businesses at 4-6x EBITDA.** These are companies that serve a single vertical (municipal water billing, golf course tee-time management, funeral home management, optometry practice software) with high switching costs, 90%+ revenue retention, and minimal competition.
2. **Never sell.** CSU has never divested an operating company. The philosophy is that permanent ownership aligns incentives, retains institutional knowledge, and compounds returns.
3. **Decentralize completely.** Acquired companies retain their brand, management team, and operational autonomy. CSU provides capital allocation discipline, performance benchmarking, and best-practice sharing — nothing else.
4. **No empire-building.** No vanity M&A, no "transformational" deals, no overpayment for growth. Every acquisition must clear a 25%+ IRR hurdle.
5. **Return all excess cash via dividends or acquisitions.** Since acquisitions generate higher IRRs than dividends or buybacks, cash goes to acquisitions first. The token dividend exists as a capital discipline signal.

**Evolution of Scale (2006-2025):**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~USD 200M | ~USD 1.8B | USD 3.97B | USD 11.62B | ~24% |
| FCF | ~USD 40M | ~USD 350M | USD 1,191M | USD 2,568M | ~24% |
| Acquisitions/year | ~USD 30M | ~USD 200M | ~USD 400M | ~USD 1,072M | ~20% |
| Share count | 21.19M | 21.19M | 21.19M | 21.19M | 0% |

**The spin-off strategy.** In 2021, CSU spun off Topicus.com (European VMS, listed on TSX-V). In 2023, Lumine Group was spun off (communications and media VMS). These spin-offs serve two purposes: (1) creating independent public currency for acquisitions in their respective verticals, and (2) introducing internal competition among CSU operating groups for deal flow — raising the bar for capital allocation quality.

## PART VII · MOAT AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION

**Moat pillars:**
1. **Mission-critical VMS switching costs.** Each acquired company serves a niche vertical where its software is deeply embedded in customer operations. Switching transit scheduling software means retraining every dispatcher. Switching funeral home management software means migrating decades of records. Customer retention is 90-95%+.
2. **Decentralized organizational structure.** CSU's operating model scales infinitely because each acquisition is autonomous. There is no integration complexity that limits deal pace. The parent provides capital allocation, benchmarking, and governance — nothing more.
3. **Reputation as permanent owner.** In the VMS M&A market, CSU has a unique advantage: founder-entrepreneurs know their company will survive, their employees will keep their jobs, and their customers will continue to be served. PE buyers flip in 5-7 years. Strategic buyers absorb and eliminate. CSU preserves. This sourcing advantage is self-reinforcing.
4. **Compound learning.** After 800+ acquisitions, CSU has built institutional knowledge about VMS operations that no competitor possesses. The benchmarking database — comparing margins, retention, pricing, and growth across hundreds of VMS businesses in dozens of verticals — is itself a competitive advantage.

**Capital allocation scorecard:**
- **Acquisitions: A+.** 800+ deals, 25%+ IRR, zero share dilution, no notable failures. Best in class.
- **Dividends: N/A.** Token; irrelevant to the investment thesis.
- **Buybacks: N/A.** CSU does not repurchase shares. Leonard has stated that buybacks make sense only when the stock trades below intrinsic value *and* there are no available acquisitions at target IRRs. Given the depth of the VMS market, acquisitions always win.
- **Organic investment: B+.** CSU's acquired companies reinvest modestly in R&D and product development. The holding company does not invest organically — growth is entirely acquisition-driven.
