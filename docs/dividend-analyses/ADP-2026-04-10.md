## PART II · COMPANY SNAPSHOT

### Business description
Automatic Data Processing is the largest provider of payroll processing and human capital management (HCM) software in the US, serving over 1 million clients globally. The business has two main segments:

1. **Employer Services (ES)** — payroll, HR, benefits, talent management, time & attendance. Sold to all sizes of employers from small business to Fortune 500. ~80% of revenue.
2. **PEO (Professional Employer Organization)** — ADP TotalSource. Co-employment model where ADP becomes the employer of record for client workers, providing full HR + benefits at scale. Smaller but higher-margin. ~20% of revenue.

### Operating model
ADP processes roughly **1 in 6 US paychecks**. The business generates **extraordinary float** — it holds client funds in escrow between payroll processing and remittance to employees/tax authorities. Interest earned on this float (the "client funds extended investment strategy") has historically contributed meaningful income, and even more so in the current rate environment (rates higher = more float income).

## PART III · HISTORICAL REVIEW — 7-YEAR STRATEGIC ARC

### 2018-2019 — The Pershing Square Battle + Operating Model Refresh
In 2017-2018, activist Bill Ackman's Pershing Square took a large position in ADP and pushed hard for operational improvements, arguing that ADP's margins were structurally below what a well-run HCM software company could deliver. The battle was contentious but ultimately constructive: **ADP's then-CEO Carlos Rodriguez accelerated margin expansion initiatives**, and the company has since delivered ~200bps of margin expansion per 3-year window — which is materially above pre-Ackman pace. Pershing Square exited but the operational agenda remained.

### 2020 — COVID Impact + Recovery
ADP's revenue dropped only 2% in FY2020 (fiscal year ending June 2020) despite massive US unemployment — a testament to the resilience of the HCM/payroll model (clients pay on subscription, not on transaction volume). Revenue resumed growing in FY2021.

### 2021-2022 — The Rate Cycle Tailwind
The Fed's rate hiking cycle starting 2022 was a massive tailwind for ADP's float income. Interest earned on client funds extended investment strategy jumped meaningfully, contributing to margin expansion and EPS acceleration. FY2022 adjusted EPS grew +15-18%, far above the underlying business growth rate. **This was a non-repeatable benefit** that analysts should normalize for.

### 2023 — CEO Transition
**Maria Black** became CEO in January 2023, succeeding Carlos Rodriguez who had led ADP for 12 years. Black was a long-tenured ADP executive (20+ years) — a classic safe internal successor. The transition was smooth. Strategy continuity: AI-enablement, mid-market enterprise focus, international expansion.

### 2024 — The AI Pivot
Under Black, ADP began heavily investing in and marketing its AI capabilities. **Workforce Now NextGen** for mid-market and **ADP Lyric HCM** for enterprise became the flagship AI-enabled platforms. Key features: generative AI copilot for HR professionals, "always-on payroll processing", expedited implementation.

### FY2025-2026 — Execution Phase
FY2025 delivered +6% revenue, +9% EPS, margin expansion ~70bps. FY2026 is tracking better: Q1 and Q2 both delivered +6% revenue, +11% EPS, +80bps margin expansion. **Management is executing cleanly** and the AI investments are beginning to pay off in client satisfaction metrics (Q2 FY26 was the best client satisfaction quarter in company history per Maria Black).

## PART V · BUSINESS QUALITY & MOAT

### Market position — dominant
- **#1 payroll processor in the US** by market share
- **1 in 6 US paychecks** processed by ADP
- Leading HCM software platform by seat count (competing with Workday in enterprise)
- Largest PEO by client count (TotalSource)

### Switching costs — enormous
Payroll is one of the stickiest enterprise software categories. Moving payroll is high-risk (tax compliance, data migration, employee impact), high-cost (implementation fees, training), and low-ROI (the new vendor is rarely meaningfully better). ADP's retention rates are **~91-93% annually** — among the best in enterprise software.

### Scale advantages
- ADP has compliance infrastructure for federal + 50 state + thousands of local tax jurisdictions
- International presence (reach in 140+ countries)
- Float investment scale — ADP's client funds are large enough to negotiate favorable rates
- R&D budget scale — AI investments require scale ADP has

### Competitive threats
**The real bear case**: cloud-native HCM vendors are attacking ADP from multiple angles:
- **Workday** — enterprise HCM + financials; larger, faster-growing, higher valuation
- **BambooHR** — small-mid business, user-friendly
- **Gusto** — small business + mobile-first
- **Rippling** — modern stack, IT + HR unified
- **Paylocity, Paycor, Paychex** — direct US payroll competitors

ADP is holding serve. Client satisfaction at record highs (per Q2 FY26 call). But the **competitive intensity is real** and over 10+ years the market share math may not favor ADP as new segments emerge.

### Capital efficiency — best-in-class
- **ROIC 30%+**: extraordinary for a company this size
- **Asset-light model**: real estate and servers are maintenance, not growth
- **FCF/EBITDA 85%+**: cash conversion is gold standard
- **Capex intensity <3%** of revenue

## PART VI · CAPITAL ALLOCATION (the record)

### Dividends
Paid approximately $2.2B in FY2025. DGR consistently ~10-11%. This is the **priority use of cash** for ADP. Not dramatic buybacks, not big M&A — **dividends are the return-of-capital channel**.

### Buybacks
ADP does modest ongoing buybacks, typically $1-1.5B/year. Not aggressive, but consistent. Share count has declined modestly over 10 years (~4-5% total). Combined with dividend, total capital return is ~$3-3.5B/year.

### M&A
**Historically conservative**. ADP has made bolt-on acquisitions (usually HR tech, specialty payroll) but has NOT pursued transformative deals. This discipline has served shareholders well — no Blue Buffalo-style missteps.

### R&D investment
ADP spends ~$1B/year on R&D (roughly 5% of revenue). This has been increasing with the AI focus. Workforce Now NextGen + Lyric HCM represent multi-year development investments.

### Capital allocation grade: **A**
Near-perfect execution. Consistent dividend growth, sensible buybacks, no M&A missteps, disciplined capital spending. The only reason it's not A+ is the somewhat timid buyback pace — arguably ADP could repurchase more aggressively at dips.

- **Integration of AI**: if AI features don't deliver client value, the narrative collapses
