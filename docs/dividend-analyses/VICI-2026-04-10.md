## PART I · EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: ACCUMULATE.** **Dividend Safety: 8/10.** **Confidence: Medium-High.**

VICI owns 93 experiential properties (54 gaming + 39 other) across 26 states and Canada with a weighted average lease term of 39.6 years and 100% rent collection since formation — including through COVID (VICI 2025 10-K Item 1). Since the October 2017 CEOC spinoff, management has announced ~$39.1B of investments, grown revenue from $201M (2017) to $4.0B (2025), and compounded AFFO/share at roughly 10%/yr. The board has raised the dividend 8 consecutive years from $0.72 (2018) to $1.80 annualized in Q3 2025. At $27.76 you get a 6.5% starting yield, a 73% AFFO payout, and an investment-grade balance sheet financed 99.2% fixed-rate (VICI 2025 10-K Item 7A).

That is the clean sell. The variant is Caesars. Caesars is 39% of annualized rent, MGM is 35% — 74% in two non-IG gaming operators (VICI 2025 10-K Item 1 line 415). On paper this is a triple-net REIT; in practice it is a leveraged bet on two casino balance sheets cross-defaulted under master leases that run to 2035 at the earliest. Management telegraphed in the 2025 10-K Item 7 that "Caesars' continued underperformance within the regional portfolio... [has had] an adverse effect on our stock performance" and that VICI has "engaged in preliminary discussions with Caesars regarding... the Caesars Regional Master Lease" (lines 4203-4206). This is not a risk factor — it is a disclosed live negotiation. The stock's 6.5% yield is roughly 200 bps wide of Realty Income (O) because the market is pricing non-zero probability of a haircut. We think the master lease + parent guaranty makes an outright dividend cut unlikely, but we size the position accordingly.

The second variant is rate regression. VICI was added to the S&P 500 in June 2022 at a 10-year yield near 3% and is now refinancing into a 4.5-5.5% environment. April 2025's $1.3B issuance priced at 4.75% (2028s) and 5.625% (2035s). Interest expense hit $843.6M in 2025 vs. $826.1M in 2024. Against this, 42% of 2025 rent and 90% of long-term rent is CPI-linked (VICI 2025 10-K Item 1 line 132) — a structural inflation hedge no competing net-lease REIT at scale offers. AFFO/share still grew 5.1% in 2025.

**Top 3 reasons to own at $27.76:** (1) 6.5% yield + 8/10 safety + ~10% historical AFFO/share CAGR; (2) mission-critical, irreplaceable Strip real estate (Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, Venetian, Mandalay Bay); (3) COVID stress test already passed — 100% rent collection through the worst casino closure event in history.

**Top 3 reasons NOT to add today:** (1) Caesars rent renegotiation is now management-acknowledged, not hypothetical — any headline re-rates the multiple; (2) 74% tenant concentration is structurally high for a core-equity holding; the tail is binary-ish; (3) rate cycle refi drag compresses AFFO/share growth into the mid-single-digits through 2028.

**12m catalysts:** (i) Caesars Regional lease resolution; (ii) Golden Entertainment closing mid-2026 (+$87M rent); (iii) Fed cuts resetting REIT cost of capital; (iv) Caesars/MGM regional EBITDAR data.

## PART III · HISTORICAL REVIEW — 10-YEAR ARC (since 2017 spinoff)

**Phase 1: Caesars Spinoff (2017-2018).** VICI was organized July 2016 as a Delaware LLC subsidiary of Caesars Entertainment Operating Company (CEOC) and converted to Maryland corporate form in May 2017. It emerged from CEOC's Chapter 11 reorganization in October 2017 holding real estate that had previously backed CEOC debt (VICI 2025 10-K Item 1 line 467). FY2017 posted $201M revenue and $43M net income (partial year); no dividend was paid (FMP cashflow). The first dividend declared was $0.16/share for the Feb 5 – Mar 31, 2018 pro-rata period (VICI 2019 10-K Distributions note, line 2864). Strategic significance: VICI was born as the largest landlord to the most distressed major casino operator in the US — a structure in which the thesis depended entirely on whether Caesars could get its balance sheet in order. It did.

**Phase 2: Scale-up (2019-2020).** VICI added smaller operators beyond Caesars: JACK Cleveland/Thistledown, Greektown, Margaritaville, Hard Rock Cincinnati, Century Portfolio. Dividend grew from $0.72 (2018) to $1.16 (2019), reflecting a first full year of payments.

**Phase 3: COVID Stress Test (2020).** March 2020: all 28 VICI casino properties closed by regulatory order. **VICI collected 100% of rent for every month of 2020 and into February 2021** (VICI 2020 10-K Note 1 line 1560: "All of our tenants have fulfilled their rent obligations in full through February 2021"). Mechanism: master lease + parent guaranty + cross-default. If Caesars stopped paying on even one property, it was in default on the entire master lease and would lose its full VICI-leased portfolio including Caesars Palace. So Caesars paid. MGM paid. Penn paid. FY2020 revenue rose to $1,226M (+37%), net income $892M, DPS raised to $1.21. **The dividend was raised during COVID.** This is the single most important durability data point in the VICI case. The July 2020 Eldorado-Caesars merger was used to combine CPLV and HLV into a Las Vegas Master Lease and replace the Non-CPLV lease with a Regional Master Lease — the same Regional Master Lease under discussion in 2025.

**Phase 4: Venetian Acquisition (2021-2022).** VICI announced and closed the Venetian Resort Las Vegas acquisition for $4.0B (Feb 2022) and the MGM Growth Properties merger for ~$17.2B in stock and assumed debt (April 2022), becoming the largest casino real estate owner on earth. FY2022 revenue jumped from $1.51B to $2.60B (+72%). Share count rose from 577M diluted (2021) to 880M diluted (2022). June 2022: added to the S&P 500 — fastest IPO-to-S&P 500 path for any REIT in history.

**Phase 5: Rate Cycle Pressure (2022-2024).** The Fed tightening cycle introduced meaningful rate sensitivity for the first time. Interest expense climbed $540M → $818M → $826M → $844M (2022–2025). The stock de-rated from ~16x AFFO (peak ~$34 in early 2022) to ~11.7x AFFO ($27.76 in April 2026). VICI responded by locking 99.2% of debt at fixed rates, extending maturities (April 2025 $1.3B refinance), and running the ATM equity program. AFFO/share still grew 10.1% (2023), 8.7% (2024), 5.1% (2025) — deceleration visible but not broken.

**Phase 6: Current (2025).** The 2025 10-K is the first filing where management has acknowledged, in the main body of Item 7 rather than a boilerplate risk factor, that Caesars Regional operations are underperforming and that "we have engaged in preliminary discussions with Caesars regarding its recent operating performance and the Caesars Regional Master Lease" (VICI 2025 10-K Item 7 line 4206). This is a disclosed live negotiation. Concurrent deal activity: Golden Entertainment $1.16B deal announced (7 properties, $87M initial rent, closing mid-2026); One Beverly Hills mezzanine $450M; North Fork Casino loan $510M; PENN Master Lease combination combining Greektown + Margaritaville into one $80.7M lease.

### Dividend cut probability — stress test

- **Scenario D: Rate cycle + portfolio saturation.** AFFO/sh flat-lines at ~$2.40 through 2028. 0% DGR. Multi-year de-rating, no cut.

## PART VI · TENANT CONCENTRATION + COVID STRESS TEST

This is the section that matters most for the dividend durability question. It is also where VICI's structure is unique among triple-net REITs.

### The numbers — 74% in two tenants

VICI 2025 10-K Item 1 line 415, verbatim: "Caesars and MGM, our two largest tenants representing 39% and 35%, respectively, of our annualized rent as of December 31, 2025." Peer comparison (approximate from respective 10-Ks):

| REIT | Largest tenant | Largest % | Top 3 % |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| VICI | Caesars | 39% | ~75% |
| Realty Income (O) | 7-Eleven | 3.8% | ~10% |
| NNN REIT | 7-Eleven | 4.7% | ~12% |
| Agree Realty (ADC) | Walmart | 6.8% | ~16% |
| STAG Industrial | Amazon | 3.0% | ~8% |
| W. P. Carey (WPC) | U-Haul | 3.0% | ~8% |

VICI is an order of magnitude more concentrated than any peer. This is the structural price of operating in gaming — maybe six operators of scale exist globally, and diversification below Caesars/MGM means lower credit, not different credit.

### Why the concentration is somewhat mitigated

Three structural features reduce the bite. **(1) Master lease + cross-default.** Caesars has three master leases (Las Vegas, Regional, Joliet). MGM has two (MGM Master, MGM Grand/Mandalay Bay). Every property inside each master lease is cross-defaulted against every other. You cannot reject one money-losing casino and keep Caesars Palace — you take the whole portfolio or none. **(2) Parent guaranty.** Caesars Entertainment, Inc. and MGM Resorts International have each executed full guaranties of all monetary and non-monetary lease obligations for the entire term (VICI 2025 10-K Item 1 lines 417-418). VICI has a direct claim on the multi-billion-dollar parents. **(3) Mission-critical real estate.** Replacement cost of a mid-tier Strip casino is $1-2B before entitlements and brand damage. These are not Dollar General stores.

### The COVID stress test in detail

Q2 2020: Las Vegas Strip casinos closed 78 days. Regional casinos closed 45-90 days. Caesars, MGM, Penn all reported Q2 EBITDAR losses — bleeding cash while legally required to pay rent. **Every one of them paid in full.** VICI 2020 10-K Note 1 line 1560 verbatim: "All of our tenants have fulfilled their rent obligations in full through February 2021." This is the single most powerful durability data point in the triple-net REIT universe — not because of what VICI management did, but because of what the lease structure forced tenants to do.

### The Caesars Regional problem — different from COVID

The 2025-2026 Caesars Regional situation is structurally different. During COVID, Caesars could not pay from operating cash flow but did pay from the parent balance sheet because the alternative was losing Las Vegas. In 2025, Caesars regional operations are profitable but declining, and management is lobbying for relief to redirect cash into capex and debt paydown. This is a negotiation, not a credit event.

VICI's negotiation leverage: (a) 2035 expiration — Caesars owes another 9+ years of escalating rent; (b) cross-default; (c) parent guaranty; (d) Caesars' own creditors and equity need the lease in force to preserve enterprise value. VICI's weakness: (a) public dispute damages both sides; (b) Caesars could plausibly file Chapter 11 to try to reject the lease; (c) VICI's stock takes collateral damage.

**Base case: 5-10% rent reduction on the Regional Master Lease in exchange for term extension, additional collateral, or tighter covenants.** Cost to VICI: ~2-4% of total rent. Dividend impact: frozen one year, not cut.
