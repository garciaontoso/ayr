## PART I -- EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Verdict: BUY below $40. STRONG BUY below $35. Dividend Safety: 9/10. Confidence: High.**

Novo Nordisk is the world's dominant GLP-1 franchise holder -- two products sharing the same active ingredient (semaglutide) generated DKK 206 billion in FY2025 revenue -- trading at $37.51 per ADR, down approximately 75% from its June 2024 peak near $147. The central question: is this a secular growth story whose moat has been permanently breached, or a generational entry point into the most profitable pharmaceutical franchise on the planet?

The answer leans decisively toward the latter. Three facts dominate the analysis:

First, the revenue deceleration is real but wildly overpriced. FY2025 revenue grew 6.4% (constant exchange rates) to DKK 309.1 billion, with Wegovy still growing 36% year-over-year to DKK 79.1 billion even as Novo Nordisk lost US GLP-1 market share leadership to Eli Lilly. The 20-F explicitly states that "the global branded obesity market doubled by volume in 2025" (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 654). The pie is growing faster than Novo's slice is shrinking. At 11.5x forward earnings, the market is pricing in competitive destruction that the top line does not yet reflect.

Second, the balance sheet transformation from near-zero debt to DKK 104.5 billion net debt is purposeful, not distressed. The debt financed: (a) three Catalent fill-finish sites acquired for $11.7 billion from Novo Holdings in 2024 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 1114), (b) the $4.7 billion Akero Therapeutics acquisition in December 2025 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 641), and (c) DKK 90.1 billion of CapEx building manufacturing capacity that competitors cannot replicate within 5-7 years. At 0.7x net debt/EBITDA, this remains among the most conservative leverage ratios in global pharmaceuticals.

Third, the free cash flow compression from DKK 70 billion (FY2024) to DKK 29 billion (FY2025) is entirely explained by the CapEx cycle. Operating cash flow was flat at DKK 119.1 billion versus DKK 121.0 billion. The dividend, at approximately DKK 51.7 billion, temporarily exceeded free cash flow -- but this is an investment-cycle artifact, not a structural coverage problem. As major facilities come online during 2027-2029 and CapEx normalizes from 29% of revenue toward the mid-teens, FCF will recover to DKK 70-100 billion annually.

The dividend thesis at NVO is unusual for an income investor. The yield is approximately 4.4% at $37.51 -- elevated relative to NVO's historical range but modest in absolute terms. The real attraction is the dividend growth rate: DPS has compounded at approximately 25% annually over five years, driven by the GLP-1 revenue explosion. At a ~50% payout ratio with 23 DKK EPS, the dividend is safely covered by earnings even as FCF temporarily lags. The 340B program resolution -- the company will recognize $4.2 billion of previously constrained revenue in Q1 2026 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 931) -- provides additional near-term cash flow tailwind.

**Key numbers at a glance:**
- Price / Yield / Payout ratio: $37.51 / ~4.4% / ~50% (EPS basis)
- Last dividend increase: FY2025 proposed DPS DKK 11.65, +18% [approx]
- 5y dividend CAGR: ~25% (FY2020 DPS DKK 4.08 to FY2025E DPS ~DKK 11.65)
- Net debt / EBITDA: ~0.7x
- FCF coverage: ~0.6x (temporarily depressed by CapEx; normalized ~1.3x)

**Top 3 reasons to OWN:**
1. **The GLP-1 TAM is barely penetrated at <5% of clinically eligible patients globally.** The International Diabetes Federation projects 780 million people with diabetes by 2045, and more than 800 million people currently live with obesity (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 928). Even a 35-40% market share in a $100B+ annual obesity market yields enormous revenue.
2. **CagriSema + Wegovy Pill extend the competitive runway by 5-7 years.** CagriSema showed 22.7% weight loss in REDEFINE 1 -- best-in-class Phase 3 obesity data. Wegovy Pill launched Q1 2026 to "incredible uptake" per CEO Doustdar on the Q4 2025 call. NDA filing for CagriSema expected 2026.
3. **Manufacturing capacity is the binding constraint, and NVO is building it.** Total committed CapEx across announced facility expansions exceeds DKK 150 billion, with facilities coming online 2026-2029. The three Catalent sites provide fill-finish capacity that would take competitors 5-7 years to replicate from greenfield.

**Top 3 reasons NOT TO ADD:**
1. **Eli Lilly has taken US GLP-1 market share leadership.** Lilly's Zepbound overtook Wegovy in total weekly prescriptions in early 2025; Mounjaro overtook Ozempic during 2025 (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 651-653). The duopoly dynamic has shifted permanently.
2. **Medicare Part D price negotiations compress US pricing from 2027.** Ozempic, Rybelsus, and Wegovy were all included in the second round of IRA negotiations. The MFN agreement expands access but at lower per-unit economics (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 659).
3. **CapEx cycle temporarily destroys free cash flow.** The DKK 90.1 billion CapEx in FY2025 pushed FCF to DKK 29 billion, barely covering the ~DKK 51.7 billion dividend obligation. Any demand miscalculation risks expensive overcapacity.

**12-month catalysts:**
- CagriSema NDA filing and potential FDA advisory committee (expected 2026)
- Wegovy Pill US launch trajectory data (Q1/Q2 2026 earnings)
- Wegovy 7.2mg high-dose approval (filed December 2025, approval expected Q1 2026)
- Q1 2026 recognition of $4.2 billion 340B constrained revenue
- Medicare Part D obesity coverage pilot (expected mid-2026)
- REDEFINE 4 and REDEFINE 11 Phase 3 readouts for CagriSema

## PART II -- COMPANY SNAPSHOT

B shares listed on Nasdaq Copenhagen (NOVO-B); ADRs on NYSE (NVO), each representing one B share. As of year-end 2025: 1,074,872,000 A shares and 3,390,128,000 B shares outstanding. Nominal value DKK 0.10 per share. (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 45-48)

- **Sector / Industry:** Healthcare / Pharmaceuticals (GLP-1 therapeutics)
- **Business segments:** (i) Obesity and Diabetes care (~93% of revenue), (ii) Rare Disease (~7%)
- **Geographic exposure:** US Operations ~63% of revenue; International Operations ~37% (EUCAN, Emerging Markets, APAC, Region China)

**Inflection points:**

1. **FY2016-2018 -- The Stagnation.** Revenue was flat at ~DKK 112B for three consecutive years. NVO was perceived as a mature insulin company under US pricing pressure. Victoza was growing but not yet transformative. The stock traded in the $40-60 range. This period established the base from which everything that followed looks extraordinary.

2. **FY2019-2020 -- The Ozempic Inflection.** Ozempic launched February 2018 and became the most successful drug launch in diabetes history. Revenue broke out from DKK 112B to DKK 127B. Net income crossed DKK 40B. The STEP trials for semaglutide 2.4mg in obesity -- later branded Wegovy -- demonstrated ~15% weight loss, establishing GLP-1s as genuine medical treatment for obesity rather than a lifestyle drug.

3. **FY2021-2024 -- GLP-1 Mania.** Everything accelerated: revenue surged from DKK 141B to DKK 290B in four years (+106%), operating margins expanded to 44.2%, and the stock rocketed from ~$40 to ~$147. NVO briefly became the most valuable company in Europe at over $600B market cap. The global branded obesity market doubled by volume in 2025.

4. **FY2025 -- The De-Rating.** Revenue growth decelerated to 6.4% (constant exchange rates). CagriSema REDEFINE 1 showed 22.7% weight loss versus whispered 25%+ expectations. Eli Lilly's tirzepatide took US market share leadership in both diabetes (Mounjaro vs Ozempic) and obesity (Zepbound vs Wegovy). Gross margin compressed 370bps to 81.0% due to manufacturing expansion costs and unfavorable product/geographic mix. A company-wide restructuring eliminated ~9,000 positions. Lars Fruergaard Jorgensen stepped down as CEO, replaced by Mike Doustdar. The stock declined 75% from peak. No new buyback program was initiated.

## PART IV -- DIVIDEND DEEP DIVE

Novo Nordisk's stated dividend policy is to distribute approximately 50% of net profit as dividends. The company pays dividends annually in DKK on A and B shares; ADR holders receive the USD equivalent via the JPMorgan depositary, which charges a maximum fee of $0.05 per ADR per distribution (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 179).

- **Current yield:** ~4.4% at $37.51 (based on estimated FY2025 DPS of ~DKK 11.65 / ~$1.66)
- **Payout ratio (EPS basis):** ~50%
- **Payout ratio (FCF basis):** ~179% (temporarily distressed by DKK 90.1B CapEx)
- **Dividend streak:** NVO has increased DPS annually since at least FY2015 (continuous streak ~10+ years)
- **5y dividend CAGR:** ~25% (FY2020-FY2025E)
- **10y dividend CAGR:** ~16% [approx]
- **Last hike:** FY2025E proposed DPS ~DKK 11.65, +18% over FY2024 DPS of DKK 9.90
- **Pattern of hikes:** Annual, following FY results announcement in February
- **Dividend coverage by FCF:** ~0.6x (FY2025); normalized ~1.3x at mid-cycle CapEx
- **Currency of payment:** DKK (converted to USD for ADR holders by depositary)
- **Withholding tax:** Danish WHT 27% at source; reduced to 15% under US-Denmark treaty (0% for certain pension funds). Excess WHT refundable via Form 6166 and Danish Claim for Refund within 3 years.

**Dividend sustainability narrative:**

The dividend is safe through a mild recession. At a ~50% payout ratio on DKK 23.06 EPS, NVO would need EPS to halve (to ~DKK 11.65) before the dividend would consume all earnings -- a scenario requiring a ~50% revenue decline or operating margin collapse to the low 20s, neither of which occurred even during the FY2016-2018 stagnation period when margins held above 42%.

The genuine stress point is the FCF coverage, which is temporarily inverted. Total dividends paid of ~DKK 51.7 billion exceeded FY2025 FCF of DKK 29.0 billion, requiring the company to fund the gap from its DKK 26.5 billion cash balance and its existing debt facilities. This is an investment-cycle phenomenon: if CapEx normalized to ~DKK 50 billion (still elevated), FCF would be approximately DKK 69 billion, and FCF coverage would be 1.3x -- comfortable. Management has guided CapEx declining in FY2026 and "a steeper slope in the coming years" per CFO Knudsen on the Q4 2025 call.

**What would have to break for a cut:** Simultaneous competitive destruction (NVO loses >50% of GLP-1 market share), regulatory failure (CagriSema denied approval), and a sustained US pricing collapse. The probability of all three occurring within the dividend planning horizon is extremely low (<5%).

**Critical new WHT risk:** In late 2025, the Danish National Tax Tribunal issued a ruling raising uncertainty about whether US ADR holders qualify as "shareholders" for Danish tax purposes, potentially affecting their ability to claim the treaty-rate refund of Excess Withholding Tax (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 113). If ADR holders cannot claim the treaty rate, the effective WHT increases from 15% to 27%, reducing the after-tax dividend by approximately 14%. This is novel, unresolved, and material for income investors.

## PART V -- BUSINESS QUALITY AND MOAT

Novo Nordisk's moat is wide but narrowing at the edges.

- **Moat type:** Scale + switching costs + regulatory barriers + manufacturing complexity
- **Moat trajectory:** Stable to slightly narrowing (Lilly competition)
- **Pricing power evidence:** US net prices declined in FY2025 due to PBM pressure, IRA negotiations, and the MFN agreement. However, gross margin at 81.0% remains among the highest in global pharmaceuticals, demonstrating that pricing power persists at the gross level even as net-price realization declines.
- **ROIC trend:** ROIC has expanded from approximately 50-60% (FY2020-2021) to over 80% during the GLP-1 boom years, then moderated. At any level above 30%, NVO remains one of the highest-ROIC businesses in global healthcare.

**Moat pillars in detail:**

1. **First-mover advantage and clinical depth in GLP-1.** NVO pioneered the class with liraglutide (Victoza/Saxenda) and semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy/Rybelsus). The company has 20+ years of GLP-1 safety data and deep physician familiarity. This cannot be replicated.

2. **Manufacturing scale at prohibitive cost.** DKK 90.1 billion of CapEx in a single year. The company disclosed specific committed investments exceeding DKK 150 billion across facilities in Kalundborg, Hilleroed, Chartres, Clayton NC, Odense, Tianjin, and others (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 791-802). The three Catalent fill-finish sites acquired in 2024 provide capacity that would take competitors 5-7 years to replicate from greenfield. Biological peptide manufacturing requires specialized facilities and years of regulatory validation.

3. **Pipeline depth extending beyond the 2032 patent cliff.** CagriSema (semaglutide + cagrilintide dual mechanism) showed 22.7% weight loss in Phase 3. Wegovy Pill (oral semaglutide for obesity) launched Q1 2026 to "incredible uptake." Efruxifermin (via Akero acquisition) addresses MASH. Icodec (once-weekly insulin) is approved in EU. The pipeline creates a succession pathway that de-risks the semaglutide compound patent expiry.

4. **Installed patient base.** More patients are on semaglutide globally than any other GLP-1. Switching costs are non-trivial: patients who have titrated up and achieved stable weight loss are reluctant to switch molecules. Wegovy has been launched in the US and more than 50 other countries (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 654) versus Zepbound's more limited international rollout.

**Against Eli Lilly (the only competitor that matters today):**
Lilly's tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1) has demonstrated competitive or slightly superior weight loss in some trials and has taken US market share leadership. But the GLP-1 market is not winner-take-all. Both companies can grow if the TAM expands as projected -- and at <5% global penetration, this is the base case. NVO's advantages: broader geographic footprint, oral formulation (Wegovy Pill has no Lilly equivalent yet), and CagriSema as a differentiated next-generation asset. Lilly's advantages: slightly superior efficacy in some indications, manufacturing scale from years of aggressive CapEx, and orforglipron (oral GLP-1, Phase 3) in the pipeline.

**Against pipeline competitors:** Amgen (MariTide, Phase 2), Pfizer (danuglipron, Phase 3), Viking Therapeutics (VK2735, Phase 2), Roche/Carmot (oral GLP-1, Phase 2). All are 3-5 years behind NVO and Lilly. None will launch before 2028-2029 at the earliest. The competitive moat is narrowing but remains wide enough for NVO to generate enormous cash flows over the next 5-7 years.

## PART V-A -- PIPELINE AND PRODUCT DEEP DIVE

The investment thesis for NVO at $37.51 depends heavily on the pipeline creating a succession pathway beyond the 2032 semaglutide patent cliff. Here is a product-by-product assessment.

**Ozempic (injectable semaglutide, 0.5/1/2mg for type 2 diabetes):** DKK 127.1 billion in FY2025. Launched February 2018. In its eighth year on the market, Ozempic remains a category-defining product that fundamentally changed the treatment paradigm for type 2 diabetes by demonstrating both glycemic control and cardiovascular benefit. However, during 2025 Ozempic lost its position as the leading GLP-1 for type 2 diabetes in the US by total weekly prescriptions to Eli Lilly's Mounjaro (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 653). US Operations generated DKK 88.5 billion (69.6% of Ozempic sales). Compound patent expires 2032. Multiple ANDA settlements have been reached with generic manufacturers under confidential terms (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 708-715). The 2032 cliff is real but manageable if CagriSema and next-generation assets are on the market by then.

**Wegovy (injectable semaglutide 2.4mg for obesity):** DKK 79.1 billion in FY2025, up 36% year-over-year. Launched June 2021. Revenue growth of 36% on a DKK 58 billion base is extraordinary for a product in its fifth year. Wegovy has been launched in the US and more than 50 other countries (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 654). US Operations generated DKK 51.0 billion (64.5% of Wegovy sales). The Medicare Part D coverage pilot (expected mid-2026 under the MFN agreement) could be a major volume catalyst if it demonstrates that expanded access generates net savings for the healthcare system.

**Wegovy Pill (oral semaglutide 25/50mg for obesity):** Just launched Q1 2026. Approved in the US under the brand name Wegovy Pill (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 875). This is potentially transformative -- the first oral GLP-1 for obesity eliminates the injection barrier that has deterred a significant portion of eligible patients. CEO Doustdar stated on the Q4 2025 call that the pill had "incredible uptake" in its first month, with availability across 70,000 US retail pharmacies. When asked about competition from Lilly's orforglipron (12.4% weight loss), Doustdar was direct: Wegovy Pill at 16.6% weight loss provides superior efficacy plus cardiovascular benefits. He also confirmed supply confidence: "We launched the pill in the U.S. at a time where we will be confident enough to know we will not run into supply situation anymore." The lower gross margin relative to the injectable (per CFO Knudsen: "below that of the Wegovy injectable, but... still an attractive gross margin") means the pill expands the market more than it enhances per-unit profitability -- but this is the right trade-off for a growth compounder.

**Rybelsus (oral semaglutide 14mg for type 2 diabetes):** DKK 22.1 billion in FY2025. The existing oral semaglutide product for diabetes has grown steadily with strong uptake in International Operations (DKK 13.3 billion, 60% of sales). Rybelsus demonstrates that NVO can successfully commercialize oral semaglutide formulations -- a proof of concept for Wegovy Pill.

**CagriSema (semaglutide + cagrilintide, in development):** The most important pipeline asset. CagriSema combines semaglutide (GLP-1 receptor agonist) with cagrilintide (an amylin analog) to create a dual-mechanism approach to weight loss. REDEFINE 1 Phase 3 showed 22.7% weight loss -- the highest ever demonstrated in a Phase 3 obesity trial at that time. NDA filing for obesity is expected in 2025/2026 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 881). Additional Phase 3 studies are ongoing for heart failure, MASH, and sleep apnea. CSO Martin Holst Lange noted on the Q4 2025 call that REDEFINE 11 (with optimized flexible titration) represents the "full weight loss potential" of CagriSema, while REDEFINE 4 (amended from the original protocol) may not capture all improvements. If CagriSema achieves regulatory approval, it extends NVO's competitive moat by providing a differentiated next-generation product before the 2032 semaglutide patent cliff.

**Wegovy 7.2mg (high-dose injectable semaglutide):** Filed with FDA in December 2025 under the CNPV voucher program; approval expected Q1 2026 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 877). CEO Doustdar was emphatic on the Q4 call: "We have shown in a step up that when you increase the dose of sema to 7.2, you get very close on par with tirzepatide... on top of on par weight loss, you have also CV benefit, kidney benefits and liver benefits." This closes the efficacy gap with Lilly's Zepbound and may recapture prescriber preference.

**Akero Therapeutics / Efruxifermin (MASH):** Acquired December 2025 for $54/share ($4.7 billion) plus a $6/share CVR contingent on US regulatory approval for compensated cirrhosis due to MASH (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 641). EFX is in three ongoing Phase 3 studies. MASH is a massive unmet need (estimated 16 million patients in the US alone) with strong overlap with the obesity/diabetes patient population. The US patent for EFX expires in 2029, but up to 5 years of patent term extension is available plus 12 years of regulatory data protection from market authorization (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 908). The acquisition positions NVO for combination therapy opportunities with semaglutide or CagriSema.

## PART VIII-B -- TAX CONSIDERATIONS FOR US INVESTORS

Danish dividend withholding tax is 27% at source. Under the US-Denmark tax treaty, eligible US Holders can claim a reduced treaty rate of 15% (0% for certain pension funds). The difference (12% excess withholding) can be reclaimed from the Danish tax authorities via Form 6166 and a Danish Claim for Refund. Refund claims must be filed within three years of dividend payment. Refund administration is through Globe Tax Services / JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A. (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 105-110).

**Critical new development:** In late 2025, the Danish National Tax Tribunal (Landsskatteretten) issued a ruling which raises uncertainty regarding whether US ADR holders qualify as "shareholders" for Danish tax purposes, potentially affecting their ability to claim the treaty-rate refund of Excess Withholding Tax (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 113). This is a novel legal issue. The FY2024 20-F contained no equivalent language -- this risk factor is entirely new in the FY2025 filing. If unresolved unfavorably, the effective WHT becomes 27%, reducing the after-tax yield from ~3.7% (at 15% WHT) to ~3.2% (at 27% WHT) on a $37.51 cost basis. US holders should consult tax advisors and monitor developments closely.

For dividends received, US investors can generally claim a foreign tax credit against US federal tax for Danish taxes withheld at the treaty rate. Dividends are likely treated as qualified dividend income eligible for preferential US tax rates, subject to holding period requirements. The depositary charges a maximum fee of $0.05 per ADR per distribution (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 179).

Note for the portfolio holder: As a China tax resident under the US-China treaty with 10% WHT on US dividends, the interaction between Danish WHT on NVO dividends and Chinese tax obligations may create a complex multi-treaty scenario. Consult a cross-border tax specialist.

## PART X -- RISKS AND STRESS TESTS

**Risk #1: Competitive erosion from Eli Lilly.** Probability: high. Severity: medium. Lilly's tirzepatide has taken US market share leadership in both diabetes (Mounjaro vs Ozempic) and obesity (Zepbound vs Wegovy). If Lilly also launches orforglipron (oral GLP-1, Phase 3), competitive intensity increases further. Mitigant: GLP-1 market is not zero-sum -- both companies can grow if TAM expands. NVO has Wegovy Pill first-mover advantage in oral obesity.

**Risk #2: US pricing pressure from IRA and MFN.** Probability: certain. Severity: medium. Ozempic, Rybelsus, and Wegovy were included in the second round of IRA Medicare Part D price negotiations, with negotiated prices effective 2027 (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 659). The MFN agreement expands access but compresses per-unit pricing. CFO Knudsen acknowledged on the Q4 2025 call that "continued pricing pressure within Obesity and Diabetes Care is expected" for 2026. Mitigant: Volume uplift from expanded access may offset price reduction -- management's base case. However, the math is unproven.

**Risk #3: Semaglutide patent cliff in 2032.** Probability: certain. Severity: high (long-term). Compound patent expires in the US in 2032. Multiple ANDA settlement agreements with generic manufacturers are already in place under confidential terms (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 708-715). Generic/biosimilar semaglutide will eventually enter the market. In Canada, generic semaglutide is expected in 2026 (CFO Knudsen noted on Q4 call: "sema LOE in international markets will impact group sales by low single digits"). Mitigant: CagriSema and next-generation assets should be on market by 2028-2029, creating a succession pathway.

**Risk #4: Manufacturing execution risk.** Probability: medium. Severity: high. DKK 90.1 billion of CapEx in a single year is unprecedented. The company has over DKK 150 billion in committed facility investments across Denmark, France, US, and China (NVO FY2025 20-F, lines 791-802). Construction delays, quality issues, or demand miscalculations could create either supply shortfalls or expensive overcapacity.

**Risk #5: Danish withholding tax uncertainty for ADR holders.** Probability: medium. Severity: low-medium. The late-2025 Danish National Tax Tribunal ruling (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 113) raises novel uncertainty about ADR holders' status as "shareholders" for tax treaty purposes. If unresolved unfavorably, effective WHT increases from 15% to 27%, reducing after-tax dividend by ~14%.

**Risk #6: Compounding pharmacies.** Probability: ongoing. Severity: low-medium. Despite resolution of the GLP-1 shortage, unauthorized compounding of semaglutide continues (NVO FY2025 20-F, line 655). NVO is pursuing legal action, but enforcement across hundreds of compounding pharmacies is challenging.

**Stress test scenarios:**

- **Revenue decline -20% (severe competitive loss):** Revenue falls to ~DKK 247B. At 41% operating margin, operating income = ~DKK 101B. Net income = ~DKK 77B. EPS = ~DKK 17.3. DPS at 50% payout = ~DKK 8.65. Dividend still covered by earnings, but FCF coverage depends on CapEx reduction.
- **Margin compression -300 bps (pricing pressure):** Operating margin falls to 38.3%. Operating income declines from DKK 127.7B to DKK 118.4B. Net income falls ~7%. Dividend remains comfortably covered.
- **Refinancing at +400 bps:** Interest expense on DKK 131B debt increases by ~DKK 5.2B to ~DKK 9.4B. Interest coverage drops from 37x to ~17x. Still extremely safe.

**Composite stress (all three simultaneously):** Revenue DKK 247B, 38.3% operating margin, DKK 9.4B interest = operating income ~DKK 85B, pre-tax income ~DKK 76B, net income ~DKK 58B, EPS ~DKK 13.0. DPS at 50% payout = ~DKK 6.50. Dividend survives even the worst-case composite scenario, though FCF coverage would require CapEx reduction.
